# Today's Robotics & AI Papers Summary

## Dexterous Manipulation & In-Hand Manipulation

### [Support-Constrained RL Enables Real-World Policy Improvement without Real-World Experience](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27475)
Proposes SCORE, a real-to-sim-to-real framework that constrains RL in simulation to the support of a generative policy pretrained on real data. Uses flow steering to ensure transferable behaviors. Across 8 real-world dexterous multi-fingered manipulation tasks, improves average success rate from 37.8% to 89.9%, with 36.8% fewer steps.

### [Learning Stable In-Grasp Manipulation in a Non-Dropping Action Space](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28196)
Decomposes dexterous manipulation skills into analyzable components guided by classical physics and control theory. Shows that for stable in-grasp repositioning/reorientation with different objects, skill learning becomes efficient with physics-informed priors.

### [DexCompose: Reusing Dexterous Policies for Multi-Task Manipulation with a Single Hand](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28323)
Proposes role-aware residual composition framework that reuses pretrained dexterous policies for multi-task manipulation. Uses finger-level action ownership to identify which fingers are necessary for task preservation vs. new tasks. Achieves 77.4% average composite success rate across 16 tasks.

### [Web2Grasp: Learning Functional Grasps from Web Images of Hand-Object Interactions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05517)
Leverages human grasp information from web images to train functional grasping for dexterous hands. Uses 3D reconstruction from RGB images, interaction-centric models, and geometry-based filtering. Achieves 75.8% simulation success rate and 77.5% real-world success rate across 12 objects.

## Visuomotor Policies & VLA Models

### [Direct Action-Head Injection of a Grounded 3D Point Unlocks Spatial and Task Generalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27663)
Proposes lightweight module that represents grounding signal in 3D, computes relative displacement to gripper, and injects spatial embedding into action head. On LIBERO-PRO, improves GR00T-N1.6 from 31.2 to 77.5 under task perturbation and from 28.1 to 60.2 under position perturbation.

### [Drop-Then-Recovery: How Redundant Are Vision-Language-Action Models?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27755)
Studies architectural redundancy in VLA models. Finds language backbones are highly redundant for manipulation tasks—removing half of LLM blocks even improves OpenVLA-OFT from 95.0% to 98.3%. Vision and action pathways are less tolerant to removal.

### [S²-VLA: State-Space Guided Vision-Language-Action Models for Long-Horizon Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27872)
Introduces State-Space Guided Adaptive Attention mechanism that maintains belief state tracking task progression. Despite compact 2B parameters, outperforms 7B-scale models on long-horizon manipulation benchmarks (LIBERO, SimplerEnv).

### [StereoVLA: Enhancing Vision-Language-Action Models with Stereo Vision](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21970)
First VLA model to incorporate rich geometric cues from stereo data. Uses Geometric-and-Semantic vision encoder with two synergistic objectives: depth estimation and camera parameter estimation. Achieves 33.4% absolute gain in success rate over baselines.

### [SpikeVLA: Vision-Language-Action Models with Spiking Neural Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27807)
Proposes spiking VLA architecture for embodied navigation with energy-efficient inference. Uses event-driven spiking layers to reduce energy consumption while maintaining competitive performance for low-power real-time scenarios.

## Teleoperation & Imitation Learning

### [Translation as a Bridging Action: Transferring Manipulation Skills from Humans to Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28133)
Proposes relative wrist translation within initial head-camera frame as bridging action representation shared by humans and robots. Builds π₀-like VLA model with interleaved action tokens. Transfers human manipulation knowledge to bimanual robot more effectively than noisy 6DoF human actions.

### [WARP-RM: A Warp-Augmented Relative Progress Reward Model for Data Curation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28320)
Self-supervised algorithm for learning dense progress signals from demonstrations via time-warp augmentations. Generates frame-level progress targets by variable playback speeds and reversals. On bimanual T-shirt folding, maintains 19/20 success rate vs. vanilla BC's collapse to 2/20.

### [Data Scaling Laws in Imitation Learning for Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.18647)
Comprehensive empirical study on data scaling in imitation learning across 40K+ demonstrations and 15K+ real-world rollouts. Finds policy performance follows power-law with number of environments/objects; diversity matters more than absolute number of demonstrations.

### [LA4VLA: Learning to Act without Seeing via Language-Action Pretraining](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27295)
Language-action pretraining framework that enables policies to acquire language-conditioned action priors without visual observations. Creates LA-33K dataset from existing demonstrations. Mixed LA-VLA pretraining improves success rate by up to 45.0 percentage points in real-world tasks.

## Bimanual Manipulation

### [PA-BiCoop: A Primary-Auxiliary Cooperative Framework for General Bimanual Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28192)
Proposes single-model bimanual cooperation framework with dynamic primary-auxiliary arm differentiation. Uses two specialized decoders with shared encoder and dynamic role assignment. Outperforms baselines by 48% in simulation and 50% in real-world tasks.

## Humanoid & Whole-Body Control

### [SceneBot: Contact-Prompted General Humanoid Whole Body Tracking with Scene-Interaction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27581)
Unified motion-tracking framework for free-space locomotion, terrain traversal, and whole-body manipulation. Conditions policy on reference motions and per-link contact labels. Trained on 7.5 hours of contact-rich data, generalizes to unseen motions and environments.

### [CWI: Composite Humanoid Whole-Body Imitation System for Loco-manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27676)
Decouples MoCap data for upper-body manipulation and lower-body locomotion. Uses dual discriminators for stable locomotion and multi-critic architecture. Deployed on full-size LimX Oli humanoid for practical teleoperation without full-body MoCap.

### [Booster Lab: A Data-Centric Pipeline for Learning Deployable Humanoid Locomotion Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27813)
Data-centric pipeline integrating motion data curation, real-to-sim adaptation, AMP-based RL, and sim-to-real deployment. Validated on Booster T1 and K1 humanoid robots.

## Tactile & Contact-Rich Manipulation

### [SceneBot: Contact-Prompted General Humanoid Whole Body Tracking with Scene-Interaction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27581)
Addressed above—notable for contact-conditioned policies handling contact-rich manipulation.

### [PPO-EAL: Exact Augmented Lagrangian Proximal Policy Optimization for Safe Robotic Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27861)
Safe RL framework integrating exact augmented Lagrangian into PPO. Demonstrates zero-shot sim-to-real deployment in contact-rich gear assembly task, improving success rate and reducing peak contact force.

## Sim-to-Real & Simulation

### [SimFoundry: Modular and Automated Scene Generation for Policy Learning and Evaluation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28276)
Modular system for zero-shot real-to-sim scene construction from video. Generates sim-ready digital twins with object/scene/task editing. Across 7 manipulation tasks and 5 policy architectures, simulation evaluations strongly predict real-world performance (Pearson correlation 0.911).

### [DeformGen: Dynamics-Based Topology Augmentation for Deformable Manipulation Policy Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25939)
Augmentation framework achieving topological diversity for deformable objects by applying localized physical disturbances and forward-simulating dynamics. Transfers trajectories via deformation-field warping. Generally improves policy learning over original demonstrations.

### [PhysisForcing: Physics Reinforced World Simulator for Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28128)
Strengthens physical consistency in video generation for embodied world simulation. Uses pixel-level trajectory alignment and semantic-level relational alignment. As world model, raises closed-loop success rate from 16% to 24% under action-planner protocol.

## Object Goal Navigation & Embodied Planning

### [LocalNav: Distilling Frontier VLMs and Embodied RL for On-Device Object Goal Navigation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27871)
Distills spatial-semantic reasoning from large frontier models into lightweight 4B-parameter local VLM for edge execution. Achieves 34.5% success rate on HM3D OVON benchmark with 82.8% reduction in inference latency via quantization and token regularization.

## Multi-Robot Systems

### [AO-ARC: Almost-Surely Asymptotically Optimal Multi-Robot Motion Planning with ARC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27495)
Anytime multi-robot motion planning method that achieves initial solution times comparable to state-of-the-art feasibility solvers while converging faster. Provides theoretical analysis proving asymptotic optimality properties.

### [P-ARC: Exploiting Subproblem Independence for Parallel Multi-Robot Motion Planning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27625)
Parallel variant of adaptive robot coordination approach. Exploits independent subproblems across individual solutions, conflict detection, and conflict resolution. Achieves 4X speedup over sequential version with 16 CPU cores.

## Manipulation Benchmarks & Data

### [LIBERO-Safety: A Comprehensive Benchmark for Physical and Semantic Safety in VLA Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23686)
Parametric safety benchmark with 19,664 collision-free demonstrations. Systematic cross-paradigm evaluation of 8 VLA and 2 embodied foundation models. Reveals generalization-safety tension: high-diversity training fosters safer trajectories but task success is bottlenecked by sub-optimal synthesis.

### [MobileManiBench: Simplifying Model Verification for Mobile Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05233)
Large-scale benchmark for mobile-based manipulation featuring 2 mobile platforms, 630 objects, 5 skills, 300K trajectories. Built on NVIDIA Isaac Sim with RL-powered autonomous generation.

### [SC3-Eval: Evaluating Robot Foundation Models via Self-Consistent Video Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18610)
Self-consistent video generation recipe for evaluating generalist manipulation policies. Enforces forward-inverse dynamics consistency, cross-view consistency, and test-time consistency. Achieves Pearson correlation of 0.929 across seven real-world VLA policies.

## Error Recovery & Safety

### [FailSafe: Reasoning and Recovery from Failures in Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01642)
Failure generation and recovery system producing diverse failure cases paired with executable recovery actions. Fine-tuned FailSafe-VLM improves three SOTA VLA models by up to 22.6% across tasks in ManiSkill.

### [DIM-WAM: World-Action Modeling with Diverse Historical Event Memory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27677)
Memory-augmented world-action model integrating multi-scale historical context for long-horizon tasks. Uses multiple memory banks with similarity-based merging. On RMBench raises success from 28.4% to 69.8%; on real-world Franka tasks improves full-task success from 52.5% to 80.0%.
