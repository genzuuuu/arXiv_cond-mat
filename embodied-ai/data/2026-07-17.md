### **Dexterous Hands, Tactile Sensing & In-Hand Manipulation**

*   **[MIDAS Hand: Modular low-Impedance Direct-drive Anthropomorphic Sensing Hand](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14487)** - A new open-source, low-cost ($3k BOM) human-scale dexterous hand with 13 active DoF, direct-drive actuation for high backdrivability, and 283 tactile taxels. It is designed for easy 3D printing and assembly, serving as a balanced platform for tactile dexterous manipulation research.
*   **[KineFuse: Kinematic-Aware Haptic Fusion for In-Hand Occluded-Object Pose Tracking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14842)** - Studies how to combine sparse haptic signals (proprioception, force/torque, binary contact) with visual pose trackers under occlusion. Finds that a kinematic-aware finger-level encoder learns task-specific cross-modal gating, and sequential tracking evaluation is critical for distinguishing different fusion architectures.
*   **[TAC-LOCO: Unified Whole-Body Control for Quadrupedal TACtile-Informed LOCO-Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10132)** - A reinforcement learning framework that integrates tactile feedback from a gripper for unified leg, arm, and gripper control on a quadruped. The policy learns to regulate grasp force during dynamic loco-manipulation, achieving a 47% reduction in grasping force and less than 1% object drop rate.
*   **[VQ-Touch: A Data-Efficient Tactile Generation Framework Across Sensors and Scenarios](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14728)** - A tactile image generation framework (VQ-Touch) designed for cross-sensor and multi-scenario use. It uses a discrete diffusion model and a unified conditioning interface to generate high-fidelity tactile data from limited samples, reducing reliance on expensive sensors.

### **Visuomotor Policies & VLA Models**

*   **[Beyond Implicit Force: Evaluating Explicit Force-Torque Proxies in Action Chunking with Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14578)** - Examines how Action Chunking with Transformers (ACT) learns force awareness from the implicit tracking error in leader-follower teleoperation. It shows removing this implicit cue causes task failure, but adding simple joint-torque proxies (from motor current) effectively recovers contact-aware behavior.
*   **[Never Too Late for Force: Accelerating VLA Post-Training with Reactive Force Injection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14236)** - Introduces LIFT (Late Reactive Injection of Force), a force-aware post-training framework for pretrained VLAs. It grafts a reactive action expert that uses 6D force feedback to improve performance on contact-rich tasks like towel folding and insertion, enhanced with an online DAgger loop for distribution shift.
*   **[Safe-Night VLA: Seeing the Unseen via Thermal-Perceptive VLA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.05754)** - Integrates long-wave infrared (thermal) perception into a VLA model for safety-critical manipulation. It allows reasoning based on thermodynamic properties and enforces safety constraints via a Control Barrier Function filter, outperforming RGB-only baselines in unstructured environments.
*   **[Reflex: Real-Time VLA Control through Streaming Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14695)** - Addresses the latency issue of flow-matching VLAs. Realizes the "Timestep-Invariance Property" of perception encoders to enable `O(1)` incremental KV-cache updates, achieving a 2.58x speedup and 50Hz streaming inference for real-time deployment.
*   **[Lights, Camera, Malfunction: When Illumination Robustness Leaves VLA Models Blind to Color](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14698)** - Presents FLARE, an adversarial lighting attack that drops VLA success rates to zero. It also identifies a defensive pitfall: naive color augmentation can cause models to discard color information, and proposes "ChromaGuard" to preserve color-dependent task performance.
*   **[Image-to-Point Cloud Registration Made Easy with Rectified Flow-based LiDAR Upsampling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14639)** - A simple I2P method that treats LiDAR as an imaging sensor. It generates dense LiDAR intensity images using a rectified flow model, then matches them with camera images using a pre-trained feature matcher, achieving effective 6-DoF pose estimation.
*   **[DiMaS: Distribution Matching for Steering Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14280)** - A new steering strategy (DiMaS) for flow-matching VLAs. It uses distribution matching in representation space to control behavior (e.g., grasp style) rather than classical linear steering, which is shown to be insufficient in the visuomotor setting.
*   **[Steering Robustness into World Action Models via Mechanistic Interpretability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14943)** - Uses mechanistic interpretability to study robustness in WAMs. Finds low-dimensional steering directions for some models and proposes WA-LQR, a minimal-invasive feedback controller to steer model behavior against perturbations.

### **Teleoperation & Human-Robot Interaction**

*   **[AHEAD: Anticipatory Hand-Driven Teleoperation via Human Intent Prediction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15172)** - An anticipatory VR teleoperation system for pick-and-place. It predicts the operator's intended grasp object and placement slot from a short window of hand/head signals, reducing robot reaction latency by 0.6-1.4 seconds and lowering operator workload in user studies.
*   **[Zero2Skill: Bootstrapping Robot Skills through Autonomous Data Collection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14047)** - A human-robot symbiotic system for autonomous data collection. Human corrections are stored in "Corrective Memory" and reused, reducing working time to 16% compared to full teleoperation, while the resulting data trains policies competitive with teleoperation-trained ones.
*   **[HELP: Human-Efficient Large-Scale Robot Post-Training with Rollout Segmentation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09776)** - A post-training pipeline that maximizes human efficiency by having two specialized operators supervise 12 robots concurrently. It uses a learned "rollout segmentation critic" (VLAC-CUT) to separate useful rollout segments from failures for efficient retraining.

### **Sim-to-Real, World Models, and Generalization**

*   **[DriftWorld: Fast World Modeling through Drifting](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15065)** - An action-conditioned world model (DriftWorld) based on drifting generative models. It learns a single-step drift from observation to future frame, generating rollouts 17x faster than diffusion baselines while maintaining accuracy, enabling efficient online planning.
*   **[RoboTTT: Context Scaling for Robot Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15275)** - Introduces Test-Time Training for robot policies, scaling visuomotor context to 8K timesteps. This enables one-shot in-context imitation from human video, on-the-fly policy improvement, and robust long-horizon task completion, showing context length as a new scaling axis.
*   **[RADAR: Closed-Loop Robotic Data Generation via Semantic Planning and Autonomous Reset](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11811)** - A fully autonomous data generation engine. It uses a VLM for task planning, a GNN policy for execution, and a FSM for autonomous scene resets and failure recovery, achieving up to 90% success on complex long-horizon tasks in simulation and showing real-world applicability.
*   **[SafeRelBench: A Spatial-Relation-Aware Safety Benchmark for VLM-Driven Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14543)** - Introduces a benchmark to evaluate process-level safety in VLM-driven embodied agents. It reveals a gap between task success and safety compliance, showing that agents often violate safety constraints related to spatial relations (e.g., support, containment) during task execution.
*   **[ConFlow: Constraints-Guided Learning with Flow Matching for Motion Generation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14424)** - A constraint-guided flow matching framework for robot motion generation. It incorporates constraint information (e.g., collision, smoothness) directly into the training objective using a conditional Gaussian Process, improving trajectory quality and constraint satisfaction.
*   **[Representation-Aligned Tactile Grounding for Contact-Rich Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14609)** - Proposes a Latent Tactile Predictor (LTP) for VLA policies. By analyzing where tactile supervision is most effective, they found aligning it with intermediate action-expert features significantly outperforms predictions at other interfaces for contact-rich tasks.

### **Datasets & Benchmarks**

*   **[Open-AoE: An Open Egocentric Manipulation Dataset and Toolchain for Embodied Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14183)** - A large-scale dataset of 2,000 hours of egocentric manipulation videos from 500+ contributors. It provides structured annotations (MANO hand poses, trajectories, actions) and a toolchain for data processing and training robot learning models.
*   **[Beyond Visual Grasping: Benchmarking Complex Grasping from Detection to Execution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14341)** - Introduces GCA-Bench, a benchmark for "grasping with complex action" that requires multi-step reasoning and semantic understanding. Evaluations show current large foundational models achieve success rates below 70%, highlighting critical limitations.
*   **[GigaWorld-Policy-0.5: A Faster and Stronger WAM Empowered by AutoResearch](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13960)** - An action-centered World Action Model that uses future visual dynamics only during training for supervision, but decodes actions only at inference. It achieves an 85ms inference latency on an RTX 4090 by separating visual and action experts in a Mixture-of-Transformers architecture.

### **Object-level Mapping & Scene Change Detection**

*   **[OASIS-Map: Object-Level Change Detection in Multi-Session Mapping](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14899)** - A multi-session mapping system (OASIS-Map) for maintaining spatio-temporally consistent object-level maps. It uses patch-level semantic correspondences to detect scene changes and incrementally associate objects across revisits for long-term robotic inspection.
*   **[PanoAffordanceNet: Towards Holistic Affordance Grounding in 360° Indoor Environments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.09760)** - Proposes the task of holistic affordance grounding in 360° indoor environments and introduces a dedicated dataset and framework, PanoAffordanceNet, to address challenges like geometric distortion and semantic dispersion in equirectangular projection.
