### Dexterous Manipulation & Robotic Hands

**[29] SyLink Hand: A Synergy-Inspired Linkage-Driven Anthropomorphic Hand for Human-Like Dexterity**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14250))
Presents the SyLink Hand, an anthropomorphic hand that uses biomechanical synergy principles and optimized linkage mechanisms to coordinate joint motions, achieving human-like kinematics with 19 joints driven by 11 actuators in a compact, low-cost design.

**[42] ORCA: A Platform for Open-Source Dexterity Research**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14561))
Introduces an open-source research stack for dexterous manipulation, unifying control, simulation, teleoperation (via VR), and retargeting for anthropomorphic hands. Integrates with the `lerobot` framework, demonstrating an end-to-end workflow for in-hand reorientation.

**[45] Impedance MPC with Disturbance Estimation for Dexterous Hand Control**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14606))
Presents an Impedance Model Predictive Control (MPC) framework for dexterous fingers. It uses an actuator-agnostic formulation to enable precise trajectory tracking and compliant contact, achieving high tracking accuracy and fast rejection of contact disturbances.

**[71] EquiDexFlow: Contact-Grounded SE(3)-Equivariant Dexterous Grasp Generative Flows**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12728))
A flow-matching model that jointly predicts wrist pose, joint angles, and contact forces for dexterous grasping. The architecture is SE(3)-equivariant and ensures forces lie within the Coulomb friction cone, demonstrating successful open-loop pick-and-hold on a physical robot.

### Tactile Sensing & Haptics

**[2] Scalable Dynamic Tactile Sensing Enabled by Passive and Flexible Acoustic Waveguides**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13746))
Presents a passive tactile sensing paradigm using acoustic waveguides. It achieves high-resolution real-time localization and waveform reconstruction for low-frequency signals, demonstrated on fingertip arrays, tactile gloves, and large-area skins, detecting stimuli from hair contact to arterial pulses.

**[19] The N2D Haptic Glove: A Multi-Finger Glove for 2D Directional Force Feedback for Contact Rich Manipulation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14083))
A multi-finger wearable haptic glove that renders planar directional forces at the fingertip. A user study shows that planar directional feedback significantly reduces contact force error and improves consistency during teleoperated manipulation compared to visual-only or single-axis haptic feedback.

### Visuomotor & Imitation Learning Policies

**[3] μ0: A Scalable 3D Interaction-Trace World Model**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13769))
Predicts future 3D trajectories of interaction points (objects, hands) as a compact, embodiment-agnostic motion interface. Trained on diverse video data with automatic trace extraction, it can be paired with downstream action experts to achieve competitive manipulation performance without action-supervised pretraining.

**[31] ReactVLA: Fast and Lightweight Reactive Robot Manipulation via Improved Mean Flow Action Generation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14255))
A lightweight VLA framework that reduces diffusion-based action generation to one-to-few steps using an improved Mean Flow method. Achieves up to a 1.65x improvement on precision tasks and over 4x faster inference than leading VLA models, enabling fast reactive control.

**[40] Spatially Conditioned Diffusion Policy: Learning Precise and Robust Manipulation with a Single RGB Camera**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14535))
A diffusion-based visuomotor policy using a single global RGB camera. It uses a spatial conditioning module that samples features along predicted end-effector trajectories, achieving precise manipulation comparable to multi-camera setups.

**[48] EgoGuide: Egocentric Guidance for Efficient Robot-Free Demonstration Collection and Learning**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14665))
Introduces a data collection interface that records synchronized wrist and head/egocentric views with online quality guidance. A Gated Egocentric Residual Policy handles the viewpoint-varying egocentric camera, improving data efficiency and robustness under visual occlusion.

**[73] Improving Robotic Generalist Policies via Flow Reversal Steering**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13675))
Proposes Flow Reversal Steering (FRS) for flow-matching-based generalist policies. It reverses suboptimal actions to their latent noise and steers them toward better action modes, enabling zero-shot control from VLMs and policy improvement via RL-based bootstrapping without training the base policy.

### World Models & Simulation for Manipulation

**[8] ContactWorld: What Matters in Vision-Tactile World Models for Contact-Rich Manipulation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13877))
A systematic benchmark for vision-tactile world models in contact-rich tasks. Finds that spatially structured (point-cloud) and temporally continuous representations are key for long-horizon planning, and that tactile force-field representations improve performance when spatially compatible with visual observations.

**[23] Robustness without Wrinkles: Parallel Simulation and Robust MPC for Certified Deformable Manipulation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14188))
Presents CORD-SLS for safe deformable object manipulation (ropes, cloth). It uses a GPU-parallel differentiable simulator for gradient-based planning, a robust MPC algorithm, and conformal prediction to calibrate perception bounds for high-probability safe control in real-time.

**[51] WAM4D: Fast 4D World Action Model via Spatial Register Tokens**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14048))
Uses lightweight spatial register tokens during training to transfer pretrained geometric priors into a causal video-action transformer, then removes them for lightweight inference. Improves spatial consistency and action prediction in 4D world action models.

**[68] FAWAM: Force-Aware World Action Models for Closed-Loop Contact-Rich Manipulation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.08555))
Incorporates force feedback at three levels: perception, prediction, and closed-loop execution. A residual correction module uses predicted wrench trajectories to refine actions in real time, significantly improving success rates on contact-rich tasks over vision-only baselines.

**[77] OGPO: Sample Efficient Full-Finetuning of Generative Control Policies**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.03065))
An off-policy RL algorithm for fine-tuning generative control policies (diffusion/flow models). Uses a modified PPO objective to propagate gradients through the full generative process, achieving state-of-the-art performance on tasks like precise insertion and dexterous manipulation, especially from poor BC initializations.

### Sim-to-Real & Reinforcement Learning

**[6] Efficient Domain-Adaptive Policy Learning via Kernel Representation with Application to Quadrotor Control under Non-Stationary Disturbances**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13842))
Models unknown sim-to-real gaps as disturbances via a differentiable kernel approximation (random Fourier features). Enables rapid offline training and real-time online adaptation by updating kernel parameters via least-squares, demonstrated on quadrotor control under various disturbances.

**[12] Learning Dynamic Swing-Up of an Inverted Pendulum using Remote Magnetic Actuation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13915))
Demonstrates the first swing-up of a magnetically actuated inverted pendulum using Iterative Learning Control (ILC) to overcome complex magnetic actuation dynamics. ILC refines commands from trial to trial, successfully stabilizing an otherwise infeasible maneuver.

**[43] Sensitivity Shaping for Latent Modeling**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14585))
Introduces a regularization method for learned dynamics models that promotes sensitivity to control inputs in high-support regions. This improves out-of-distribution detection and safer closed-loop planning in vision-based obstacle avoidance and manipulation.

**[54] Provably Safe, Yet Scalable Reinforcement Learning**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14536))
A two-phase RL framework for learning provably safe policies. A learned "backup policy" generates an implicit control-invariant set online, which is then used in a differentiable projection layer to enforce safety guarantees, scaling to higher-dimensional tasks than previous provably safe methods.

**[105] SkillAudit: Ground-Truth-Free Skill Evolution via Paired Trajectory Auditing**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14239))
An offline framework for evolving agent skills (procedural packages) without any ground-truth feedback. It works by comparing trajectories from runs with and without the skill to diagnose failures and generate targeted edits, outperforming static expert skills.

**[228] An Analysis of the Coordination Gap between Joint and Modular Learning for Job Shop Scheduling with Transportation Resources**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24117))
Investigates when joint training of agents is necessary for job-shop scheduling with transport. Quantifies the "coordination gap," showing modular training is a viable alternative when a single scheduling task dominates in bottleneck environments.

**[262] RAMAC: Multimodal Risk-Aware Offline Reinforcement Learning and the Role of Behavior Regularization**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.02695))
Introduces RAMAC, a risk-averse offline RL framework combining an expressive generative (diffusion) actor with a distributional critic. Optimizes for Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) while a behavioral cloning term suppresses out-of-distribution actions, showing improved performance on stochastic tasks.

### Navigation & Autonomous Driving

**[5] Multi-Agent Embodied Autonomous Driving: From V2X Information Exchange to Shared World Models**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13840))
A survey of multi-agent autonomous driving, reviewing literature on V2X communication, collaborative perception, and cooperative planning. Identifies key research priorities for safe multi-agent systems, including verifiable shared states and robust coordination under communication constraints.

**[9] AnyGoal: Vision-Language Guided Multi-Agent Exploration for Training-Free Lifelong Navigation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13878))
A training-free multi-robot navigation system that uses a VLM for frontier-based exploration and a shared Bayesian Value Map (BVM) for lifelong evidence accumulation. Achieves state-of-the-art results on the GOAT-Bench by using distributed coordination without a central controller.

**[32] FloVerse: Floor Plan-Guided Multi-Modal Navigation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14267))
Introduces FloVerse, a task for floor plan-guided navigation unifying PointNav, ObjectNav, and ImageNav. Proposes ThreeDiff, a two-stage IL policy that uses a diffusion-based goal-reasoning module trained via masked-modality modeling to implicitly capture spatial priors from floor plans.

**[56] Cross-Stage Sensorimotor Perception Scheduling and Sparse Map Encoding for Efficient Edge Embodied Navigation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.14154))
Optimizes ObjectGoal navigation on edge hardware. Introduces SKIP, an adaptive scheduler that safely skips semantic mapping steps, and SCOUT, a sparse-context encoder using submanifold convolutions, achieving up to a 1.7x speedup and 50% lower memory usage.

**[58] Schrödinger's Navigator: Imagining an Ensemble of Futures for Zero-Shot Object Navigation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21201))
A belief-aware framework for zero-shot navigation that reasons over multiple imagined 3D futures. Uses a Future-Aware Value Map to aggregate these futures for robust action selection, outperforming baselines in occlusion-heavy environments on a real Go2 robot.

### Safety, Robustness, & Evaluation

**[7] Output-Level Regularization Eliminates the Seed Lottery in Single-GPU VLA Fine-Tuning**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13856))
Identifies the "seed lottery" in VLA fine-tuning, where the same code can fail catastrophically due to random seed. Traces the cause to "output collapse" and shows that output-level regularizers like VICReg and Dropout eliminate this failure mode, while weight-level methods do not.

**[11] PhysVLA: Towards Physically-Grounded VLA for Embodied Robotic Manipulation**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13886))
A plug-and-play inference-time framework to wrap frozen VLA models with physical grounding. Uses a phase-aware FSM and a selective Euler-Lagrange correction gate to enforce rigid-body dynamics, improving success rates and trajectory quality on existing VLA backbones without any retraining.

**[20] Self-Improving VLA Policies: Selected Diffusion Noise for Spurious-Robust Action Smoothing**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14084))
A test-time method for diffusion-based VLAs that selects noise vectors to be robust to spurious correlations and produce smoother actions. Consistently improves success rates and reduces action jitter across multiple VLA policies and benchmarks.

**[28] When and How Severely: Scenario-Specific Safety Envelopes for Driving VLAs**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14238))
Evaluates safety envelopes of driving VLA models using a GMM to identify discrete severity bands of failure. Shows that aggregate safety metrics mask scenario-specific vulnerabilities, arguing for two-dimensional (when/how severely) safety specifications over single aggregate values.

**[38] CADET: Physics-Grounded Causal Auditing and Training-Free Deconfounding of End-to-End Driving Planners**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14438))
A training-free framework to detect and repair spurious correlations (e.g., learning roadside objects) in end-to-end driving planners without parameter updates, making them more reliable in long-tail scenarios.

**[46] Safe Reinforcement Learning of Autonomous Highway Driving: A Unified Framework for Safety and Efficiency**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14609))
A unified safe RL framework (MoE-RM-SRL) that integrates safe distance rules, reward machines, and a mixture-of-experts (MoE) architecture to improve both safety and efficiency in autonomous highway driving, validated in CARLA and a VR driving simulator.

**[53] Causal Object-Centric Models for Planning with Monte Carlo Tree Search**
([Link](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14418))
Introduces COMET, a model-based RL algorithm that performs MCTS in a slot-structured latent space. It uses object-causal attention for decision-making, achieving faster learning on visually diverse tasks compared to monolithic baselines.
