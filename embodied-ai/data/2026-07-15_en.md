### Dexterous Manipulation & In-Hand Manipulation

*   **[Robust In-Hand Manipulation via Priors in Reinforcement Learning and Mechanical Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12105)**
    This paper introduces two physics priors for learning robust in-hand rolling without external sensing. A global grasp-quality prior (from classical grasp analysis) is used for reward shaping, while a local contact-geometry prior is embedded in fingertip curvature. These priors significantly improve rotation efficiency, grasp stability, and disturbance rejection in sim-to-real transfer for a multifingered hand.

*   **[A Minimalist Retargeting-Guided Reinforcement Learning Recipe for Dexterous Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11874)**
    This work presents REGRIND, a simple pipeline for learning dexterous manipulation from a single human demo. It retargets human hand-object motion to preserve spatial/contact relationships, then uses residual RL to track object-centric keypoints in simulation. It achieves fluid, human-like behavior on real hardware for tasks like scissors and screwdriver manipulation.

*   **[DECO: Decoupled Multimodal Diffusion Transformer for Bimanual Dexterous Manipulation with a Plugin Tactile Adapter](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.05513)**
    Proposes DECO, a diffusion transformer that decouples vision, proprioception, and tactile inputs via specialized conditioning pathways. A lightweight tactile adapter improves performance on contact-rich tasks. It is trained on a 50-hour bimanual manipulation dataset (DECO-50) and achieves a 21% improvement over baselines.

### Foundation Models & Visuomotor Policies (VLA, WAM)

*   **[VistaVLA: Geometry- and Semantic-Aware 3D Gaussian-Grounded VLA for Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12356)**
    VistaVLA constructs a 3D cognitive map from Gaussian primitives, lifting multi-view features into 3D. A "Merge-then-Query" mechanism compresses dense Gaussians into compact spatial tokens, achieving a 99% token reduction while preserving geometric and semantic context. It improves success rates by 22.8% on real-world tasks over baselines.

*   **[FlowWAM: Optical Flow as a Unified Action Representation for World Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.13017)**
    This work proposes using optical flow as a "video-native" action representation for World Action Models (WAMs). This allows joint modeling of RGB and flow videos within a pre-trained video generator, enabling both action prediction and future video generation. It achieves high success rates on manipulation tasks and improves world modeling accuracy.

*   **[TS-Mask VLA: 2D Temporal-Spatial Masking for Vision-Language-Action Model with Effective Bridging](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09818)**
    TS-Mask VLA introduces a Discrete Diffusion Action Expert with a bridge attention module and a 2D masking strategy for discrete action tokens. This improves the modeling of cross-time dependencies and inter-dimensional coupling, leading to stronger long-horizon performance on benchmarks like LIBERO and CALVIN.

*   **[Harness VLA: Steering Frozen VLAs into Reliable Manipulation Primitives via Memory-Guided Agents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08448)**
    This framework treats a frozen VLA as a retryable, contact-rich primitive. It composes this VLA with analytic skills for grounding, transport, and placement using a memory-augmented agent. This approach extends pre-trained VLAs to handle deployment perturbations (e.g., spatial-layout shifts) without any fine-tuning, showing large gains on LIBERO-Pro and RoboCasa.

### Teleoperation & Data Collection

*   **[Smooth Operator: A Real-Time Sampling-Based Algorithm for Kinematic Hand Retargeting](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07491)**
    Introduces SBR, a sampling-based retargeting method for mapping human hand poses to robot hands. It is designed for low-jitter, real-time operation. A user study with 18 participants shows it reduces operator cognitive fatigue (lowest NASA-TLX score) and achieves the highest task success rate compared to gradient-based baselines.

*   **[LapSurgie: Humanoid Robots Performing Surgery via Teleoperated Handheld Laparoscopy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.03529)**
    Presents the first humanoid-robot-based laparoscopic teleoperation framework. It uses an inverse-mapping strategy for manual-wristed tools, adhering to remote center-of-motion constraints for precise hand-to-tool control of off-the-shelf surgical instruments, offering a deployable platform for telesurgery.

### Tactile Sensing & Contact-Rich Control

*   **[TouchThinker: Scaling Tactile Commonsense Reasoning to the Open World with Large-scale Data and Action-aware Representation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11637)**
    This work introduces TouchThinker-1M, a million-scale tactile reasoning dataset covering many objects and sensors. It also proposes an action-aware modeling mechanism to improve tactile representation efficiency for tactile-language models, enabling competitive performance across multiple benchmarks.

*   **[TACTIC: Tactile and Vision Conditioned Contact-Centric Control for Whole-Arm Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09218)**
    Proposes a receding-horizon controller for whole-arm manipulation. It combines RGB-D and distributed tactile sensing with a learned dynamics model and analytical kinematics. The controller uses contact Jacobians for action sampling and predicts contact configurations and forces, consistently outperforming baselines in simulation and on real-world tasks.

*   **[Context Probing for Tactile Few-Shot Class-Incremental Learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25115)**
    Addresses the challenge of tactile recognition when sensing varies due to different acquisition contexts (e.g., devices, contact states). The proposed CoP-FSCIL framework uses context-probing to diagnose and suppress context-sensitive variations in tactile representations, improving few-shot class-incremental learning on datasets like HapTex.

### Sim-to-Real & Reinforcement Learning

*   **[Action Map Policy: Learning 3D Closed-loop Manipulation via Pixel Classification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10706)**
    AMP reformulates 3D manipulation as a classification problem in image space. By projecting 3D actions onto camera image planes and treating pixel locations as discrete classes, the method controls dimensionality while preserving multi-modality and visual signals. It achieves faster inference than diffusion policies and strong performance on various tasks.

*   **[Pix2Act: Image-Space Manipulation Policies with Equivariant Augmentation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11167)**
    Representing actions as 2D trajectories in the camera plane (image-space keypoints) allows for equivariant data augmentation. Jointly rotating camera images with their action trajectories improves generalization and performance across diverse manipulation tasks, making the policy robust to camera perturbations.

*   **[Jetson-PI: Towards Onboard Real-Time Robot Control via Foresight-Aligned Asynchronous Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12659)**
    Addresses the challenge of deploying VLA models on low-power onboard devices like the Jetson Orin. It uses a lightweight correction module to predict future states, mitigating perception-execution misalignment from asynchronous inference. This achieves 8.66x improvement in control frequency over baselines while maintaining high success rates.

*   **[Measure the Sim-to-Real Gap: Designing an Affordable Real-World Benchmark Platform for RL in AIoT Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10309)**
    Proposes an affordable real-world benchmark platform for studying the Sim-to-Real gap in RL. The platform uses a robot agent playing video games, which is inherently safe. Experimental results show a 1160% performance degradation when transferring a simulation-trained agent to the real physical platform, highlighting a significant Sim-to-Real gap.

### Systems & Simulators for Robotics

*   **[VAST: A Vision-Assisted Framework for Learning Agile, Stable, and Safe Quadruped Locomotion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.15995)**
    **Note:** This paper's official title is "PUMA: Perception-driven Unified Foothold Prior for Mobility Augmented Quadruped Parkour". It presents an end-to-end learning framework that integrates visual perception for parkour. It estimates egocentric polar foothold priors (relative distance and heading) from terrain features to guide the robot's posture adaptation, demonstrating agility and robustness on complex discrete terrains.

*   **[Pipette: An Embodied Simulation Platform, Benchmark, and Data-Efficient Augmentation Framework for Wet-Lab Robotics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12936)**
    Pipette is a simulation platform for wet-lab robotics. It provides over 100 open-source lab assets and a data augmentation pipeline that replays human demos in simulation with various perturbations. This approach significantly improves VLA training from limited demonstrations, achieving 71.8% success on SmolVLA (up from 40.4%) on a 12-task benchmark.

*   **[RoboDesign1M: A Large-scale Dataset for Robot Design Understanding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.06796)**
    Introduces a large-scale, multimodal dataset of 1 million samples from scientific literature for robot design understanding. It includes design images, VQA data, and retrieval tasks, serving as a new benchmark for AI-powered design assistants and automated design generation.

### Understanding & Reasoning for Robotics

*   **[See like a Robot: Robot-Centric Pointmaps for Vision-Language-Action Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11498)**
    This paper addresses the frame mismatch between camera observations and robot actions. By using "robot-centric pointmaps" (images storing 3D coordinates in the robot's frame), the VLA policy observes the scene directly in the action space, making it more robust to different camera viewpoints. This improves performance on RoboCasa and real-world robots, especially under unseen camera placements.

*   **[VIA: Visual Interface Agent for Robot Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11119)**
    VIA re-frames robot control as a software agent task. An off-the-shelf FM (e.g., Claude Code) controls a manipulator through a browser-based 3D interface (taking screenshots, issuing commands). Without any robot-specific fine-tuning, it achieves 96.7% success on LIBERO-Goal tasks, showing that a general-purpose coding agent can act as a robot control agent with the right visual interface.

*   **[ActiveFly-Bench: Aligning Embodied Question Answering with Vision-Language-Action for Aerial Embodied Perception](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10180)**
    A benchmark for UAV embodied perception that bridges high-level reasoning (EQA), behavior planning, and fine-grained control (FLUC). The results show current VLA models struggle with behavior planning and viewpoint adjustment for active perception tasks, highlighting a significant gap for aerial embodied intelligence.
