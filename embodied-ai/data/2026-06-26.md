### Dexterous Manipulation & Hands

*   **[Play2Perfect: What Matters in Dexterous Play Pretraining for Precise Assembly?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26428)**: Proposes Play2Perfect, an RL framework for task-agnostic pretraining through diverse play to acquire reusable manipulation priors (grasping, in-hand reorientation). This prior enables 33x more sample-efficient fine-tuning on precise assembly tasks like tight insertions (0.5mm clearance) and multi-part assembly, demonstrating zero-shot sim-to-real transfer.
*   **[VibeAct: Vibration to Actions for Contact-Rich Reactive Robot Dexterity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27344)**: Proposes VibeAct, a framework bridging real vibrotactile sensing (piezoelectric microphones in a hand) with simulation-based RL via a shared contact/slip representation. A tactile estimator predicts contact/slip from microphone waveforms, enabling reactive dexterous policies for tasks like regrasping and in-hand reorientation.
*   **[Tactile-WAM: Touch-Aware World Action Model with Tactile Asymmetric Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26663)**: Introduces Tactile-WAM, a touch-aware World Action Model with a Tactile Asymmetric Attention Mechanism (TAAM) that prevents "tactile pollution" of visual predictions. It uses a VideoClean mask to keep tactile tokens for action generation without degrading video prediction, improving success rates on contact-rich tasks by 86%.
*   **[PressMimic: Pressure-Guided Motion Capture and Control for Humanoid Robot Imitation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26741)**: Integrates pressure sensing into the full humanoid imitation pipeline (perception and control). FRAPPE++ uses RGB + pressure for better pose estimation, and a Pressure-Supervised Policy (PSP) uses pressure signals in RL to produce physically consistent contact patterns.

### Visuomotor Policies & Embodied AI (VLA)

*   **[RMTL: Reinforced Micro-task Learning for Long-Horizon Manipulation with VLM Rewards](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26175)**: Decomposes long-horizon tasks into micro-tasks with stage-specific VLM prompts for more informative rewards. The agent uses a hierarchical manager to switch between micro-tasks, drastically improving learning speed compared to a single global VLM reward.
*   **[CoStream: Composing Simple Behaviors for Generalizable Complex Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26423)**: Framework orchestrating foundation models into composable core behaviors (semantic, predictive, reactive). These behaviors output to a shared SE(3) interface, composed via right-multiplication for high-precision tasks like GPU seating. Demonstrates robust performance on 8 real-world tasks.
*   **[SSI-Policy: Learning Structured Scene Interfaces for Vision-Language Robotic Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26800)**: Proposes a robot-agnostic RGB-only intermediate representation (SSI) encoding monocular depth, object layouts, and 2D motion trajectories. This decouples perception from control, enabling few-shot learning (only 10 demos) on LIBERO, improving prior methods by 15%.
*   **[Improving VLA Model Fine-Tuning with Structured Stage and Keyframe Supervision (StaKe)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26801)**: A plug-in auxiliary supervision framework that uses demonstration gripper states to automatically create a stage classifier and keyframe predictor. These heads enrich representations during VLA fine-tuning, improving success rates by up to 56% on long-horizon tasks.
*   **[LA4VLA: Learning to Act without Seeing via Language-Action Pretraining](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27295)**: Proposes language-action pretraining to acquire reusable manipulation skills without visual observations. This reduces reliance on visual shortcuts and improves VLA policy robustness, boosting success rates by up to 45% in real-world tasks.
*   **[RouterVLA: Turning Smoke Tests into Supervision for Heterogeneous VLA Selection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27355)**: Studies whether pre-deployment test rollouts can be reused to supervise policy selection. A simple probe-success rule significantly improves held-out success, showing that commissioning-aware routing is a powerful complement to individual policy scaling.
*   **[World Action Models Enable Continual Imitation Learning with Recurrent Generative Replays (REGEN)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27374)**: Uses World Action Models to synthesize pseudo-replay trajectories for continual imitation learning. REGEN reduces catastrophic forgetting by up to 50% compared to fine-tuning without requiring stored original demonstrations.
*   **[Scalable Behavior Cloning with Open Data, Training, and Evaluation (ABC)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27375)**: Releases the largest open-source teleoperation dataset (ABC-130K) and a full stack for BC. Trains policies on diverse tasks including dexterous ones like box folding, and provides a simulation pipeline for correlated real-world evaluation.
*   **[PAMAE: Phase-Aware-MoE Action Experts Towards Reliable Flow-Matching VLA Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27144)**: Replaces a single action expert in a flow-matching VLA with a phase-aware sparse mixture-of-experts. A router allocates actions to different experts based on the execution stage, improving task success by 9.2%.
*   **[PhysReflect-VLA: Physical Feasibility and Self-Reflective Regulation for Reliable VLA Policies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27146)**: A plug-and-play framework for VLA policies that adds an execution-time reliability loop. A feasibility operator checks dynamic consistency, and an LLM-based reflection module analyzes state discrepancies for corrective guidance, improving robustness by 5.4%.
*   **[In-Context World Modeling for Robotic Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26025)**: Introduces ICWM, where robot policies infer system variables (e.g., camera viewpoint) from a short history of self-generated interactions. This enables adaptation to novel configurations without parameter updates, outperforming standard VLA baselines.

### Teleoperation & Data Collection

*   **[Bridging Handheld and Teleoperated Supervision... (BRIDGE)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26603)**: Finds that mixing handheld (observed actions) and teleoperation (desired actions) data can hurt performance. Proposes BRIDGE, a mixture of diffusion policy experts that routes tasks to phase-specific heads (e.g., use teleop data for contact phases), improving success by up to 36.7%.
*   **[HumanoidUMI: Bridging Robot-Free Demonstrations and Humanoid Whole-Body Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27239)**: Extends the UMI concept to humanoids. Uses VR devices and UMI-inspired grippers for robot-free whole-body data collection, which trains a high-level policy to predict keypoints for a whole-body controller.
*   **[Learning to Fold: prizewinning solution at LeHome Challenge 2026](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27163)**: A solution for bimanual garment folding. It combines VLA policy with a self-supervised RL loop where the model acts as its own value function, enabling efficient sim-to-real transfer and achieving 1st place in simulation.

### Sim-to-Real & Generalization

*   **[LiMoDE: Rethinking Lifelong Robot Manipulation...](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26183)**: A lifelong learning scheme using dynamic Mixture-of-Experts. A multi-task pre-training stage extracts prior knowledge, and a lifelong adaptation mechanism learns new experts that combine with frozen ones for new tasks, preventing catastrophic forgetting.
*   **[IDEA: Insensitive to Dynamics Mismatch via Effect Alignment for Sim-to-Real Transfer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26575)**: Improves sim-to-real for multi-agent control by combining random environmental structures with discrete semantic actions and an action synchronization mechanism. This elevates policy learning to a semantic abstraction level, insensitive to low-level dynamics mismatch.
*   **[Learning to Recover Task Experts from a Multi-Task Merged Model (ReTeX)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26902)**: Models parameter interference in multi-task merging as additive offsets and learns to predict and undo them to recover task-expert performance from a single checkpoint, achieving >95% of individual expert performance.

### Humanoids & Loco-Manipulation

*   **[OmniContact: Chaining Meta-Skills via Contact Flow for Generalizable Humanoid Loco-Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26201)**: A hierarchical framework centered on "contact flow." A low-level policy learns a library of loco-manipulation skills, and a high-level module synthesizes future contact-flow sequences for robust long-horizon chaining, achieving 98.7% success on "carry box."
*   **[A System for Fast, Resilient, and Adaptable Loco-Manipulation Behaviors on Humanoid Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26425)**: A robot-local, runtime-editable behavior authoring system combining Affordance Templates and Behavior Trees. Deployed on multiple humanoid robots (Atlas, Valkyrie, Nadia, H1-2), demonstrating fast adaptation of loco-manipulation tasks in minutes.
*   **[Humanoid-DART: Humanoid Loco-Manipulation using Diffusion-guided Augmentation...](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26855)**: A self-supervised framework bootstrapping from sparse demos. It combines diffusion-based trajectory generation with RL tracking to progressively expand a goal-conditioned policy's behavioral repertoire for loco-manipulation.
*   **[TaskNPoint: How to Teach Your Humanoid to Hit a Backhand in Minutes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26215)**: A training protocol where a human provides a few key inputs (skill set, one demo, interaction window). RL fills in the trajectory, enabling skill learning (hitting, kicking, picking) in under an hour on a single GPU with zero-shot generalization to new target locations.
*   **[Racing a Wheeled Quadruped: Active Load Transfer Mitigation via MPC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26313)**: Combines MPC and RL for active roll control during racing of a wheeled quadruped. The MPC minimizes lateral load transfer (LTR), improving lap time by 8.7% and peak lateral acceleration by 21.3%.
