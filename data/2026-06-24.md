### Superconductivity

*   **Electron-Hole Asymmetry in Trilayer Hubbard Model:** [Yushi Yamada et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24126) studied the trilayer Hubbard model using the fluctuation exchange approximation, finding that superconductivity is more favored in the hole-doped regime near 1/3 filling for large onsite $U$.
*   **Even-Denominator FQHS at $\nu=3/4$ and 5/4:** [Siddharth Kumar Singh et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23990) discovered new even-denominator fractional quantum Hall states in the lowest Landau level of a wide GaAs quantum well, explained by a bilayer composite fermion framework.
*   **Pairing Symmetry of Moiré Graphene:** [Sayak Biswas et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23994) constrained the superconducting order parameters in magic-angle moiré graphene, proposing definitive tests to distinguish between singlet and triplet pairing via Zeeman field response experiments.
*   **Multiple Topological Phases in K₂Cr₃As₃:** [Seigo Ogawa et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24108) reported NMR evidence of a spin-triplet topological superconductor in K₂Cr₃As₃ with three distinct phases: helical, chiral, and a line-nodal phase, showing high tunability.
*   **Density-Wave Enhanced Phonon Thermal Hall Effect:** [Qiaochao Xiang et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24125) observed a phonon thermal Hall effect in trilayer La₄Ni₃O₁₀ that is strongly enhanced below the density-wave transition, pointing to magnon-phonon hybridization as the mechanism.
*   **Superconducting Berry Curvature Dipole:** [Oles Matsyshyn et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.21363) revealed a superconducting Berry curvature dipole (BCD) in noncentrosymmetric superconductors that is sensitive to the order parameter phase, enabling dissipationless nonreciprocal responses.
*   **Coulomb Screening of Superconductivity in MATBG:** [Julien Barrier et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.01577) showed that increasing the carrier density in an adjacent bilayer completely suppresses superconductivity in magic-angle twisted bilayer graphene, supporting an unconventional pairing mechanism.

*   **Domain-Wall Drift in a Ferrimagnet:** [Murod Mirzhalilov et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23831) investigated domain-wall dynamics in ferrimagnets under an oscillating in-plane field, finding that weak out-of-plane bias can rectify wall oscillations into a finite drift velocity.
*   **Gate-Controlled Spin Qubits in Altermagnets:** [Hamed Vakili](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24150) proposed gate-defined spin qubits in altermagnetic quantum dots, achieving qubit rotations and two-qubit entanglement without spin-orbit coupling.
*   **Electrical Readout of Néel Vector in vdW AFM:** [Raghvendra Posti et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24527) demonstrated electrical detection of 180-degree Néel vector reversal in atomically thin CrSBr via spin-dependent tunneling magnetoresistance.
*   **Proximity-Induced Spin Reorientation in CrI₃:** [Dániel Tibor Pozsár et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24305) found that WTe₂ substrate reorients the magnetization of monolayer CrI₃ from out-of-plane to in-plane and enhances the magnetic ordering.
*   **Altermagnet-Superconductor Heterostructure for Braiding:** [Themba Hodge et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.08095) proposed an altermagnet-superconductor heterostructure as a scalable platform for braiding Majorana zero modes, enabling topological quantum gates.
*   **Emergent Surface Altermagnetism in Antiferromagnets:** [Colin Lange et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.08773) classified over 150 antiferromagnetic materials with emergent altermagnetism at their surfaces, resolving contradictions in experimental ARPES reports.
*   **Fractional Magnonic Frequency Combs:** [Qian-Nan Huang et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24673) reported fractional magnonic frequency combs in a magnetic sphere driven by a main and a detuned microwave, generating high-density spectral grids via parametric three-magnon scattering.

*   **Chiral Anomaly Induced Transport:** [Rishi G. Gopalakrishnan et al.](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.21498) investigated chiral anomaly-induced planar transport coefficients in 3D spin-orbit coupled metals, predicting unique angular and exponential temperature dependences.
