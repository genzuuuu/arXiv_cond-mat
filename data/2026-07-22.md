### Topological & Correlated Matter: New Electronic Phases & Spin Textures

*   **Weyl Excitonic Insulators & Sound-Induced Hall Currents:** Theoretical work shows that in gapped Weyl excitonic insulators, a sound wave can generate a dissipative Hall current. This is a new transport signature that probes the interaction-generated mass in these materials and is distinct from transport in gapless Weyl semimetals. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18335)]
*   **Fragile Topology Instability:** A systematic framework demonstrates that fragile topological phases are not stable under unit-cell enlargement (translation refinement). They can be adiabatically connected to an atomic insulator in a finite supercell, suggesting their physical signatures are not unique to fragile topology and can be altered by symmetry-breaking perturbations like charge-density waves. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18386)]
*   **Exotic Order in Parabolic Kagome Semimetal:** A study of an interacting kagome lattice model reveals a hierarchy of unconventional orders, including loop-current order, altermagnetism, and spin-loop-current order. This work highlights quadratic band-touching semimetals as a promising platform for novel symmetry-breaking phases. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18389)]
*   **Altermagnetic Magnons & Photomodulation:** Experiments on the altermagnet MnTe reveal an extremely weak intrinsic anisotropy, making it highly susceptible to optical tuning. A photoinduced enhancement of this anisotropy can drive coherent spin waves, enabling optical and mechanical control over the spin-splitting in the electronic band structure. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18421)]
*   **Asymmetric Antibimerons:** A new topological spin texture called an asymmetric antibimeron (AAB) is theoretically predicted. This in-plane texture can be stabilized by anisotropic Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interaction, and its collisions can manipulate the system's topological charge. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10557)]

### Unconventional Superconductivity & Superfluidity

*   **Chiral Superconductivity from Doping:** Thermodynamic-limit evidence is provided for chiral superconductivity emerging from doping a chiral topological phase (Hofstadter-Hubbard model). This work shows a uniform chiral superconducting state with a finite complex pairing order parameter in the infinite system limit. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21503)]
*   **Self-Consistent GW Theory for SrTiO3:** A full frequency- and momentum-dependent *GW* calculation shows that the pairing scale in SrTiO3 is dramatically overestimated by one-shot methods. Self-consistency is crucial, and the pairing is driven by Fröhlich phonons screened by incipient ferroelectricity. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18757)]
*   **Josephson Diode Effect in a Helimagnet:** Experimentally, a Josephson diode effect with efficiency >20% is observed at zero applied field in a junction using the chiral helimagnet Cr1/3NbS2. The effect is linked to the material's spin chirality and pinned Abrikosov vortices. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.01379)]
*   **Non-Hermitian Superfluids:** A theoretical framework for collective modes in non-Hermitian fermionic superfluids is developed. It predicts a new gapped phase mode, absent in Hermitian systems, and a resonance spectrum dependent on the initial phase of the order parameter. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18814)]
*   **Higher-Winding Topological Superconductors:** A coefficient-based method maps out the phase diagrams of non-Hermitian topological superconductors, predicting phases with higher winding numbers and multiple Majorana zero modes. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10418)]
*   **Signatures of Superconductivity in La5Ni3O11:** A theoretical analysis of the hybrid nickelate La5Ni3O11 shows a two-gap nature with interlayer and intralayer pairing. The lower Tc compared to La3Ni2O7 is attributed to a diminished interlayer coupling contribution. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18094)]
*   **Quantized Heat Flow in Moiré Chern Bands:** Thermal conductance measurements in a bilayer graphene moiré superlattice show quantized heat flow in units of the thermal conductance quantum, determined solely by the Chern number of the topological state, independent of its microscopic origin. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19205)]

### 2D Materials & Heterostructures

*   **Anomalous PL Enhancement in MoS2/Graphene:** An unexpected photoluminescence enhancement is observed in a MoS2/graphene heterostructure. This is attributed to a dominant trion-to-exciton conversion and interlayer charge transfer, enabled by a specific wet-transfer process. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18733)]
*   **Doping-Tunable Charge Density Waves:** Experimental and theoretical work on misfit layer compounds shows that chemical alloying in the rocksalt subunit can be used to tune the CDW order in the NbSe2 layers, stabilizing different patterns like 2x2 or 3x3. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19095)]
*   **Charge-Transfer Physics in T'-La2NiO4:** First-principles theory suggests that the hypothetical T' phase of La2NiO4 is a manifest charge-transfer insulator with a cuprate-like charge gap and a strong carrier asymmetry, offering new pathways for correlation physics in nickelates. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19217)]
*   **Unified Tight-Binding for Misfit Compounds:** A new tight-binding model describes the electronic structure of misfit layered compounds, revealing that the intervening layers mediate an interlayer spin-orbit coupling, which is crucial for the Ising protection of superconductivity. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19308)]
*   **Long-lived Carriers in MoC Nanosheets:** Combined experimental and theoretical work explains the anomalously long nanosecond carrier lifetimes in molybdenum carbide nanosheets, which are due to a large mass difference between Mo and C atoms that restricts phonon decay pathways. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.04327)]

### Computational Advances: Machine Learning & Simulations

*   **Universal Model for Wannier Hamiltonians:** G(Wa)NN is a new deep-learning model trained on over 111,000 Wannier Hamiltonians to predict the electronic Hamiltonian of solids in an orthogonal basis. It enables massive-scale transport simulations and comes with a new post-processing package. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18403)]
*   **Data-Efficient Training of MLIPs:** A study shows that using leverage-guided subset selection to choose training configurations can reduce the DFT labeling workload by a factor of 2-3x for training linear Atomic Cluster Expansion (ACE) potentials without sacrificing accuracy. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18524)]
*   **ATLAS: A Foundation Sampler for Amorphous Materials:** This neural sampler learns a diffusion process to generate Boltzmann-distributed amorphous structures directly from a target energy function, achieving high accuracy and efficiency in sampling and steering towards desired properties. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19198)]
*   **Free Energy from Alchemical Thermodynamic Integration:** A new code implements an alchemical thermodynamic integration scheme that couples *ab initio* force calculators during simulations, enabling first-principles free-energy calculations in solutions. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18750)]
*   **Universal Framework for Rigidity:** A new theory unifies the Lindemann criterion for melting, vitrification, and jamming of hard spheres across multiple dimensions, providing a parameter-free master equation that predicts phase transitions with high accuracy. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19185)]
*   **Accelerated Study of Catalysis:** Graph neural networks are used to predict transition metal adsorption energies on graphene quantum dots with an MAE of 0.101 eV, reducing computational cost by six orders of magnitude compared to DFT and enabling rapid screening. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18591)]

### Spintronics, Magnonics & Quantum Transport

*   **Pure Spin Bulk Photovoltaic Effect:** A theoretical study shows that in a PT-symmetric altermagnetic topological insulator heterostructure, the Néel vector orientation can control whether the bulk photovoltaic effect generates a pure spin photocurrent without an accompanying charge current. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19018)]
*   **Robust Helical Edge Channels:** Dual-gating in III-V quantum spin Hall insulators (InAs/GaInSb) is shown to suppress parasitic bulk conduction and stabilize robust, quantized helical edge transport over a wide electric field range. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19051)]
*   **Programmable Spin Conversion:** A theoretical proposal for programmable spin conversion in ultracold gases uses a gradient in the spin-dependent self-energy to convert a force-driven scalar anisotropy into a spin source. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19105)]
*   **Nonlinear Magnon Interactions:** Experiments observe pump-induced double anticrossings in a magnetic system. The gap sizes are strongly power-dependent, identifying the mechanism as nondegenerate three-magnon splitting that creates two independent coupling channels. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18807)]
*   **Complete Hierarchy of Odd-Parity Spin Splitting:** Group theory establishes a complete classification of odd-parity spin splitting in collinear magnets, revealing h- and k-wave splittings up to l=9, and predicting the conditions for an anomalous Hall response. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19303)]

### Quantum Information & Thermodynamics

*   **Information Compression at Criticality:** The dynamics of highly excited states at the ergodicity boundary can be simplified by truncating the Hamiltonian spectrum. The resulting spectrum has a fractal structure, showing that a vanishing fraction of eigenlevels suffices to reproduce the power-law survival probability. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18388)]
*   **Quantum-Enabled "Small" Antennas:** A review of a new generation of antennas whose operation is based on non-classical principles, allowing them to be miniaturized well below the radiated wavelength while maintaining high efficiency and even enabling beam steering with a single element. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18469)]
*   **Quantum Critical Fan in 2D Dirac Semimetals:** A non-perturbative field-theory approach maps out the finite-temperature phase diagram of a 2D Dirac semimetal near a quantum critical point, confirming emergent relativistic symmetry at zero temperature and Ising universality at the classical transition. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18396)]
*   **Universal Heat Transport in Moiré Systems:** Direct measurement of thermal conductance in a moiré superlattice confirms that heat flow is quantized and determined by the Chern number of the topological states, including interaction-driven symmetry-broken Chern insulators. [[Paper Title](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19205)]
