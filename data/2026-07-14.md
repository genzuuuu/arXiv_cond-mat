### Superconductivity
- **Pairing Symmetry & Mechanisms:** A study on pressurized La₃Ni₂O₇ shows that strong electronic correlations, treated via DMFT, reverse the leading pairing instability from a \(d_{xy}\)-wave to a correlation-stabilized \(s_{\pm}\) state, challenging weak-coupling predictions [Correlation-renormalized spin-fluctuation pairing and the stabilization of \(s_{\pm}\) superconductivity in pressurized La₃Ni₂O₇](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11786). Spin-fluctuation pairing is also analyzed in the context of iron-based superconductors, reviewing evidence for sign-changing \(s_{\pm}\) pairing and material-specific gap structures [Iron-Based Superconductors: A Decade of Materials, Magnetism, and Mechanisms](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11355). A new hopping mechanism for superconductivity is proposed from DFT calculations on MgB₂, linking cosine band asymmetry, Fermi surface nesting, and the superconducting gap [Hopping mechanism for superconductivity revealed by Density Functional Theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.21417).

- **Novel Superconducting Phases & Materials:** A geometrically frustrated breathing-pyrochlore lattice in Mo₄PtGa₁₇ is identified as a unique route to heavy-fermion-like superconductivity in \(d\)-electron materials, driven by flat bands and enhanced ferromagnetic spin fluctuations [A d-Electron Route to Heavy-Fermion-Like Superconductivity via Geometrical Frustration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09924). A superconducting dome is mapped in PLD-synthesized Nd₁₋ₓEuₓNiO₂ thin films, with optimal T_c ~31 K and observation of field-enhanced superconductivity [Superconducting dome and field-enhanced superconductivity of PLD synthesized Nd1-xEuxNiO2 thin films](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10332). Strain-tuned nodal superconductivity is discovered in the kagome metal CsV₃Sb₅ using NQR, revealing degenerate \(s_{\pm}\) and nodal gap states that split under uniaxial pressure [Strain-Tuned Nodal Superconductivity in the Charge-Ordered Kagome Metal CsV$_3$Sb$_5$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11115). A new theory for acoustic plasmon-mediated superconductivity in bilayers is developed via Eliashberg theory, tracing evolution from acoustic-plasmon to acoustic-phonon mediated pairing [Evolution from an acoustic-plasmon-mediated superconductivity to an acoustic-phonon-mediated superconductivity in bilayers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10835).

- **Josephson & Proximity Effects:** Magnetic-field-induced Andreev state quantum interference is shown to produce coherent supercurrent oscillations in superconducting nanojunctions [Landau-Zener tunneling and quantum interference of Andreev states](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09859). Moiré-engineered graphene Josephson junctions are demonstrated to sustain phase-coherent transport up to 6 T in the fractal Hofstadter regime, a new platform for high-field superconducting interferometry [High-field Josephson effect enabled by a moiré Hofstadter spectrum](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11721). Anomalous dissipation in current-biased Josephson junctions is explored, predicting enhanced quantum escape and re-trapping for applications in microwave photon detection [Anomalous Dissipation in Current Biased Josephson Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11647).

- **Fluctuations & Theory:** Superconducting fluctuations are shown to induce nonreciprocal dichroism and gyrotropy above T_c in noncentrosymmetric superconductors, linking to the superconducting diode effect [Effect of superconducting fluctuations on nonreciprocal dichroism and gyrotropy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10464). The mixed-state linewidth in the second superconducting dome of LaFeAsO₁₋ₓMₓ exhibits an anomalous field dependence, attributed to enhanced Pauli-paramagnetic effects [Anomalous field evolution of the mixed-state linewidth in the second superconducting dome of LaFeAsO$_{1-x}M_x$ ($M={\rm F,H}$)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10348). A high-pressure soft point-contact Andreev reflection spectroscopy technique is developed, providing evidence for unconventional superconductivity in CsCr₃Sb₅ and La₂PrNi₂O₇ [Soft point-contact Andreev reflection spectroscopy in a palm-type cubic anvil-pressure cell](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10668).

### Topological Phases & Quantum Geometry
- **Altermagnetism & Spin Splitting:** A comprehensive catalog of altermagnetic orders in all 2D and 3D magnetic space groups is provided, extending the classification to non-centrosymmetric crystals and revealing new nonsymmorphic patterns [Catalog of Altermagnetism in Magnetic Wallpaper/Space Groups and Nonsymmorphic Altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10857). A symmetry-driven electronic cascade of charge order is uncovered in the \(d\)-wave altermagnet Rb₁₋δV₂Te₂O using STM [Discovery of a symmetry-driven electronic cascade in a \(d\)-wave altermagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11567). A novel stripe-ordered altermagnetic phase is predicted to emerge from a correlation-driven spin-density-wave instability in a Hubbard model [Stripe-Ordered Altermagnetism Emerging from Correlation-Driven Spin-Density-Wave Instability](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11532). Ultrafast, reversible control of nonrelativistic spin splitting (NRSS) is demonstrated via THz nonlinear phononics in NiO and LaFeO₃ [Dynamical Nonrelativistic Spin Splitting via THz Nonlinear Phononics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10091).

- **Quantum Geometry & Flat Bands:** A Lifshitz-Kosevich theory is derived for anomalous Landau levels in topological flat bands, showing that thermal damping of quantum oscillations directly probes the quantum metric [Lifshitz-Kosevich Theory of Anomalous Landau Levels in Topological Flat Bands](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10328). Flat bands in a buckled silicon kagome lattice are shown to be strain-tunable, with the potential for realizing electronic correlation physics [Strain-controlled crystalline–amorphous transition and flat-band tuning in buckled silicon kagome](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11790). Superconductivity induced by quantum geometry near a fractional Chern insulator is predicted from a coupled-wire construction [Quantum-Geometry-Induced Superconductivity near a Fractional Chern Insulator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.21166).

- **Other Topological States:** The equivalence between the axion invariant and the \(S_4\) symmetry indicator is established for three-dimensional axion insulators [Equivalence between the Axion Invariant and the $S_4$ Symmetry Indicator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05719). A minimal model for the Weyl nodes and Fermi arcs of PtBi₂ is developed, guided by DFT and symmetry [A minimal model for the Weyl nodes and Fermi arcs of PtBi$_2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10937). The magnetic orbital Hall effect is experimentally observed in the altermagnet RuO₂, establishing altermagnets as intrinsic sources of orbital currents [Magnetic Orbital Hall Effect in Altermagnet RuO$_2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12593). A review discusses engineering topological phases in 2D materials via van der Waals interface design and external modulation [Engineering Topology by Design in Two-dimensional Materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10049).

### Correlated Systems & Magnetism
- **Quantum Magnetism:** NMR spectroscopy confirms that the magnetization plateaus in SrCu₂(BO₃)₂ are Wigner crystals of spin-2 bound states, a new paradigm for plateaus [Pinwheel-shaped bound triplet pairs in the magnetization plateaus of SrCu$_2$(BO$_3$)$_2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10877). A theoretical framework predicts scale-invariant flux jamming and bimodal kinetics in finite frustrated spin networks [Flux Jamming and Bimodal Dynamics in Bounded Spin Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10045). A classical Blume-Capel model on a diamond chain is shown to exhibit entropically-driven pseudo-transitions with sharp features [Still life in a classic Blume-Capel model: pseudo-transitions in a spin-1 diamond chain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11669).

- **Active & Driven Matter:** Spatiotemporal disk packing is used to direct the growth of complex geometries, demonstrated experimentally using frontal polymerization [Spatiotemporal Disk Packing for Directed Growth of Complex Geometries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09991). Nonreciprocal multi-body interactions are shown to activate a liquid state in acoustically levitated particle ensembles [Nonreciprocal multi-body interactions activate liquid state of acoustically levitated particle ensembles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09960). Active Brownian particle crystals are studied, revealing a breakdown of effective-temperature descriptions for vacancy hopping [Arrhenius-Type Description and Noise-Composition Dependence of Single-Vacancy Hopping in Active Brownian Crystals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10192).

- **Computational & Theoretical Methods:** A self-consistent phonon spectral function framework is introduced for thermal transport beyond the quasiparticle approximation, benchmarked on HgTe [Self-Consistent Phonon Spectral Functions and Thermal Transport Beyond the Quasiparticle Approximation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10211). A machine-learning interatomic potential benchmark reveals that amorphous materials are a central challenge for universal potentials [Amorphous materials as a frontier challenge for universal interatomic potentials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11384). A new method, the imposed wave method, is presented for extracting the dispersion of elastic waves in disordered solids [A method for measuring the dispersion of elastic waves in disordered computer-solids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11395). A generalized Keldysh formalism is developed for computing nonequilibrium two-particle correlation functions, demonstrated for fluctuation dynamics [Generalized Keldysh formalism for nonequilibrium correlation functions and its application to fluctuation dynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11055).

### 2D Materials & Heterostructures
- **Moiré & Twisted Systems:** A new mechanism for moiré EPC is revealed in twisted WSe₂ and MoTe₂ via interference selection rules for Raman-active breathing modes [Interference-Enhanced Large Electron-Phonon Coupling from Raman-active Breathing Modes in Moiré Semiconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11425). Bragg interferometry is reviewed as a technique for mapping atomic reconstruction in moiré superlattices [Bragg Interferometry of Moiré Superlattices: From Geometric Phase Principles to Atomic Reconstruction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09901). Correlated plasmonic excitation is demonstrated in reconfigurable twisted plasmonic superlattices assembled from hybrid nanorods [Correlated Plasmonic Excitation in Twisted Nematic Plasmonic Superlattices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10122).

- **Synthesis & Properties:** A 2D electride, monolayer Ca₂N, is studied via Landau-level spectroscopy, showing remarkably free-electron-like interstitial anionic electrons [Probing the Nature of Interstitial Anionic Electrons in 2D Electride Ca$_2$N via Landau-Level Spectroscopy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10024). Magnetism in moiré Mott-Wigner crystals is predicted to be induced by electronic phonons, without spin interactions [Electronic phonon-induced magnetism in moiré Mott-Wigner crystals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13172). A ferroelectric–superconducting interaction is observed in epitaxial YBa₂Cu₃O₇₋δ/BaTiO₃ heterostructures [Ferroelectric–Superconducting Interaction in Epitaxial YBa2Cu3O7-{\delta}/BaTiO3 Films](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11003).

### Spectroscopies & Characterizations
- **AI & Data-Driven Methods:** A machine-learning audit protocol combining SHAP attribution and DFT verification is applied to spin Hall conductivity predictions, revealing hidden training data errors [Auditing Machine-Learning Models and Their Training Data with Explainability and First-Principles Verification: Application to Spin Hall Conductivity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09910). A large dataset of 1,035 solid-state reactions, the Precursor Genome, is released for training predictive models of solid-state reactivity [The Precursor Genome: A Pairwise Reaction Dataset for Solid-State Synthesis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09903). A unified entropic framework, Geometrothermodynamics, is applied to real fluids, revealing universal geometric features of the critical regime [Geometric Universality and Thermodynamic Microstructure of Real Fluids in a Unified Entropic Framework](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10076).

- **Experimental Techniques:** A variable-angle polarizer method for accurate MOKE measurement is demonstrated on Co thin films [Measurement of Kerr rotation using a variable-angle polarizer method](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11002). A MOSAIC framework is introduced for connecting diffuse scattering to atomic-site-resolved displacement fields in structural models [Connecting Diffuse Scattering to Atomic-Site-Resolved Occupancy and Displacement Fields through Fourier Filtering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10440). An in-situ XRR cell for laboratory diffractometers enables temperature-controlled vapor dosing experiments [Enabling temperature controlled in-situ vapor dosing for lab source X-ray reflectivity measurements](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10339).
