### Superconductivity & Correlated Electron Systems

*   **New Chiral Superconductors:** Three new chiral-structured superconductors, RhGe₄, IrGe₄, and IrSi₄, were synthesized under high pressure. Their chiral structure was directly confirmed by atomic-resolution STEM, overcoming limitations of traditional diffraction methods. Calculations show mixed-parity pairing and symmetry-protected Weyl points near the Fermi level. [Chiral-Structured Superconductors TrX4 (Tr = Rh, Ir; X = Ge, Si)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05881)
*   **Superconductivity in Rhombohedral Graphene:** A new, switchable chiral superconducting state (SCH) was discovered in rhombohedral hexalayer graphene. This state is induced by an out-of-plane magnetic field and can coexist with other chiral superconducting states, forming a "switchable chiral-superconductor quartet" with potential for topological devices. [Multi-Knob Switchable Chiral Superconductivity Quartet in Rhombohedral Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06520)
*   **Magnon-Mediated Pairing:** A microscopic theory shows that in a 2D ferromagnet, a small easy-plane magnetic anisotropy generates an attractive interaction for equal-spin p-wave pairing mediated by magnons. This coupling can become strong enough to yield a sizable critical temperature, especially near the ferromagnetic transition. [Magnon-Mediated Superconductivity in a 2D Itinerant Ferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05754)
*   **Cavity-Enhanced FQHE Gaps:** Using a composite-fermion framework, researchers show that cavity-mediated attractive interactions can enhance the charged excitation gap of fractional quantum Hall states (ν=1/3 and 1/5). The gap enhancement scales strongly with electron number and the vacuum-field gradient. [Composite-Fermion Study of Cavity-Modified Fractional Quantum Hall Excitation Gaps](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06298)
*   **Correlation of Tc in Cuprates:** A study of the Emery model identifies two key mechanisms controlling the maximum superconducting critical temperature in cuprates: the size of the charge-transfer gap and the oxygen hole content. \(T_c^{\rm max}\) is optimized deep in the charge-transfer regime, offering new paths for material optimization. [Correlation of maximum superconducting critical temperature](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06460) & [Charge-transfer gap size and oxygen hole content](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06462)
*   **Theory of Phonon-Mediated p-wave Pairing:** A study of a single-flavor metal shows that pure phonon-mediated retardation, overlooked in static BCS approximations, can lead to p-wave superconductivity. Berry curvature can further stabilize a chiral p-wave state. [Superconductivity from phonon-mediated retardation in a single-flavor metal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.23790)

### Topological States, Magnetism & Spintronics

*   **Topological Phase Transitions in a Janus Monolayer:** An electric field can induce simultaneous topological phase transitions in both momentum-space (Chern number) and real-space (skyrmion formation) in Janus monolayer MnBi₂Se₂Te₂, as shown by first-principles calculations. This offers a platform for controlling coexisting topological orders. [Electric Field Induced Multi-Space Topological Phase Transitions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05631)
*   **Equivalence of Axion and Symmetry Indicators:** A proof establishes the equivalence between the axion invariant (θ/π) and the S₄ symmetry indicator for 3D S₄-symmetric axion insulators, linking topological field theory with band theory classification. [Equivalence between the Axion Invariant and the S₄ Symmetry Indicator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05719)
*   **Real-Space Topological Invariant for Non-Hermitian Chains:** A machine learning method (random forest regression) is used to predict the optimal crop-length parameter for calculating topological invariants in finite non-Hermitian chains. This provides a physically interpretable way to reliably capture the non-Hermitian skin effect topology. [Machine learning prediction of the convergence criterion for a topological invariant](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05900)
*   **Altermagnetism in a Checkerboard Lattice:** Using a minimal Hubbard model, researchers show that the checkerboard lattice is a natural platform for altermagnetism. The magnetic excitations exhibit a strong dependence on chirality and direction, a salient feature of this new class of magnetic materials. [Weak-coupling altermagnetism and chiral magnetic excitations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06106)
*   **Layer-Selective Chirality Switch:** Intercalating bilayer graphene with Janus monolayers (WSSe or MnSSe) is predicted to induce a layer-selective switch of the Rashba spin texture. This allows for gate-tunable, layer-selective spintronic devices. [Layer-selective chirality switch in bilayer graphene intercalated by Janus monolayers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06159)
*   **Piezoaxial Coupling for Ferroaxial Control:** A symmetry-based theory demonstrates that homogeneous strain can serve as a conjugate field for ferroaxial order. First-principles calculations on Na₂BaMg(PO₄)₂ confirm strain-selected ferroaxial domain control, suggesting a route to domain engineering via strain-field cooling. [Piezoaxial coupling for strain-selected ferroaxial domain control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06209)
*   **High-Temperature Ferromagnetism in Monolayers:** Epitaxial monolayers of Co-doped Fe₅GeTe₂ were successfully synthesized, demonstrating ferromagnetic ordering up to ~200 K. The mechanisms for Curie temperature enhancement via Co doping were identified using element-specific XMCD and DFT. [High temperature ferromagnetism in epitaxial monolayers of Co-doped Fe5GeTe2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05962)
*   **Strain-Selected Ferroaxial Domain Control:** A symmetry-based hierarchy of strain-derived conjugate fields for ferroaxial order is formulated and demonstrated using first-principles calculations on Na₂BaMg(PO₄)₂. This provides a route to controlling ferroaxial domains via strain-field cooling. [Piezoaxial coupling for strain-selected ferroaxial domain control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06209)

### Quantum Fluids, Gases & Lattices

*   **Dirac Spin Liquid Candidate:** A quantum simulator using 114 dipolar Rydberg atoms on a kagome array provides evidence for a gapless U(1) Dirac spin liquid. The observed correlations in the prepared liquid state agree well with a simple ansatz for the Dirac spin liquid. [Dirac Spin Liquid Candidate in a Rydberg Quantum Simulator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.14323)
*   **Self-Bound Droplets of Polar Molecules:** Ground-state path integral Monte Carlo simulations study a Bose-Einstein condensate of strongly interacting NaCs polar molecules with double microwave shielding. Self-bound droplets are observed, which are estimated to form a superfluid array. [Self-Bound Droplets of Ultracold Dipolar Molecules](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06130)
*   **Chiral Graviton Modes in Lattice FQH States:** For the first time, a long-lived chiral graviton mode is demonstrated in a lattice non-Abelian fractional quantum Hall state (Moore-Read state) using exact diagonalization and matrix-product-state simulations. This signal could be observed in cold-atom experiments. [Chiral Graviton Modes in Non-Abelian lattice Fractional Quantum Hall states](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06267)
*   **Universal Self-Similar Evolution of 2D BECs:** The nonstationary evolution of 2D Bose-Einstein condensates is studied via the Gross-Pitaevskii equation. Universal self-similar solutions are identified, with dynamics governed by a dimensionless constant β. [Universal self-similar evolution of two-dimensional Bose-Einstein condensates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06062)

### 2D Materials, Heterostructures & Moiré Systems

*   **Interfacial Effects on Correlated States:** In twisted double bilayer graphene on CrOCl, an enhanced correlated insulating state is observed, driven by a charge-transfer process rather than a magnetic proximity effect. This highlights a new route for manipulating correlated states via interfacial engineering. [Correlated Insulating States in Twisted Double Bilayer Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06492)
*   **Twist-Tunable Flat Bands in Oxide Bilayers:** A deep-learning Hamiltonian combined with DFT is used to study twisted bilayer SrTiO₃. The model reveals twist-tunable flat bands, enhanced second-harmonic generation, and a clear twist-angle-dependent shift current, showcasing twist engineering in complex oxides. [Deep-learning Hamiltonian reveals twist-tunable flat bands](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06053)
*   **Half State in Twisted Double Bilayer Graphene:** A fractional quantum Hall state at total filling ν=-1/2 in decoupled twisted double bilayer graphene is observed, consistent with a two-component Halperin (Ψ331) state. A displacement field can induce a transition to a one-component non-Abelian state. [Half state at νtot = -1/2 and its transition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06547)
*   **Gap Structure of Twisted Bilayer Cuprates:** A microscopic tight-binding model predicts the gap structure of twisted bilayer cuprates as a function of twist angle, interlayer tunneling, and doping. The time-reversal symmetry-breaking d+id’ state is linked to the position of the Van Hove singularity. [Gap structure and phase diagram of twisted bilayer cuprates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10865)
*   **Effect of Defects on Band Alignment:** DFT calculations investigate how interfacial vacancy defects in TiO₂/MAPI/Cu₂O perovskite solar cells alter band alignment and device performance, with p-type doping of TiO₂ having the most significant impact. [Impact of interface defects on the band alignment and performance](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.19594)

### Quantum Information & Computation

*   **Classical Reversible Logic with Coherent Spins:** A proposal for classical reversible computation uses coherent spin dynamics in a Ge/Si quantum dot array. A simulated iToffoli gate operates below the Landauer limit, suggesting dual-use hardware for quantum algorithms and classical reversible logic. [Classical Reversible Computation by Quantum Coherence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06219)
*   **Fault-Tolerant Logical Measurement:** A new low-overhead method for fault-tolerant logical measurement is introduced. It treats the logical operator as a symmetry and "gauges" it, achieving qubit overhead linear in the operator's weight up to a polylogarithmic factor. [Low-overhead fault-tolerant quantum computation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.02213)
*   **Ge/SiGe Spin Qubits with Global Gate:** A simplified fabrication process for Ge/SiGe spin qubits, using a global accumulation gate and single-layer fine gates, is demonstrated. RF single-shot readout and coherent control are achieved with performance similar to more complex devices. [Radio frequency readout and control of Ge/SiGe hole spin qubits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06342)

### Spectroscopy, Transport & Optical Phenomena

*   **Phonon Light Emission:** An experiment demonstrates the spontaneous emission of light by non-equilibrium phonons in boron nitride under femtosecond excitation. This mid-infrared photoluminescence is a new, non-thermal radiative relaxation pathway for phonons. [Spontaneous emission of light by non-equilibrium phonons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06399)
*   **Floquet Polaritons:** A practical framework describes how pump light alters polaritons in materials, creating "Floquet polaritons." The method is applied to graphene, hBN, and layered superconductors, predicting flat bands and exceptional points in pumped systems. [Floquet polaritons in optically driven materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05857)
*   **Tomographic Transport in a Corbino Disk:** A study of "tomographic" electron flow in a Corbino disk geometry reveals an extended boundary layer and parametric enhancement of magnetoresistance, offering a new regime for understanding electron viscosity. [Magnetotransport of tomographic electrons in a Corbino disk](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05540)
*   **Separation of Linewidth Broadening:** Two-dimensional coherent spectroscopy (2DCS) is shown to be superior to 1D Voigt fits for separating homogeneous and inhomogeneous contributions to spectral linewidths in MoSe₂, demonstrated by a joint uncertainty-weighted fit. [Separation of Homogeneous and Inhomogeneous Broadening](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05564)
*   **Terahertz-Driven Four-Wave Mixing in Glasses:** A new nonlinear spectroscopy technique probes low-frequency vibrational modes in glasses. Applied to PbO-silicate glasses, it reveals a non-monotonic signal linked to medium-range structural reorganization of Pb²⁺ lone pairs. [Terahertz-driven four-wave mixing at glass surfaces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.06417)

### Computational & Theoretical Methods

*   **Efficient BSE Calculations:** An efficient implementation of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) is presented, using numerical atomic orbitals and a dual-k-mesh strategy. This method leverages the short-ranged nature of the screened Coulomb interaction for efficient interpolation. [Efficient Bethe-Salpeter Equation Calculations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05853)
*   **Machine-Learning Force Field for Charged Systems:** A new extension, EquiFiLM, allows any equivariant machine-learning force field to be conditioned on continuous external inputs like charge, requiring only minimal training data for accurate MD simulations of charged systems. [EquiFiLM: Charge-Conditioned Equivariant Force Fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05559)
*   **Unsupervised Phase Identification:** An autoencoder-based unsupervised machine-learning framework, using Fourier-space preprocessing, successfully identifies nonequilibrium phases in sheared binary colloids without prior knowledge of the phases. [Autoencoder-Based Unsupervised Identification of Nonequilibrium Phases](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05860)
*   **Onsager-Variational Diffuse-Domain Methods:** A formulation of diffuse-domain methods using Onsager's variational principle provides a unified route to thermodynamically consistent models for fluid-structure interactions at microscale, including deformable vesicles and active shells. [Onsager-variational formulation of diffuse-domain methods](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13196)
*   **Model Density Approach to Ewald Summations:** A new Ewald-type technique introduces a model charge density to cancel multipole moments of the crystalline charge distribution, accelerating convergence of electrostatic sums in bulk systems. [Model density approach to Ewald summations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.21776)
