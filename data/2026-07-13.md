### Superconductivity and Topological States

*   **[Energetics of fractional anomalous Hall crystals in rhombohedral graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08822):** Establishes fractional anomalous Hall crystals (FAHCs) as energetically competitive states in pentalayer graphene, using Monte Carlo calculations. The stability follows a principle where the crystal maps to a parent quantum Hall liquid, and kinetic energy favors periods matching the material's dispersion. This supports a "continuum-and-interactions-first" route to fractional quantum anomalous Hall states.
*   **[Quantum Oscillation Signatures of Z2 Monopole Charge in Nodal-Ring Semimetals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08825):** Shows magnetic quantum oscillations can directly diagnose the hidden Z2 monopole charge in nodal-ring semimetals. The phase shift of quantum oscillations is selected by the magnetic-field direction, providing a probe for this secondary topological charge.

The transition depends strongly on growth orientation, and a strong violation of the Pauli paramagnetic limit is observed with in-plane fields, attributed to strong spin-orbit scattering.

The near degeneracy of two pairing symmetries is explained by large intralayer spin-orbit coupling, and the field-induced first-order transition is interpreted as a transition between coexistence phases.

### Strongly Correlated Systems and Magnetism

*   **[Magnetic devil's staircase in UAgBi2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09003):** Reports the observation of a magnetic devil's staircase in the 5f-electron system UAgBi2, with at least seven nearly degenerate magnetic states. The multi-step magnetization is explained by square-wave structures and is consistent with the axial next-nearest neighbor Ising (ANNNI) model.
*   **[Magnetic Field Control of the Néel Vector and Magnon Visibility in Altermagnetic MnTe](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08829):** Investigates spin-wave spectrum of altermagnetic α-MnTe. An in-plane magnetic field reorients the Néel vector but leaves magnon energies unchanged, while strongly modifying spectral intensity. This demonstrates a separation between soft orientational order and robust magnon modes, enabling reconfigurable magnon coupling.
*   **[Short-range magnetic order and multi-stage phase transitions in the easy-plane van der Waals magnet CrCl3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09421):** Combines FMR and SQUID magnetometry with DFT+DMFT to study CrCl3. The work reveals robust short-range magnetic order at room temperature, a temperature-driven crossover of interlayer exchange from ferromagnetic to antiferromagnetic, and multi-stage phase transitions.
*   **[Symmetry-Protected Pinch Curves in Classical Spin Liquids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09470):** Introduces "pinch-curve spin liquids," where pinch singularities in structure factors form one-dimensional algebraic curves in momentum space. Inversion symmetry protects these curves, with geometry that is algebraically programmable, as demonstrated in lattice models and Monte Carlo simulations.
*   **[A new perspective on the anomalous Hall effect](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09019):** Revisits the anomalous Hall effect using a microscopic formalism based on polarization, magnetization, and free charges/currents. The conductivity tensor is decomposed into Kubo, Drude, and anomalous Hall terms, with numerical calculations presented for iron.
*   **[Unlocking ultrafast spin dynamics in a rare-earth magnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09413):** Demonstrates that selective optical excitation of localized 4f states in gadolinium iron garnet can induce an ultrafast demagnetization of the Gd sublattice (38 fs), over two orders of magnitude faster than in elemental gadolinium. This shows the magnetic response is governed by the optical excitation pathway.

### Quantum Dots, Qubits, and Quantum Computing

*   **[Majorana parity qubit in coupled minimal Kitaev chains](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09511):** Demonstrates the first coherent control of a Majorana parity qubit, realized by coupling two-site Kitaev chains in quantum dot-superconductor arrays. Coherent parity oscillations are observed, establishing a key milestone for fault-tolerant quantum computing.
*   **[Silicon-Germanium Heterostructures with Enhanced Valley Splitting for Spin Qubits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09652):** Presents a device-level optimization strategy for Si/SiGe spin qubits, predicting valley splittings of 1-5 meV. The design uses a narrow quantum well with a Ge spike and pure-Ge cap, which promises to effectively eliminate valley splitting as a major obstacle for scalability.
*   **[Coherent dynamics of individual excitons in a quantum dot embedded in a nanopost](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09419):** Measures the coherent ultrafast dynamics of exciton complexes in a single InAs quantum dot within a GaAs nanopost using four-wave mixing microscopy. The results show the nanopost is an excellent structure for probing coherent couplings in solid-state quantum systems.
*   **[Confinement drives valley splitting above 4K in buried silicon quantum wells](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09570):** Measures the energy scales of spin qubits in silicon quantum wells, finding that strong electrostatic confinement can enhance the valley splitting. This demonstrates a path for using strong confinement to improve qubit performance in low-disorder heterostructures.
*   **[Complete measurement of tunnel- and valley-coupling parameters in a silicon double quantum dot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09638):** Presents a full characterization of intravalley and intervalley tunnel couplings, including their complex phases, in a silicon double quantum dot. The valley phases are shown to control measurable parameters and depend on the underlying atomic structure.
*   **[Superconducting singlet-triplet qubits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09508):** Proposes "superconducting singlet-triplet (SST) qubits" based on double quantum dots in Josephson junctions. These qubits do not require spin-orbit interaction and are predicted to support all-to-all connectivity with minimal control lines.
*   **[Action-Factored Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Scalable Quantum Device Tuning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09422):** Introduces a multi-agent reinforcement learning framework (QADAPT) for tuning quantum-dot arrays. It uses a learned factorization of the action space to decouple agents and achieve zero-shot generalization to unseen device sizes, enabling rapid calibration.

### Nonequilibrium and Driven Systems

*   **[Observation of a Rydberg-atom time crystal with an ultralong lifetime](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09247):** Reports an ultralong-lived continuous time crystal in a Rydberg-atom system, with an oscillatory lifetime exceeding 16.95 hours. This is achieved by closing the Liouvillian gap through engineered dissipation, far surpassing previous realizations.
*   **[Superheavy dark-bright soliton as a signature of spatial symmetry breaking transition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09064):** Reveals a spontaneous spatial symmetry breaking in a trapped two-component Bose-Einstein condensate. At the transition point, the effective mass of a dark-bright soliton diverges, leading to a "superheavy soliton" phenomenon.
*   **[Terahertz field-driven nonlinear Hall effect and other second order transport phenomena in two-dimensional tellurene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09256):** Studies the nonlinear Hall effect and other second-order transport in 2D tellurene driven by THz fields. The current arises from skew scattering, side jump, and Berry curvature dipole mechanisms.
*   **[Finite-time cooling and accessibility of the stripe phase in the Ising antiferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09411):** Studies the finite-rate cooling dynamics of the J1-J2 Ising antiferromagnet. The stripe-ordered phase is not automatically reached on finite time scales due to a coarsening-controlled process, separating energetic degeneracy lifting from global stripe selection.
*   **[Thermalization in a Height-Conserving Quantum Dimer Model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09580):** Analyzes thermalization in a height-conserving quantum dimer model. It finds that spectral statistics and eigenstate thermalization do not follow the same universal signatures under strong kinematic constraints, a manifestation of constrained quantum chaos.
*   **[Generic behavior of ultrastability and anisotropic molecular packing in co-deposited organic semiconductor glass mixtures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08889):** Demonstrates that co-deposited organic semiconductor glass mixtures exhibit ultrastable behavior and that their birefringence can be predicted from the pure components.

### Theoretical and Computational Methods

*   **[Stochastic Similarity Renormalization Group](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08830):** Develops a stochastic similarity renormalization group (SRGQMC) by integrating quantum Monte Carlo. This enables in-medium SRG calculations at the previously unfeasible four-body level [IMSRG](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08826), approaching the full configuration-interaction limit.
*   **[A strong-coupling theory for polarizable symmetrically charged walls with counterions only](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08851):** Proposes a Wigner-type strong-coupling theory for counterions between charged dielectric walls. The theory, based on harmonic deviations from a Wigner crystal, shows excellent agreement with Monte Carlo simulations.
*   **[Hybrid DiffractGPT-Rietveld Refinement Framework for Automated X-ray Diffraction Analysis](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08890):** Presents AGAPI-XRD, a hybrid framework integrating DiffractGPT for structure prediction, database matching, and automated Rietveld refinement for end-to-end crystal structure determination from powder XRD data.
*   **[Predictive Renormalization-Group Theory of Universality Classes in Nonlinear Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09079):** Develops an RG framework that identifies two complementary mechanisms for universality in nonlinear systems (like finite-time singularities and nonlinear diffusion). The theory is predictive, identifying entire families of equations sharing the same asymptotic self-similar solution.
*   **[Quantum Hopfion rings in the cluster mean-field approximation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09140):** Studies quantum properties of hopfion rings using a cluster mean-field approximation combined with a symmetrization procedure. It reveals quantum fluctuations that limit the classical micromagnetic model.
*   **[Effective theories for many-body systems with nonuniform symmetries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09427):** Develops a unified framework for many-body systems with nonuniform symmetries. Their effects are understood as kinematic constraints that modify dispersion relations and lead to unconventional transport, such as soft sound modes in dipole-conserving systems.
*   **[Level statistics in the fractal phase of generalized Rosenzweig–Porter models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09444):** Computes level statistics for generalized Rosenzweig-Porter models. Using free probability theory and the replica method, it shows the full counting statistics takes a universal scaling form at the Thouless energy.
*   **[A multi-ensemble mean-field reduction method for networks of globally coupled phase oscillators with arbitrary parameter distributions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09516):** Presents a method to apply the Ott-Antonsen reduction to arbitrary frequency distributions by using a data-driven multi-ensemble approach, enabling stability and bifurcation analyses for real-world systems.
*   **[Electrical manipulation of polar order in electron crystal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09425):** Demonstrates electronic manipulation of charge-ordering directions in LuFe2O4. Current pulses modulate non-reciprocal resistivity, enabling a non-reciprocal resistance memory operation.

### Quantum Fluids and Optics

*   **[The monopole plasma resonance: a smoking gun of 3D U(1) quantum spin liquids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08826):** Proposes that emergent monopoles in 3D U(1) spin liquids carry a physical electric charge and form a plasma. This plasma should display a sharp resonance at very low frequencies, serving as a clear experimental fingerprint in materials like Ce2Zr2O7.
*   **[Tunable responsivity and bandwidth in microwave kinetic inductance detectors via readout current nonlinearity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09178):** Reports a factor of 10 enhancement in the optical response of MKIDs by operating close to the bifurcation point, where the kinetic inductance nonlinearly shifts the resonant frequency. This provides a tunable operational mode for improved sensitivity.
*   **[Molecular Tuning of Charge-Transfer Resonance in Plasmonic Nanocavities](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08927):** Investigates the energetic conditions for charge-transfer resonance in plasmonic nanocavities using systematically engineered molecular monolayers. The charge-transfer enhancement of Raman scattering is governed by a well-defined interfacial resonance condition.
*   **[Formation Dynamics of Quantum Droplets for Homonuclear and Heteronuclear Mixtures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.18541):** Numerically analyzes the formation of quantum droplets in bosonic mixtures. Most droplets form but few achieve equilibrium, with many exhibiting a breathing mode. Heteronuclear droplets achieve deeper binding but larger oscillations.

### Materials and Nanostructures

*   **[Grain-boundary-mediated kinetic arrest in graphite-to-diamond transformation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08868):** Uses large-scale MD simulations to show that grain boundaries in graphite decouple diamond nucleation from transformation propagation. This leads to kinetically arrested mixed sp2-sp3 states, identifying precursor microstructure as a key control parameter.
*   **[High density two-component glasses of organic semiconductors prepared by physical vapor deposition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08881):** Show that co-deposited organic semiconductor glasses (NPD and TPD) form high-density, ultrastable glasses. The transformation of these glasses propagates from the free surface, consistent with a surface equilibration mechanism.
*   **[Ferroelastic instability in rutile TiO2 and thermodynamic suppression of the CaCl2-type phase](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.08860):** Shows rutile TiO2 undergoes a ferroelastic instability at 13.5 GPa, but the resulting CaCl2-type phase is only weakly stable and is thermodynamically suppressed by competing phases, explaining its elusive experimental observation.
*   **[Intertwined chirality and symmetry breaking in moiré domain-wall networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.23570):** Shows that domain walls in moiré graphene bilayers undergo spontaneous symmetry breaking to form chiral configurations. This chirality selects between a topological crossing point-centered regime and a wall-centered regime for low-energy states.
*   **[Microscopic screening theory for excitons in two-dimensional materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.10966):** Presents a computational approach for exciton calculations in 2D materials using a BSE framework with atomistic description and quantum screened interactions. This offers an alternative pathway for accurate dielectric screening and exciton computations.
*   **[Dispersion Polymerization in an Elastomeric Solvent](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09387):** Establishes a new solid-state analogue of dispersion polymerization within a deformable elastomeric network. This "DiPolES" approach enables fabrication of uniform microparticles with tunable size and shape.
