### Superconductivity

*   **Light-Programmed Superconductivity (LiPS):** A new light-created and fully controlled superconducting state is reported at an Al-Si heterojunction. The critical temperature (1.8-8.5K) can be tuned with femtosecond laser pulses, attributed to control of a Moiré-like misfit dislocation superlattice. The state shows features of a 2D Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition. [Phase coherence control of a programmable high-Tc superconductor created by light](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14567)
*   **Spin Fluctuation-Mediated Pairing in ThFeAsN:** First-principles calculations show that the high-Tc superconductor ThFeAsN is a spin fluctuation-mediated multiband superconductor with a calculated Tc of 22.4 K and a dxy-wave order parameter. [Spin fluctuation-mediated unconventional superconductivity in ThFeAsN from first-principles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14677)
*   **Driven-Dissipative Superconductivity:** A new protocol is proposed to prepare a superconducting state as a stationary state in a moiré heterostructure without requiring attractive interactions, instead using optically induced operations and local dissipation. [Driven-dissipative superconductivity in moiré heterostructure without attraction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15169)
*   **Superconductivity in Rhombohedral Graphene:** The highly insulating state on the low-density side of chiral superconductivity in pentalayer graphene is identified as a Wigner crystal phase. A hole-doped metallic Wigner crystal and competing magnetic-field-stabilized superconducting states are also reported. [Competing Orders Driven by Wigner Crystal Phase in Rhombohedral Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15014)
*   **Altermagnets and Superconductors:** It is shown that altermagnetic domain walls coupled to s-wave superconductors can host spin-triplet superconductivity. A reciprocal supercurrent-induced torque is also predicted. [Altermagnetic spin textures coupled to superconductors: Domain wall spin-triplet superconductivity and supercurrent-induced torques](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15249)

### Altermagnetism & Topological Matter

*   **Switchable Anomalous Hall Effects in Altermagnetic Bilayer:** The interlayer sliding direction in altermagnetic bilayer Fe₂WS₄ is shown to act as a symmetry selector. Diagonal sliding enables a switchable anomalous Hall effect, while axial sliding enables a switchable anomalous valley Hall effect. [Interlayer sliding direction as a symmetry selector in altermagnetic bilayer Fe2WS4: Switchable anomalous Hall and anomalous valley Hall effects](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14459)
*   **Fractional Quantum Ferroelectric Control in Altermagnetic Monolayer:** In monolayer Cr₂S₂, fractional quantum ferroelectricity is coupled to altermagnetism, enabling non-volatile control of spin-valley locking and valley Hall effects. [Fractional quantum ferroelectric control of spin-valley locking and valley Hall effects in altermagnetic monolayer Cr2S2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14465)
*   **Emergent d-wave Altermagnetism in FeSe:** A robust altermagnetic ground state with giant spin splitting (up to 620 meV) is engineered in monolayer FeSe by single-side Cl adsorption and hole doping. [Emergent d-wave altermagnetism in chlorine-adsorbed FeSe monolayer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15197)
*   **Monopole Spin Density Waves in Weyl Semimetals:** A new class of monopole spin density wave states is proposed for magnetic Weyl semimetals, where the order parameter inherits a nontrivial Berry phase and exhibits topologically protected nodal structures. [Monopole Spin Density Wave States in Magnetic Weyl Semimetals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14829)
*   **Ridges in 2D Altermagnets:** A new concept of "ridge-spin-layer coupling" is introduced in 2D altermagnets, where a 1D line of dispersionless states (ridge) in momentum space is locked to spin and layer, enabling "ridgetronics" for direction-discriminating currents. [Ridge-Spin-Layer Coupling and Emergent Ridgetronics in 2D Altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15009)
*   **Symmetry-Driven Electronic Cascade in a d-wave Altermagnet:** A cascade of charge orders is uncovered in a d-wave altermagnet, Rb₁₋δV₂Te₂O, where a primary density wave leads to a nematic component and an off-axis modulation, consistent with successive symmetry lowering. [Discovery of a symmetry-driven electronic cascade in a $d$-wave altermagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.11567)
*   **Quantized Photocurrents in Gapless Topological Matter:** A quantized circular photogalvanic effect is observed in the chiral topological semimetal Rh₀.₉₅Ni₀.₀₅Si, driven by optical transitions at a multifold node, providing an all-optical analogue of the quantum Hall effect. [Quantized Photocurrents in Gapless Topological Matter](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.12420)
*   **Anomalous Hall Conductivity from the Pseudogap:** Using a general scaling relation, it is predicted that cuprate superconductors in the pseudogap phase should exhibit a small but measurable anomalous Hall effect. [Derivation and application of a general scaling relation between the dc and asymptotic high-frequency optical Hall responses](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15043)

### Quantum Magnets & Spin Dynamics

*   **Ligand-Driven Kitaev Interactions in NiI₂:** Inelastic neutron scattering on the spin-1 triangular antiferromagnet NiI₂ reveals substantial Kitaev and off-diagonal Γ interactions, driven by strong spin-orbit coupling on the ligand ions, providing a new pathway for Kitaev materials. [Sizable Ligand-Mediated Bond-Dependent Interactions in a Spin-1 Triangular Antiferromagnet NiI$_2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14893)
*   **Monopole Phase in a Triangular Lattice:** A condensate of monopoles (gapless gauge excitations) is stabilized in the J₁-J₂ Heisenberg model on a triangular lattice under a small Zeeman field, confirmed by field-theory and variational Monte Carlo approaches. [Emergence of a monopole phase in the $J_1{-}J_2$ Heisenberg model on the triangular lattice for small magnetic fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14766)
*   **Theory of Magnetic Order in Bilayer Nickelates:** A new theory describes the magnetic order in La₃Ni₂O₇ in terms of effective local moments with RKKY and superexchange interactions, yielding a wavevector close to (π/2, π/2) consistent with experiments. [Magnetic Order in bilayer Ruddlesden-Popper Nickelates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15228)
*   **Long-lived Memory in Sliding Spin Chains:** A system of two sliding ferromagnetic Ising chains exhibits "magnetic friction" that dramatically slows thermalization, leading to memory retention times that increase from exponential to stretched exponential in system parameters. [Long-lived memory in sliding spin chains](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14383)
*   **Tunable Magneto-Excitonic Coupling:** Chlorine incorporation in the van der Waals antiferromagnet CrSBr is shown to tune excitonic character and magneto-excitonic coupling, systematically reducing magnetic-field-induced energy renormalization. [Tunable Magneto-Excitonic Coupling in Alloyed van der Waals Antiferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14723)
*   **Even-Denominator FQHS in Trilayer Graphene:** Robust even-denominator fractional quantum Hall states (ν=7/2, 9/2, 5/2) are observed for the first time in the zeroth Landau level of ABA trilayer graphene, attributed to Landau-level mixing. [Even-denominator fractional quantum Hall states in the zeroth Landau level of ABA trilayer graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.06245)

### Low-Dimensional & 2D Materials

*   **Anisotropic Exciton Dispersion in CrSBr:** Momentum-resolved electron energy-loss spectroscopy directly observes a strongly anisotropic and linear exciton dispersion in multilayer CrSBr, attributed to long-range electron-hole exchange. [Direct observation of anisotropic exciton dispersion in the 2D semiconductor CrSBr](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14712)
*   **Two-Particle Density Matrix of a Luttinger Liquid:** A closed, finite-size analytic expression for the equal-time two-particle reduced density matrix of a Luttinger liquid is derived, revealing a new exponent controlling off-diagonal structure beyond the Luttinger parameter. [The two-particle density matrix of a Luttinger liquid](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14402)
*   **Gyrotropy from Extrinsic Geometry in Twisted Materials:** It is shown that gyrotropy in twisted bilayer systems can arise purely from the physical geometry of the system independent of electronic states, and this must be separated from contributions due to electronic coherence. [Gyrotropy from Extrinsic Geometry in Twisted Materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15189)

### Computational Methods & Machine Learning

*   **AutoHF Solver:** A new general-purpose Hartree-Fock solver, `autohf`, is presented that uses automatic differentiation to bypass manual derivation of mean-field equations. [AutoHF: a general Hartree-Fock solver utilizing direct energy minimization with automatic differentiation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14263)
*   **SevenNet-Polar for Born Effective Charges:** An equivariant graph neural network, SevenNet-Polar, is developed for fast and accurate prediction of Born effective charge tensors, enabling large-scale, charge-aware molecular dynamics. [SevenNet-Polar for MultiTask Prediction of Energy, Forces, Stress, and Born Effective Charges](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14827)
*   **Learning the Fermion Sign Problem:** A probabilistic numerics approach reframes the fermion sign problem in path-integral Monte Carlo as a statistical inference problem, using models to learn the behavior of fermion exchange cycles and extract energies in regimes with severe sign problems. [Learning the Fermion sign structure in path-integral Monte Carlo](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15060)
*   **Fast Tensor-Network Contraction:** Subspace-iteration CTMRG (SI-CTMRG) is introduced, a QR-based projector construction that replaces large-matrix SVDs in tensor network contraction, achieving up to two orders of magnitude speedup and enabling state-of-the-art iPEPS results on a single GPU. [Fast two-dimensional tensor-network contraction via subspace iteration](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15158)
*   **Implicit Differentiation for PEPS:** Implicit differentiation techniques are applied to the optimization of projected entangled-pair states (PEPS), reducing computational cost and improving stability by bypassing derivatives of subroutines. [Implicit differentiation of tensor network algorithms](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15030)
*   **Active Learning for ML Force Fields:** A computationally cheap uncertainty estimator based on last-layer-projection regression is shown to efficiently select training sets for machine-learning force fields, recovering full-data accuracy with far fewer labels. [Full-data accuracy with fewer labels for training and fine-tuning machine-learning force fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14486)

### Topological Phases & Quantum Information

*   **Spinless Charged Excitation at Topological Interface:** Density-matrix renormalization group calculations reveal a spinless charged excitation bound at the interface between an integer quantum Hall state and a chiral spin liquid in the Hofstadter-Hubbard model. [Spinless charged excitation at the interface between a conventional topological insulator and a topological Mott insulator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14221)
*   **Single-Component Twisted ℤ₃ Orthogonal Metal:** An exotic metallic phase featuring a Fermi surface of charge-e/3 fermions coupled to a twisted gauge field is proposed as a parent state for anyon superconductivity with arbitrary chirality. [Single-component twisted $\mathbb{Z}_3$ orthogonal metal in an $e/3$-anyon fluid](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15274)
*   **Nonplanar Qubit with Tunable Gauge Symmetry:** A non-planar 3x3 crossbar Josephson array qubit is demonstrated, exhibiting flux-tunable ℤ₃ combinatorial gauge symmetry and providing a platform for exploring lattice gauge theories. [Nonplanar qubit with tunable gauge symmetry](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14229)
*   **Circuit Complexity of Quantum Spin Glasses:** A lower bound is proven on the circuit depth required to prepare near-ground states of quantum p-spin glasses, ruling out shallow-circuit preparation and providing an obstruction related to the "No Low-Energy Trivial States" problem. [Circuit complexity lower bounds for quantum spin glasses](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14384)

### Dynamics, Glasses & Active Matter

*   **Gravity-Induced Thermal Rectification:** It is demonstrated theoretically that gravity alone can induce perfect thermal rectification in gaseous systems, controlled by gravitational parameters. [Gravity-Induced Thermal Rectification in Gaseous Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14136)
*   **Memory in Self-Similar Diffusion:** A renormalization-group framework shows that memory of initial conditions can remain asymptotically relevant in self-similar diffusion processes, leading to distinct memory-retaining fixed points. [Memory of Initial Conditions in Self-Similar Diffusion: A Renormalization-Group Perspective](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14388)
*   **Active Vesicle Amoeboid Swimming:** Weakly deflated vesicles driven by activity exhibit various dynamical regimes, including synchronized oscillations and transitions between non-propelling and propelling states, leading to amoeboid swimming. [Amoeboid swimming of active vesicles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14714)
*   **Finite Entanglement Scaling Origin:** The nature of perturbations induced by matrix product state approximations of critical systems is resolved, demonstrating they differ from predictions of conformal field theory. [On the origin of finite entanglement scaling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15124)
*   **Structure Selection by Non-Conservative Forces:** Experiments and simulations show that non-conservative three-body acoustic interactions can select specific static structures in a driven-dissipative system. [Structure Selection by Non-Conservative 3-Body Acoustic Interactions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.14378)
