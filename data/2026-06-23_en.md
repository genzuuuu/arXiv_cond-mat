- **[Displacement-Field-Driven Superconductivity in MATTG (6)]**: A slave-particle theory for magic-angle twisted trilayer graphene shows that a displacement field drives a semimetal-to-superconductor transition, unifying electric-field-tunable superconductivity, Mottness, and heavy-fermion behavior.

- **[Superconductivity in Doped Spin Multimer Systems (300)]**: Density-matrix renormalization group studies of the double Kondo lattice model show that hole-doped spin multimers exhibit robust pairing correlations with universal behavior, offering new guidelines for superconductor research.
- **[Bogoliubov Quasiparticles are Integer-Charged (248)]**: A rigorous proof under a number-conserving Hamiltonian shows Bogoliubov quasiparticles in superconductors possess integer charge, calling into question the feasibility of Majorana zero-mode-based braiding for quantum computation.
- **[Parabolic Law Reaffirmed for IQH Multifractality (56)]**: Resolves a decades-old debate by showing that deviations from a parabolic multifractality spectrum at the integer quantum Hall transition are due to the non-Abelian nature of the underlying field theory, restoring the predicted parabolic form.
- **[Pairing-Induced Bloch Oscillations in Kitaev Chain (101)]**: Strong nearest-neighbor interaction in a Kitaev chain is shown to suppress hopping and enhance pairing, leading to a Wannier-Stark ladder and Bloch oscillations without an external field.
- **[Kohn-Luttinger-like Superconductivity in TBG (143)]**: Predicts that twisted bilayer graphene at large twist angles with small supercells can exhibit d-wave superconductivity mediated by screened Coulomb interactions, with transition temperatures reaching hundreds of millikelvin.
