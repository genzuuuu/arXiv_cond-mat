### Superconductivity & Pairing Mechanisms
- **MgB₂ Superconductivity Enhanced by Quantum Geometry**: A revisit of MgB₂ shows that the record-high Tc (39 K) is boosted by quantum-geometric effects from a bond-centered kagome lattice description. The study demonstrates that doping trends in phonon-mediated superconductors can be shaped by geometric contributions. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19458)
- **Anyon Superconductivity in Twisted MoTe₂**: A theoretical framework for doping a ν=2/3 FQAH state reveals a panoply of anyon-driven phases, including topological superconductors and SC* phases where superconductivity coexists with topological order, arising from color superconductivity of charge-e/3 "quarks." [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19470)
- **Kohn-Luttinger Mechanism for Unconventional CDWs**: A Kohn-Luttinger-like mechanism is proposed for p-wave and d-wave charge density waves in metals, driven by one-loop spin and pairing corrections in the charge channel. This provides an electronic mechanism for bond and loop current order on square and triangular lattices. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.11621)
- **Chiral Superconductivity Quenched by Phase Fluctuations**: Quantum phase fluctuations are shown to fundamentally suppress the chiral d+id' phase in twisted cuprate bilayers, restricting it to a narrow twist-angle window and ultralow temperatures. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00630)
- **Phase Modes in Twisted d-wave Bilayers**: A theoretical analysis of low-energy phase fluctuations in twisted d-wave superconducting bilayers reveals that higher-order Josephson coupling processes soften the Josephson plasma frequency and reduce the critical current. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00662)
- **Proximity Effect & Critical Temperature Solution**: An analytical solution to the coupled linearized Usadel and self-consistency equations is presented for a dirty superconductor near Tc with proximity effect, reducing the problem to an eigenvalue equation. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.20557)
- **Annealing Enhances Spin-Orbit Effects in NbRe Films**: Thermal annealing of NbRe films amplifies spin-orbit scattering, leading to an upper critical field that surpasses both the orbital and Pauli paramagnetic limits. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18994)

### Topological & Quantum Materials
- **Majorana Bound States in Anisotropic Dirac/Weyl Systems**: An analytical framework describes Majorana bound states in 2D Dirac and 3D Weyl systems. Tilted cones are found to suppress Majorana propagation velocity, while effective chiral p±ip pairing emerges in 3D Weyl systems from s-wave pairing. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19707)
- **Chirality-Dependent p-wave Magnetism**: An exact solution of electrons coupled to a spin spiral shows that magnetic chirality alone can generate spin-momentum locking and a spin Edelstein effect, without spin-orbit coupling. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20094)
- **Twisted Spin Symmetries**: Anisotropic spin interactions are shown to twist spin-space group symmetries via cohomology invariants, leading to a new symmetry classification. A spin-1 model with this symmetry hosts Z₂ topological quadrupolar excitations on a Brillouin Klein bottle. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.14969)
- **Axial & Multipolar Order & SHG**: A gauge-consistent theory of spatially dispersive second-harmonic generation is developed to probe centrosymmetric multipolar states like ferroaxial order. Both electric-quadrupole and magnetic-dipole contributions are shown to be essential. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19821)
- **Metamagnetism in Heavy-Fermion Systems**: A new c-f theory identifies Kondo coherence as the microscopic origin of magnetic anisotropy and metamagnetism in heavy-fermion materials like UTe₂, unifying their response across temperature, field, and pressure. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20261)
- **Magnetoresistive Memory in Paramagnetic Phase**: A novel Magnetoresistive Memory (MRM) effect is discovered in Eu₅In₂As₆, appearing at twice the Néel temperature, suggesting a hidden order or fluctuating phase as the underlying mechanism. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20287)
- **Stacking-Tuned Superconductivity & CDW in NbSe₂**: Scanning tunneling microscopy reveals that the layer stacking sequence in NbSe₂ qualitatively transforms both the charge-density wave and superconducting orders, with competing 1Q and 3Q CDW phases coexisting via phase vortices. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20335)

### Strongly Correlated Systems & Quantum Magnetism
- **Mott Semimetal in 1D Hubbard Model**: Exact diagonalization and DMRG show that a 1D Hubbard model with a sublattice-symmetry-breaking scheme hosts a quantum Mott semimetal phase—a spinful Dirac fermion coexisting with a neutral spin mode, analogous to a fractionalized Fermi liquid. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19465)
- **Anyon Crystals & Hall Crystals in Periodic Potential**: Integer and fractional quantum Hall crystals are predicted as ground states, with certain states forming "anyon crystals." An effective boson model shows that a strong interaction limit realizes a supersolid with vortex-anti-vortex pairs. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19466)
- **Loop Currents in STM**: Specific predictions for observing loop current orders in cuprates and Sr₂RuO₄ via scanning tunneling microscopy are provided. Signatures include degeneracy lifting and a finite spin polarization from spin-orbit coupling. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20030)
- **Multi-Criticality in Cuprates**: An analysis of the charge-Kondo-breakdown scenario in cuprates using RG topology shows that the quantum critical regime is an extended crossover controlled by a near-critical fixed point, not genuine scale invariance. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.19807)
- **Four-Spin Ring Exchange**: A novel sixteen-state energy mapping method is introduced to directly extract four-spin (ring) exchange from first principles, with validation on La₂CuO₄ and SrFeO₂, revealing that bilinear exchange J is itself ring-renormalized. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18986)
- **Weak Frustration in 2D Ferromagnets**: A small fraction of antiferromagnetic bonds in 2D Heisenberg ferromagnets is shown to cause anomalous spin-wave softening and a flow toward an infinite-disorder, zero-stiffness regime. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20036)
- **Ergodic-Flocking Transition**: A 1D lattice model shows that motility destabilizes absorbing ferromagnetic states, leading to a continuous transition from an ordered flock to a disordered state with activity. [Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.20034)
