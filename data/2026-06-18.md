### Superconductivity & Topological Superconductivity

*   **Magnetic Field-Induced Nodal Superconductivity in β-PdBi₂:** A theoretical model explains a recent experiment where a magnetic field drives a transition from fully gapped (s-wave) to nodal (p-wave) superconductivity. This is due to Zeeman splitting and a competition between pairing channels. [Paper 8](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18482)
*   **Cavity-Enhanced Superconductivity in NbSe₂:** By tuning a terahertz cavity to couple with phononic modes in few-layer NbSe₂, the superconducting transition temperature was increased by ~10%. This is the first experimental demonstration of vacuum fields enhancing, rather than suppressing, superconductivity. [Paper 53](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19171)
*   **Multiband Superconductivity in 2H-NbSeS:** Transport and thermodynamic measurements on a mixed-chalcogen system where charge density wave order is suppressed provide evidence for intrinsic two-band superconductivity, resolving a long-standing debate. [Paper 49](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19107)
*   **Repulsive-Interaction-Driven Topological Superconductivity:** Exact diagonalization shows that a repulsive electron interaction in a half-filled Landau level can induce topological superconductivity when proximity-coupled to an s-wave superconductor, challenging the conventional wisdom that attractive interactions are required. [Paper 92](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04700)
*   **Counterintuitive Inverse Superconducting Transition:** A new "inverse" superconducting transition is reported in Eu-based nickelate thin films, where superconductivity appears upon *increasing* temperature above the 4He-cooling limit, offering a new paradigm for quantum-phase-transition devices. [Paper 19](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18683)

### Quantum Magnetism & Altermagnetism

*   **d-wave Altermagnetism from RIXS:** Resonant inelastic X-ray scattering (RIXS) with circular dichroism provides unambiguous experimental evidence of d-wave altermagnetism in La₂O₃Mn₂Se₂, identifying it as a key spectroscopic tool for this new magnetic phase. [Paper 21](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18731)
*   **Suppression of Altermagnetism by Localized f-Electrons:** ARPES and calculations on CeNiAsO show that localized 4f electrons can strongly suppress the observable p-wave-like altermagnetic band splitting, highlighting the role of strong correlations. [Paper 116](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02422)
*   **Projected Altermagnetism at Surfaces:** Theoretical analysis shows that surface and thin-film symmetry breaking can fundamentally reshape altermagnetic spin textures, for example enabling d-wave splitting from a g-wave bulk altermagnet. [Paper 39](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18964)
*   **Strain-Induced Magnetic Phase Transition:** First-principles calculations demonstrate that shear strain can drive a transition from altermagnetism to a ferrimagnetic phase in RuO₂ and MnF₂, activating anomalous transport phenomena. [Paper 14](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18603)
*   **Ising Supercriticality & Magnetocalorics in Nd₃BWO₉:** A spiral antiferromagnet is shown to host supercritical behavior, with a divergent magnetocaloric effect that enables adiabatic demagnetization cooling down to 195 mK. [Paper 101](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.07810)

### Quantum Simulation & Computation

*   **Exclusion Statistics as a Thermodynamic Resource:** A theoretical study shows that using particles with generalized exclusion statistics (e.g., anyons or bosons) as a working medium in a quantum heat engine can provide a strictly higher maximum power output than fermions. [Paper 60](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19310)
*   **An Anyonic Thermodynamic Ensemble:** Experimental realization of generalized exclusion statistics in a 1D strongly interacting quantum gas, with thermodynamic measurements showing clear deviations from Bose and Fermi behavior. [Paper 41](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19009)
*   **ARES Ground State Preparation:** The TETRIS-ADAPT-VQE algorithm efficiently constructs highly accurate ground states for the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK) and Sherrington-Kirkpatrick (SK) models, showing potential quantum advantage for strongly correlated systems. [Paper 69](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18339)
*   **Attention-Based Foundation Model for Quantum States:** A neural network architecture is trained to predict accurate ground state wavefunctions across different Hamiltonians, system sizes, and interaction strengths, acting as a universal model for quantum matter. [Paper 98](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11962)
*   **Quantum Soliton Quantum Walks in Transmon Arrays:** A theoretical proposal demonstrates that quantum solitons can be realized in arrays of superconducting transmon qubits and their quantum walk dynamics can be observed with current technology. [Paper 86](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19339)

### Topological & Geometrical Phenomena

*   **Topological Plasmons in Weyl Metals:** A theoretical study reveals that bulk plasmons in Weyl semimetals have a topological monopole structure with finite vorticity, and selectively couple to light in a quantum-geometric manner. [Paper 2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18346)
*   **Magnon-Dislon Hybridization:** An effective field theory shows that lattice dislocations (dislons) can hybridize with magnons in magnetic insulators, creating new hybrid excitations with polarization fingerprints that can be used for spin control. [Paper 10](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18513)
*   **Bidirectional Motion of Antiferromagnetic Skyrmions:** Micromagnetic simulations demonstrate that antiferromagnetic skyrmions can reverse their motion direction when the applied current exceeds a threshold, enabling novel logic gates. [Paper 18](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18657)
*   **Chiral Packings in Deformed Cylinders:** A study of sphere packings inside elliptic cylinders reveals that even weak cross-sectional deformation can trigger entirely new chiral and non-chiral phases, explaining biological packing structures. [Paper 47](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19082)

### Transport & Electronic Properties

*   **Trion Hall Effect in Double Layers:** The Hall effect for trions (three-body bound states) is observed in MoSe₂/WSe₂ heterobilayers, appearing in both Hall drag and standard Hall effect measurements. [Paper 17](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18647)
*   **Negative Diffusivity of Excitons:** A hydrodynamic theory shows that electron-hole plasma dynamics can cause an effective negative diffusion coefficient for excitons, explaining recent experimental observations. [Paper 4](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18401)
*   **Dynamics of Monohydroxy Alcohols:** A "living chain model" for supramolecular structures formed by reversible hydrogen bonding quantitatively describes the dielectric and viscoelastic properties of monohydroxy alcohols, revealing five distinct dynamic regimes. [Paper 16](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18626)
*   **Nonlinear Spin Transport in Chiral Conductors:** A theory links the chirality-induced spin selectivity (CISS) effect to the orbital Edelstein effect, explaining its persistence even with weak spin-orbit coupling. [Paper 58](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19274)
*   **Controlling Domain Walls with Supercurrents:** A theoretical proposal shows that spin accumulation from supercurrents can drive magnetic domain wall motion in superconductor/magnetic insulator bilayers with high efficiency. [Paper 46](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19078)

### Materials Synthesis & Characterization

*   **Room-Temperature Ca Intercalation into Graphite:** Sodium catalysis enables the formation of superconducting CaC₆ at room temperature, a process previously thought impossible, opening new routes for battery electrodes. [Paper 43](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.19024)
*   **CVD Synthesis of Ni-doped FGT:** Chemical vapor deposition is used for the first time to synthesize Ni-doped iron germanium telluride (FGT) nanosheets, a promising material for spintronics. [Paper 12](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18546)
*   **Moiré Glass in Layered Silicates:** Montmorillonite clay naturally forms tunable moiré superlattices with preferred twist angles determined by discrete hydration states, defining a new "moiré glass" state. [Paper 7](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18477)
*   **Self-Aligned Metallic-Semiconducting Nanoarrays:** By using curved Cu surfaces, two distinct 2D phosphorus phases (metallic and semiconducting) are grown simultaneously, creating self-aligned nanoarrays. [Paper 34](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.18907)
*   **Polymorph Selection via Machine Learning:** Bayesian optimization dramatically accelerates the discovery of growth conditions for phase-pure γ-In₂Se₃ thin films in molecular beam epitaxy. [Paper 102](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.13156)
