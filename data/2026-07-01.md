### Topological and Correlated Phases of Matter

- **Magnetic Bloch Oscillations:** A theoretical study predicts "magnetic Bloch oscillations" in odd-wave magnets, where magnetization oscillates in response to an electric field before relaxing to a nonlinear Edelstein effect state. This phenomenon is proposed as a signature for THz spectroscopy.
    - [Magnetic Bloch Oscillations in Odd-Wave Magnets and the Nonlinear Edelstein Effect](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30732)

- **Altermagnetic Triplet Superconductivity:** A theoretical analysis of the $A$V$_2X_2$O family of surface altermagnets predicts an exotic equal-spin triplet superconducting state, driven by the material's unique magnetic sublattice structure. This triplet state is topologically non-trivial and supports spin-polarized persistent currents.
    - [Unconventional superconductivity in $A$V$_2X_2$O family of surface altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30766)

- **Synthetic Magnonic Lattice:** An experiment realizes a synthetic dimension in a magnonic system using Floquet engineering. By coupling multimode magnon resonances in a single yttrium iron garnet (YIG) device with time-periodic modulation, a reconfigurable mode-space lattice is created, enabling a high-dimensional platform for magnonic dynamics.
    - [Experimental Realization of Synthetic Magnonic Lattice via Floquet Engineering](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30845)

- **Tunable Non-Fermi Liquid in Graphene Moiré:** An experiment reports a tunable extended non-Fermi liquid (NFL) phase in twisted double bilayer graphene. The NFL behavior spans a broad range of carrier densities with a tunable resistance exponent, suggesting strong quantum fluctuations from the interplay of localized and itinerant carriers.
    - [Tunable Extended Magnetic Non-Fermi Liquid in Graphene Moiré Heterostructures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31028)

- **Ultrafast Magnetization in Semiconductors:** A high-throughput first-principles screening identifies nearly 440 non-magnetic semiconductors that can develop ultrafast spin polarization when excited by linearly polarized femtosecond laser pulses. This opens a new avenue for on-off switching of magnetism.
    - [Ultrafast magnetization induced by linearly polarized pulses is widespread in nonmagnetic semiconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31358)

### Highly Frustrated Magnetism and Quantum Spin Liquids

- **Quantum Spin Liquid in Triangular Lattice:** Using infinite projected entangled-pair states (PEPS), researchers investigate the $J_1$-$J_2$ Heisenberg model on the triangular lattice. The study finds evidence for a gapless quantum spin liquid phase, most consistent with a U(1) Dirac spin liquid, between the 120° Néel and stripe antiferromagnetic orders.
    - [Investigation of the $J_1$-$J_2$ Heisenberg model on the triangular lattice: A study with projected entangled-pair states](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31021)

- **Fully Compensated Ferrimagnetic Triferroics:** First-principles calculations identify a hidden-phase MnSe monolayer as an intrinsic fully compensated ferrimagnet (fFIM) that simultaneously exhibits ferroelectricity and ferroelasticity. The material shows nonvolatile electrical control and giant tunneling magnetoresistance.
    - [Fully compensated ferrimagnetic triferroics and multistate transport in hidden-phase wurtzite MnSe monolayer](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31419)

- **Symmetry-Enforced Ferroelectric Switching of Altermagnetism:** A universal layer-engineering paradigm is proposed to achieve deterministic switching of altermagnetic spin-splitting using ferroelectric polarization. The mechanism is validated in an In₂Se₃/MnPTe₃/In₂Se₃ trilayer, where reversing polarization flips the anomalous Hall effect signal.
    - [Symmetry-Enforced Ferroelectric Switching of Two-Dimensional Altermagnetism](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31241)

### Transport, Light-Matter Coupling, and Nonlinear Optics

- **Light-Induced Chirality:** A study investigates the origin of light-induced chirality in boron phosphate (BPO₄). It finds that a three-phonon mixing mechanism becomes dominant when the pump pulse is detuned from resonance, providing a foundation for nonlinear phononic lineshapes.
    - [Three-phonon mixing as a source of light-induced chirality](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31356)

- **Critically Enhanced Magnon Transport:** A theory predicts logarithmically diverging magnon conductivity in quasi-two-dimensional magnets with long-range dipolar interactions. This mechanism may explain the unusually large magnon conductivities observed in ultrathin yttrium iron garnet films.
    - [Critically Enhanced Magnon Transport in Low-dimensional Magnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30784)

- **Topological Random Alloy:** A minimal model of a topological random binary alloy shows that doping a trivial quantum anomalous Hall insulator with chiral dopants can create topologically-protected chiral current loops and induce a topological phase transition, even at dilute dopant concentrations.
    - [Topological random alloy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30917)

- **Giant Perpendicular Edelstein Polarization:** A theoretical study shows that combining Rashba spin-orbit coupling with bichromatic Floquet driving can generate giant perpendicular Edelstein polarizations in 2D altermagnets, a feat unachievable with monochromatic driving.
    - [Giant perpendicular Edelstein polarization in 2D compensated magnets via bichromatic Floquet driving](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31867)

### Superconductivity

- **Thermal Transport in 4Hb-TaS₂:** An experimental study investigates the low-energy excitations in the van der Waals superconductor 4Hb-TaS₂. While specific heat is broadly consistent with a nodeless gap, a finite residual density of states is present. However, thermal conductivity extrapolates to zero, suggesting these states are localized and do not contribute to heat transport.
    - [Thermal transport and low-temperature specific heat in 4Hb-TaS₂](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30874)

- **Photon Statistics in Yb³⁺-Doped CsPbCl₃:** Photon-correlation experiments on Yb³⁺-doped CsPbCl₃ nanocrystals show antibunching rather than the expected photon bunching for a quantum-cutting material. This result questions the validity of reported high photoluminescence quantum yields and supports an Auger quenching pathway instead.
    - [Photon Statistics from Yb3+-Doped CsPbCl3 are Inconsistent with Quantum Cutting](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31359)

### Materials Synthesis and Discovery

- **Computational Materials Discovery:** An adaptive workflow using a foundation-model-assisted machine learning interatomic potential (MLIP) is introduced for crystal structure prediction. Applied to the Ca-Fe-Ni system, it predicts a new high-pressure phase, Ca₆FeNi, demonstrating data-efficient discovery in complex systems.
    - [Adaptive fine-tuning of foundation models for crystal structure prediction: Discovery of high-pressure phases in the CaFeNi system](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30870)

- **Hidden Ordered Compound-Layer:** Atomic simulations reveal that the superior performance of Ge₂Sb₂SexTe₅₋ₓ (GSST) phase-change materials is governed by an in-layer compound-like structure (e.g., SeTe₂), not the previously believed pure-element-layered model. This corrects the long-standing theory-experiment gap for optical properties.
    - [Hidden ordered compound-layer and its tailoring of the electronic/optical property in Ge2Sb2SexTe5-x alloys](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31047)

- **Amorphous InZnO as a Cu Diffusion Barrier:** A combinatorial study identifies thin, amorphous indium-zinc-oxide (In-Zn-O) films as effective transparent conductive Cu diffusion barriers for solar cell metallization. The optimal Zn/(In+Zn) ratio is ~0.12 with intermediate oxygen deficiency.
    - [Accelerated development of amorphous InZnO thin films as transparent conductive Cu diffusion barriers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31605)

### Other Key Papers

- **Thermal Rectification in Nanoparticles:** A theory demonstrates that thermal rectification can occur at the contact between two nanoparticles of the same material but different sizes due to the geometric confinement of phonons, providing a scalable route to thermal diodes.
    - [Thermal rectification due to phonon confinement in nanoparticles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31263)

- **Athermality of Generalized Gibbs Ensembles:** Using the concept of "athermality," researchers quantify how different Generalized Gibbs Ensembles (GGEs) are from thermal states in integrable quantum systems. A key finding is that the athermality becomes anomalously small at quantum critical points.
    - [Athermality of generalized Gibbs ensembles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30762)

- **Intrinsic Anderson Localization in ReS₂:** By eliminating extrinsic disorder, researchers observe intrinsic Anderson localization in few-layer ReS₂. The measured localization length is an order of magnitude larger than in disorder-dominated devices, providing a quantitative benchmark.
    - [Observation of Intrinsic Anderson Localization in Few-Layer ReS$_2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31233)
