### Superconductivity & Superconducting Devices
- [Elimination of Flux Trapping in Superconducting Circuits in Ambient Magnetic Fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27415): A practical solution using integrated heaters and etched moats to eliminate flux trapping in superconducting circuits during cooldown, even in ambient magnetic fields up to 60 μT.
- [The Second Law, Symmetry of Time Reversal and Thermodynamic Equilibrium in Superconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27628): Theoretical analysis arguing that time-reversal symmetry in superconductors is a consequence of the Second Law and a mandatory property in equilibrium.
- [Thickness dependence of diode efficiency in superconducting Fe(Se,Te)/FeTe thin-film heterostructure devices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27639): Demonstration that the superconducting diode effect efficiency increases with Fe(Se,Te) layer thickness, linked to structural inversion asymmetry and Rashba spin-orbit interaction.

- [Imaging geometry- and phase-controlled spectra in a surface-state Andreev cavity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28189): Scanning tunneling spectroscopy of confined Cu(111) surface states coupled to superconducting Nb(110) reveals geometry-defined Andreev trajectories as a design principle.
- [Current-induced re-entrant superconductivity and extreme nonreciprocal superconducting diode effect in valley-polarized systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.00835): Theoretical proposal of a mechanism for extreme nonreciprocal SDE and current-induced re-entrant superconductivity via interplay between valley polarizations and applied currents.
- [Measuring intrinsic relaxation rates in superconductors using nonlinear response](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07398): Theoretical framework showing how intrinsic relaxation rates (Higgs, quasiparticle redistribution/dephasing) in s- and d-wave superconductors can be extracted from nonlinear terahertz response.

- [Higher dimensional quantum Hall effect and the analog of W∞-algebra](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.28219): Shows that gauge transformations for higher-dimensional quantum Hall effect are analogs of W∞ transformations, deriving the extended algebra from topological arguments.
- [Excitonic Proximity Effects in Fractional Chern Insulators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.14365): Demonstrates exciton-quasihole bound states in fractional Chern insulators via exact diagonalization, with binding energies accessible to photoluminescence.

- [Theory of Spin-splitter Magnetoresistance in Altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10747): Develops a theory distinguishing spin-splitter magnetoresistance from spin Hall magnetoresistance, providing a smoking-gun signature for altermagnetism.
- [Interaction enhanced altermagnet in the Hatsugai-Kohmoto model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.04141): Shows that interactions can induce a many-body Lifshitz transition to a fully spin-polarized state, termed "interaction enhanced altermagnet".
