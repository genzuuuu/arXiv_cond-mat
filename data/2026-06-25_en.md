### Superconductivity & Unconventional Pairing

- **Exactly Solvable Pair-Density Wave in Topological Flat Bands:** This work constructs exact solvable models for pair-density wave (PDW) superconductors in topological flat bands. Using a generalized quantum geometric nesting framework with magnetic translation and time-reversal symmetries, the models provide a platform for studying band topology and quantum geometry in PDW states. [Exactly solvable pair-density wave in topological flat bands from magnetic translation symmetries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25038)
- **Correlation-Driven d-Wave Superconducting Dome from Pseudogap:** A self-consistent microscopic calculation shows that the interplay between pseudogap correlations and d-wave superconducting order naturally generates a dome-shaped phase diagram in the temperature-doping plane, with optimal doping near a quantum critical point. The d-wave pairing is found to be robust across a wide doping range. [Correlation-Driven $d$-Wave Superconducting Dome from Pseudogap Spectral Reconstruction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.05761)
- **Universal Condensation Energy of BCS Bose Gases:** A Boson-Fermion formalism is used to calculate the condensation energy for various superconductors. The ratio of condensation energy to the Sommerfeld constant shows a universal scaling with Tc, confirming experimental fits for both conventional and cuprate superconductors. [Universal behavior of the condensation energy of Superconducting BCS Bose gases](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25316)
- **Quasi-2D Superconductivity in W-doped 3R-TaSe2:** W-doped 3R-TaSe2 is shown to host weakly coupled, anisotropic unconventional superconductivity at Tc ~ 2.8 K. The system exhibits a Berezinskii-Kosterlitz-Thouless transition and a two-fold rotational symmetry in the superconducting state under in-plane magnetic fields, breaking the lattice's three-fold symmetry. [Emergence of Quasi-two-dimensional Superconductivity in W-doped Bulk Noncentrosymmetric 3$R$-TaSe$_2$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25416)
- **Vortex Pinning from First Principles:** A new first-principles computational framework is established to compute the elementary vortex pinning force in type-II superconductors from defect-resolved electronic structures. Applied to FeSe and FeTe, the framework reproduces experimental STM data, linking the reorganization of Caroli-de Gennes-Matricon states to the pinning force. [First-Principles Quantum-Spectral framework for Elementary Vortex Pinning in superconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25862)
- **Dissipation in Strongly Disordered Superconductors:** A new microscopic theory is proposed to explain low-frequency dissipation in strongly disordered superconductors (SDSCs). The dissipation is dominated by bulk localized collective modes arising from spatial inhomogeneity, leading to a strong decrease in the quality factor with frequency and a temperature dependence similar to two-level systems. [Dissipation due to Bulk Localized Low-Energy Modes in Strongly Disordered Superconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11636)
- **Magnetic Fluctuations in UTe2:** A multi-orbital study using GGA+U and RPA investigates magnetic fluctuations in the spin-triplet superconductor candidate UTe2 under pressure. The calculations show that pressure evolution of magnetism is governed by the momentum-space distribution of U 5f states. [Magnetic fluctuations and anisotropy in UTe2: a multi-orbital study based on GGA+U and RPA](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.15322)
- **Tripling of Jc in BaFe2(As,P)2 after Pressure Release:** In BaFe2(As,P)2, hydrostatic pressure cycling triples the critical current density and reduces the rate of thermally activated vortex motion. The effects are irreversible and retained after pressure release, attributed to microcrack formation and a change in the dominant pinning mechanism. [Tripling of the Superconducting Critical Current Density in BaFe$_2$(As$_{1-x}$P$_x$)$_2$ Retained After Pressure Release](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.01328)

### Topological & Quantum Spin Systems

- **Non-Abelian Anyon Braiding with Quantum-Antidot Interferometry:** A new interferometry scheme using two gate-controlled quantum antidots is proposed to probe elementary exchanges of non-Abelian anyons. Tuning the antidots allows for distinct braiding processes, enabling a direct measure of non-Abelian statistics and topological spins. [Non-Abelian Anyon Braiding with Quantum-Antidot Interferometry](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24930)
- **Local Spectroscopy of Anyons Bound to Charge Traps:** Scanning tunneling microscopy is used to study the excitation spectrum of fractional quantum Hall states near charged impurities in graphene. The observation of additional energy splittings at fractional fillings (ν=1/3, 2/5) provides direct spectroscopic evidence of anyons bound to impurity potentials. [Local spectroscopy of anyons bound to charge traps](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25024)
- **Real-space Imaging of Quantum Hall Quasiparticles:** Scanning tunneling spectroscopy is used to image quantum Hall quasiparticles in graphene. In fractional quantum Hall states, the technique reveals spatial and spectroscopic signatures of localized anyons, with theoretical calculations linking the data to a three-anyon bound state at ν = 5/3. [Real-space Imaging of Quantum Hall Quasiparticles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25036)
- **Non-Hermitian Chern Bulk-Boundary Correspondence in Active Matter:** A minimal frustrated Vicsek-Kuramoto active-particle model is shown to realize a non-Hermitian Chern bulk-boundary correspondence. The model exhibits robust one-way boundary flow, with the topology fixed by the spin structure of the dispersion matrix. [A Minimal Active-Particle Realization of Non-Hermitian Chern Bulk-Boundary Correspondence](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24926)
- **Symmetry-Protected Topological Phases in Majorana Ladders:** A family of symmetry-protected topological phases is discovered in a model of interacting Majorana fermions on triangular-lattice ladders. The work reveals a mechanism where interactions among Majorana modes generate nontrivial symmetry-fractionalized orders. [Symmetry-Protected Topological Phases in the Triangular Majorana-Hubbard Ladder](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.24887)
- **Pauli Stabilizer Formalism for TQFTs:** New classes of lattice gauge theories are constructed as Pauli stabilizer models, realizing a wide range of topological quantum field theories, including a (4+1)D fermionic-loop toric code. The framework systematically determines the generalized statistics of extended excitations in any dimension. [Pauli stabilizer formalism for topological quantum field theories and generalized statistics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.00064)
- **Higher Berry Curvature and Second Chern Numbers:** A lattice model of a 4D Chern insulator is rewritten as a family of infinite chains. The higher three-form Berry curvature is computed, showing that the topological phase diagram matches that given by the second Chern number, demonstrating a quantized computation method. [Higher Berry curvature, second Chern numbers and magnetoelectric coupling in crystalline insulators](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26096)
- **Topological Entanglement in a Monitored Rashba Nanowire:** The topological value of the disconnected entanglement entropy in a monitored Rashba chain persists for a time linear in the system size, even with dissipation at the boundaries, due to switching between topological states and poisoning by ballistically propagating quasiparticles. [Long-lasting Topological Entanglement in a Monitored Rashba Nanowire](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25653)

### Quantum Fluids, Lattices & Criticality

- **Fractionalized Vortices Drive KT Transitions:** In dipole-conserving superfluids, the conventional Kosterlitz-Thouless (KT) transition is driven by the deconfinement of fractionalized vortices. These unconventional vortices have logarithmically divergent self-energies and can lead to a hierarchy of KT transitions with anisotropy. [Fractionalized Vortices Drive Kosterlitz-Thouless Transitions in Dipole-Conserving Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25340)
- **Curvature-Induced Smectic-C Order on a Sphere:** In the rigid locked-orientation limit of hard spherocylinders on a sphere, curvature induces a smectic-C order. The tilt angle and boundaries of the smectic area window are geometrically determined and confirmed by Monte Carlo simulations without fitted elastic constants. [Curvature-induced smectic-C order of tangentially anchored hard spherocylinders on a sphere with a rigidly locked director field](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24961)
- **Self-Organized Stabilization of Dark Solitons:** Anisotropic long-range interactions in a quasi-2D dipolar BEC stabilize a straight dark soliton against modulational instability. Spontaneous stripe order in the supersolid phase further pins the soliton, increasing its bending stiffness. [Self-Organized Stabilization of Straight Dark Solitons in Stripe Supersolids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25687)
- **Fractional Phase Slips Across CDW Domain Walls:** Scanning tunneling microscopy reveals a robust 2π/3 phase shift of the charge density wave (CDW) order across domain walls in 1T-TiSe2. This finding contradicts a simple phonon-driven picture and points to the importance of electronic interactions in shaping the CDW order parameter. [Fractional phase slips across the charge-density-wave domain walls in 1-T TiSe2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25963)
- **Quantum Back-Action Expands Excitonic Hilbert Space:** In lead-halide perovskite nanocrystals, femtosecond spectroscopy shows that quantum back-action between an electronic excitation and a lattice-polarization field expands the excitonic Hilbert space in real time. This demonstrates a new regime where strong system-bath coupling actively creates excitonic states. [Quantum Back-Action Expands the Excitonic Hilbert Space in a Soft Polar Semiconductor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26063)
- **Electronic Sound in Disordered Multiband Metals:** A hydrodynamic and microscopic study shows that 'demon' modes (acoustic plasmons) in disordered multiband metals become purely imaginary, leading to diffusive behavior. This provides a parameter-free explanation for the anomalous nonlinear dispersion reported in Sr2RuO4. [Electronic Sound and Diffusion in Disordered Multiband Metals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17002)
- **Emergent SUSY at Fractionalized Quantum Criticality:** Numerical and analytical analysis of the Kitaev honeycomb model with spin-phonon coupling shows that a fractionalized quantum critical point between a Dirac spin liquid and a valence-bond-solid phase features emergent N=2 spacetime supersymmetry. [Emergent spacetime supersymmetry at 2D fractionalized quantum criticality](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.07383)

### Altermagnets & Spintronics

- **Residual Orbital Magnetization Governs AHE in Altermagnets:** The anomalous Hall effect in altermagnets is shown to be governed by residual orbital magnetization via the Středa relation. A microscopic mechanism is proposed for generating this net orbital moment from the interplay of crystal field and spin-orbit coupling. [Residual orbital magnetization governs the anomalous Hall effect in altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25999)
- **Epitaxial Strain Activates Altermagnetic Spin Splitting in RuO2:** Angular-resolved spin-torque measurements show that altermagnetic spin splitting in RuO2 thin films is a strain-stabilized emergent state. The effect is strongest in the strained regime and is suppressed as the lattice relaxes toward the bulk limit, resolving a longstanding discrepancy. [Epitaxial Strain Activates Altermagnetic Spin-Splitting Torques in RuO2(100)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26023)
- **Dephasing-Protected Spin Qubits in Altermagnets:** Altermagnetic semiconductors are introduced as a platform for all-electrical, magnetic-field-free spin qubits. The qubits are protected from pure dephasing by construction and compatible with superconducting resonators, enabling a complete gate set including the fSim family. [All-electrical dephasing-protected spin qubits in altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26066)

### Moiré & 2D Materials

- **Layer-Tunable Hubbard Bands in MoSe2/WS2 Heterostructures:** By using moiré excitons as local probes, the layer-dependent on-site Coulomb repulsions (U) are quantitatively extracted in a MoSe2/WS2 heterobilayer. A vertical electric field is shown to be a deterministic knob for tuning layer-selective Hubbard physics and stabilizing generalized Wigner crystals. [Layer-tunable Hubbard bands probed via moiré excitons in MoSe$_2$/WS$_2$ heterostructures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25071)
- **Spin-flip Optical Excitations in CrPS4:** Previously unreported spin-entangled optical resonances are identified in the antiferromagnet CrPS4. The magnetic-field dependence of these resonances provides key magnetic parameters and demonstrates a potential for all-optical probing of spin states. [Spin-flip optical excitations in van der Waals antiferromagnet CrPS$_4$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25470)
- **Strong Photogalvanic Effect in Weyl Materials:** The photogalvanic effect in Weyl semimetals under a magnetic field is studied, focusing on the shift current. A series of magnetic resonances are identified analytically and confirmed with a semiclassical Boltzmann approach that incorporates finite scattering. [Strong photogalvanic effect in Weyl materials due to magnetic resonances](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26067)
- **Defect Engineered MoS2 for Neurotransmitter SERS:** Defect-engineered MoS2 with sulfur vacancies is used as a platform for Surface-Enhanced Raman Scattering (SERS) detection of catechol-containing neurotransmitters. The platform achieves sub-nanomolar detection with 100% accuracy in distinguishing dopamine and epinephrine using machine learning. [Defect Engineered 2D MoS2 Materials for ML-enabled Neurotransmitter SERS Detection](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25235)

### Experimental & Computational Techniques

- **Operando Spectro-Ptychography of Battery Electrodes:** A fast operando soft X-ray spectro-ptychography platform is developed to create chemical-state-resolved movies of redox-active electrodes. Applied to an alkaline Fe anode, it reveals the interplay between fast, reversible charge storage and slower, irreversible degradation chemistry. [Operando spectro-ptychography reveals dynamical charge-storage and degradation pathways in redox-active electrodes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25175)
- **Accelerating Chemical Potential Calculations with Normalizing Flows:** A "minimal" normalizing flow (MNF) is introduced to accelerate free energy calculations in molecular simulations. These low-dimensional, physically-informed transformations can be trained quickly on a GPU, speeding up chemical potential calculations for Lennard-Jones particles and ion solvation by up to 10x. [Accelerating Chemical Potential Calculations with Minimal Normalizing Flows](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25229)
- **Differentiable DFT Framework for Inverse Design:** A framework integrating reverse-mode automatic differentiation into the KKR-CPA method enables gradient-based optimization of continuous atomic compositions to find materials with target properties. This is demonstrated by designing magnetic alloys and half-metals from compositional spaces with dozens of elements. [A Differentiable DFT-Based Framework for Inverse Materials Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25502)
- **Automatic-Differentiation-Enabled Dynamic Parameter Retrieval:** A full-waveform inversion framework using automatic differentiation is presented to retrieve time-dependent physical observables (e.g., scattering rate) from time-resolved THz spectroscopy with sub-pulse-width resolution, even when the response function is ill-defined. [Automatic-differentiation-enabled dynamic parameter retrieval with sub-pulse-width resolution](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25479)
- **BLS Spectroscopy of Magnons at Sub-Kelvin Temperatures:** The first optical detection of propagating spin waves via Brillouin Light Scattering spectroscopy inside a dilution refrigerator is demonstrated, bringing free-space optical access to magnons into the sub-kelvin regime. [Brillouin Light Scattering Spectroscopy of Propagating Magnons at Sub-Kelvin Temperatures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25834)
- **KuboMap: Real-Space Conductivity Mapping:** A new representation of electronic conductivity, KuboMap, is introduced. Derived from the Kubo-Greenwood formula, it provides a real-space map of transport-active regions, distinguishing metallic, insulating, and hopping-like conduction pathways in complex materials like aluminum and amorphous silicon. [Real-Space Mapping of Electronic Conductivity in Complex Materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24914)
- **Spectral Leakage in Hyperuniformity Measurement:** A unified theoretical framework quantifies how finite observation windows and masks distort the measured structure factor in hyperuniform systems. The framework establishes criteria for extracting true intrinsic scaling exponents and distinguishing genuine hyperuniform order from artifacts. [Spectral Leakage and Masking Effects in the Measurement of Hyperuniformity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24904)
- **XY-Mixer QAOA for Defect Configurations:** A constraint-aware quantum optimization workflow for defect configuration search in doped ZrO2 is developed. A constraint-preserving XY-mixer QAOA places 86% of probability mass within 1 meV of the exact optimum, and a fault-tolerant Grover Adaptive Search oracle is validated. [Constraint-Aware Quantum Optimization of Defect Configurations in Doped ZrO2: XY-Mixer QAOA and Grover Adaptive Search](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24922)
