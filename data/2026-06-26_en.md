### Superconductivity
- **[New Superconductors in the PtPb$_3$Bi Structure Type](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26258)**: Reports new anisotropic type-II superconductors in the $M$Pb$_{4-x}$Bi$_x$ family (M = Au, Pd, Rh), with T$_c$'s up to 4.9 K. This establishes a platform to study how site disorder and Pb-Bi mixing govern superconductivity.
- **[Fluctuation-Induced Magnetoelectric Effect in Noncentrosymmetric Superconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26261)**: Shows that superconducting fluctuations in 2D noncentrosymmetric materials create a magnetoelectric effect even for s-wave pairing, a mechanism forbidden in centrosymmetric systems, and enhances NMR relaxation rates.
- **[Photon-Assisted Tunneling in Double Quantum Dot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26622)**: Formulates photon-assisted tunneling in a double quantum dot using scattering theory, revealing coherent transport through polariton states and an Aharonov-Bohm effect even in side peaks.
- **[Challenging the $p$-type Paradigm: Intrinsic $n$-type Mobility in Antiferromagnetic Cr$_2$O$_3$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26269)**: Uses ab initio calculations to show Cr$_2$O$_3$ is intrinsically n-type, revising its role in transparent electronics and explaining commonly observed p-type behavior as extrinsic.
- **[Quasiclassical theory of nonlinear response in d-wave superconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26909)**: Finds that, contrary to s-wave, clean d-wave superconductors show no Eliashberg enhancement effect, and that third-harmonic generation is dominated by charge-density fluctuations, not Higgs modes.
- **[Spin-orbit coupling driven topological superconductivity in twisted bilayer graphene-WSe$_2$ heterostructures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27013)**: Demonstrates that proximity-induced spin-orbit coupling in tBLG/WSe2 heterostructures can drive topological superconductivity with Chern numbers, with phase boundaries matching direct band gap closings.
- **[Coexistence of static order and spin dynamics in an S = 5/2 frustrated triangular antiferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26921)**: Presents evidence that MnSnB$_2$O$_6$ orders magnetically but retains persistent spin dynamics and unconventional power-law specific heat, indicating exotic low-energy excitations.
- **[Evidence of intertwined pair density and charge density wave orders in UTe2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.08688)**: Using STM, identifies new charge modulations in UTe2 that vanish near Tc, providing strong evidence for a pair density wave (PDW) order and its descendant CDW orders.
- **[Hidden-ordered Dirac fermions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27368)**: Proposes a new class of fermionic quasiparticles with linear dispersion arising from a hidden ordering, which pushes quantum phase transitions to stronger coupling and survives near quantum critical points.
- **[Quasiparticle tunnelling in two coupled chiral SYK model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.18298)**: Shows that coupling two chiral SYK models does not generate a mass gap, but instead yields massless collective modes and quasiparticle tunneling, distinct from the zero-dimensional case.

### Topological Phases & Phenomena
- **[Anomalous Hall viscosity of altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26239)**: Shows that the phonon Hall viscosity is a sensitive probe of altermagnetism, distinguishing it from ferromagnets and conventional antiferromagnets, and is tied to a strain-space Berry curvature monopole.
- **[Toric code made subsystem: a framework for topological subsystem codes using anticommuting quantum spin liquids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26226)**: Introduces a new framework for topological subsystem codes based on anticommuting quantum spin liquids, featuring extensive gauge qubits and local check operators.
- **[Quantum Hall effect in three-dimensional lattice induced by Wannier-Stark-Landau localization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26444)**: Demonstrates a 3D quantum Hall effect in a cubic lattice under parallel E and B fields, with quantized Hall conductance and topological chiral hinge modes.
- **[Topological phase transition driven by in-plane spin rotation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26548)**: Establishes a framework where a 60° in-plane magnetization rotation can reverse the Chern number in a kagome ferromagnet, offering a low-energy path for switching topological states.
- **[Floquet-Engineered Chern Insulator in two-dimensional $d_{x^2-y^2}$-Wave Altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26632)**: Shows that circularly polarized light induces quantum anomalous Hall phases in d-wave altermagnets, achieving high Chern numbers (up to ±3).
- **[Floquet Topological Phases and Anomalous Hall Signatures in Irradiated Two-dimensional $d_{xy}$-Wave Altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26667)**: Identifies light-induced topological phases in d-wave altermagnets with Chern numbers up to 4, characterized by anisotropic Dirac points and distinct Berry curvature distributions.
- **[Non-Hermitian Bloch Oscillations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26480)**: Generalizes Bloch oscillations to non-Hermitian lattices, predicting non-reciprocal, non-smooth oscillations and temporal Goos–Hänchen shifts under a dc force.
- **[Light-Induced Topological Phase Transitions and Anomalous Thermal Transport in d-Wave Altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20164)**: Demonstrates that linearly polarized light enables all-optical switching between topological phases in d-wave altermagnets, with quantized thermal Hall and Nernst responses.
- **[Noninvertible Kramers-Wannier duality symmetries for the discrete-time quantum Ising chain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.03947)**: Shows that the trotterized critical transverse field Ising model is integrable and reveals non-invertible Kramers-Wannier duality operators, mapping between different phases and Floquet evolution protocols.
- **[Valley Valves at Domain Walls in Symmetry-Broken Rhombohedral Graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14878)**: Shows that valley domain walls in rhombohedral graphene are impenetrable barriers, requiring intervalley interactions for transport, which is crucial for Josephson junctions in the superconducting phase.
