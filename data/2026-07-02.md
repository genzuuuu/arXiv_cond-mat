### Quantum Phases and Phase Transitions

- **[Dissipation splits the Mott transition in one dimension](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00086)**: Theory shows that dissipation fundamentally reshapes the 1D Mott transition, splitting it into two distinct critical phenomena and introducing a novel intermediate "dissipative phase".

- **[Phase distinction of Gibbs states without symmetry breaking: topological invariants of the 3D toric code](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00134)**: Introduces the "decoded Wilson-loop correlation" as a robust topological invariant for mixed states, which distinguishes topological phases at finite temperature even when all higher-form symmetries are broken.

- **[Quench of chiral superconductivity by quantum phase fluctuations in twisted cuprate bilayers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00630)**: Demonstrates that quantum phase fluctuations fundamentally quench chiral superconductivity in twisted cuprate bilayers, restricting it to a narrow parameter window and producing a first-order transition.

- **[Deconfined criticality between an antiferromagnetic insulator and a nodal d-wave superconductor: a quantum Monte Carlo study](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00762)**: Uses a parton representation to find evidence for a second-order deconfined quantum phase transition between a Néel insulator and a nodal d-wave superconductor on the square lattice.

- **[Continuous time crystals as a PT symmetric state and the emergence of critical exceptional points](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.09018)**: Shows that Lindbladian PT-symmetry generically produces persistent periodic oscillations (continuous time crystals) in a wide class of open quantum systems.

### Topological and Correlated Materials

- **[Topological Hall plateau in quasi-2D kagome magnet YMn₆Sn₆](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00150)**: Reports a topological Hall plateau driven by a disordered skyrmion phase in quasi-2D YMn₆Sn₆, arising from planar DMI and ferromagnetic exchange.

- **[Facet-selective ballistic supercurrent in a weak topological insulator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00683)**: Demonstrates facet-selective ballistic supercurrent in ZrTe₅ Josephson junctions, linking the supercurrent distribution to the underlying bulk Weyl topology.

- **[Observation of Flat Bands in Type-II Weyl Semimetal TaRhTe₄](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01186)**: Reports the observation of flat bands near the Fermi level in the bulk type-II Weyl semimetal TaRhTe₄, a rare occurrence in non-magnetic bulk systems.

- **[Kagome edge states under lattice termination, spin-orbit coupling, and magnetic order](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12223)**: Shows that edge states in the kagome lattice are highly sensitive to boundary geometry, with topological protection emerging from Kane-Mele spin-orbit coupling.

- **[Kinetic instability and superconductivity in Li₂AuH₆ and Li₂AgH₆ at ambient pressure](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12367)**: Finds that predicted ambient-pressure hydride superconductors are kinetically unstable, with H₂ partial dimerization leading to a revised superconducting Tc of ~22 K.

### Spintronics and Spin Phenomena

- **[An analog ac voltage amplifier based on a single straintronic magnetic tunnel junction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00104)**: Proposes and analyzes a voltage amplifier based on the linear conductance region of a straintronic magnetic tunnel junction, with gain tunable by an external power supply.

- **[Universal Scaling of the Spin Hall Effect](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00343)**: Derives a universal scaling theory for the spin Hall effect in Dirac materials, identifying superclean, moderately dirty, and dirty regimes with distinct scaling relations.

- **[Atom-selective spin-polarized transport in a charge-ordered altermagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00506)**: Shows that the charge-ordered altermagnet α-Fe₂PO₅ exhibits real-space spin selectivity, with electron and hole doping activating spin-polarized channels on distinct Fe ion sites.

- **[Magnon-polaron mediated spin Seebeck effect in altermagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00993)**: Predicts a pronounced directional anisotropy in the spin Seebeck effect of altermagnets, with magnetoelastic coupling providing a robust experimental signature.

- **[Modulation of anomalous Hall angle in a magnetic topological semimetal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00396)**: Reports modulation of the anomalous Hall angle up to ~25° in Co₃Sn₂S₂, achieving high Hall sensitivity for magnetic field detection.

- **[Geometry-Driven Magnetoelectric Coupling in Two-Dimensional Compensated Ferrimagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00997)**: Establishes a "geometry-driven" spin-ferroelectric coupling mechanism in bilayer breathing kagome lattices, enabling deterministic reversal of spin splitting via polarization switching.

### Superconductivity

- **[Resilient j=3/2 superconductivity in topological semimetal YPtBi](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00765)**: Shows that the superconducting Tc of YPtBi is remarkably robust against disorder and carrier density changes, suggesting phase stiffness governs Tc.

- **[Exact interlayer triplet-pairing eigenstates in the extended Hubbard model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01038)**: Constructs exact triplet-pairing eigenstates for the multi-layer extended Hubbard model, showing coexistence of singlet and triplet pairs.

- **[Breathing mode inducing dynamical pairing in Kagome materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01052)**: Demonstrates that the breathing mode in kagome materials can induce odd-frequency dynamical Cooper pairs, even from conventional s-wave superconductivity.

- **[Interaction-Split Edge Spectral Flow in an Integer Hubbard Thouless Pump](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.23769)**: Shows that local Hubbard correlations can split the edge spectral flow of an integer Thouless pump into separated charged and neutral spinful sectors.

### 2D Materials and Heterostructures

- **[Valley-dependent electron optics using quantum dots in bilayer graphene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00271)**: Proposes a platform based on electrostatically defined quantum dots in BLG for tunable valley-dependent electron optics, including valley splitters and filters.

- **[Bandwidth-Limited Critical Currents in Electrically Tunable Moiré Bands](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01056)**: Demonstrates that the critical current in bilayer graphene moiré superlattices is directly linked to the moiré miniband bandwidth.

- **[Substrate-dependent electrical transport in individual single-walled carbon nanotubes grown across SiO₂ and hexagonal boron nitride](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00985)**: Provides direct evidence that hBN enhances carrier transport in individual SWCNTs compared to SiO₂, using a within-tube comparison.

### Computational and Theoretical Methods

- **[Holographic Quantum Transformer: A Generalist Neuro-Symbolic Architecture for Solving Frustrated Systems via Generative Attention](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00398)**: Introduces the Holographic Quantum Transformer (HQT), a generative architecture that resolves non-local entanglement in frustrated quantum systems on large lattices.

- **[Weak-coupling tensor cross interpolation impurity solver for nonequilibrium dynamical mean-field theory](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00702)**: Implements a nonequilibrium impurity solver based on tensor cross interpolation, enabling efficient evaluation of integrals without stochastic sampling and mitigating the sign problem.

- **[Tensor Network Solvers for Ultra-large Tight-binding Hamiltonians: Algorithms and Applications](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00991)**: Presents a tensor-network methodology to solve tight-binding problems for systems with billions of sites, enabling studies of moiré heterostructures and quasicrystals.

- **[Graph-Native Reinforcement Learning Enables Traceable Scientific Hypothesis Generation through Conceptual Recombination](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00924)**: Develops a graph-native reasoning model fine-tuned with reinforcement learning to generate traceable scientific hypotheses for materials design.

- **[Universal Short-Imaginary-Time Quantum Critical Dynamics Near Boundaries](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01076)**: Develops a universal scaling theory for short-imaginary-time critical dynamics in quantum systems with boundaries, uncovering new dynamic critical exponents.

### Experimental Techniques and Materials Science

- **[The BiP-PRISM algorithm for fast and scalable core-loss STEM-EELS simulations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00756)**: Introduces the BiP-PRISM algorithm for fast, scalable core-loss STEM-EELS simulations, enabling full-resolution elemental mapping on consumer-grade GPUs.

- **[In vacuo synthesis of single-layer Ni₃(HITP)₂ on HOPG surface using metallo-organic precursor](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00614)**: Develops a method to grow clean single-layer c-MOFs on inert HOPG substrates, opening avenues for exploring their intrinsic quantum properties.

- **[Surface Platinum Alloying for Passivation of Oxide Interfaces on Superconducting Niobium Films](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00429)**: Demonstrates that a Pt alloy layer can chemically stabilize Nb surfaces, suppressing further oxidation and providing a pathway towards higher-coherence qubits.

- **[Imaging superconducting weak spots through vortex-assisted THz near-field photovoltage](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00914)**: Demonstrates the first application of THz near-field photovoltage nanoscopy to a superconductor, imaging nanoscopic weak spots at 300 nm resolution.

- **[Strongly frustrated 2D magnetism in a 3D hexagonal perovskite](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.00532)**: Reports a rare instance of strongly frustrated 2D magnetism in the 3D perovskite Ba₂La₂MnTe₂O₁₂, with a 120° AFM order within the plane and disorder along the c-axis.
