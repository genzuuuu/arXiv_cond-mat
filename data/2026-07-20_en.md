### Superconductivity & Quantum Computing

*   **[Fermion parity of an Andreev molecule probed by nonlocal Josephson effect](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15786):** Demonstrates detection of global fermion parity in a carbon nanotube-based Andreev molecule via nonlocal Josephson response, showing characteristic π-phase shifts.
*   **[First-Order Topological FFLO Transition and Superconducting Diode Sign Reversal in Altermagnetic Nanowires](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15720):** Theoretically predicts topological FFLO states in altermagnetic nanowires via a first-order transition, marked by a sharp sign-reversing superconducting diode effect.
*   **[Coherent driving of displacive Higgs fluctuations in superconductors](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16055):** Shows that intense THz laser pulses can coherently drive Higgs mode oscillations via a displacive mechanism in both s-wave and d-wave superconductors, validated by experiments on NbN.
*   **[Drosophila of phonon-mediated superconductivity: Full Eliashberg theory of the jellium model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15996):** Full numerical solution of Migdal-Eliashberg equations for jellium model yields critical temperatures below 1K, contrasting earlier claims of room-temperature superconductivity in hydrogen solids.
*   **[Phase-controlled quasi-bound states in the continuum and thermoelectric enhancement in Majorana-quantum-dot nanostructures](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15990):** Shows that phase control of Majorana zero modes in a quantum dot can convert BICs into quasi-BICs, enabling a highly enhanced thermoelectric figure of merit (ZT ~ 0.75).
*   **[Topology meets superconductivity in a one-dimensional t-J model of magnetic atoms](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.03387):** Proposes a setup with ultracold magnetic atoms in an optical lattice to realize a topological triplet superconductor, where topology and superconductivity coexist.
*   **[Thermodynamic Constraints on Perfect Equilibrium Superconducting Diodes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21384):** Provides a general thermodynamic argument showing that a perfect equilibrium superconducting diode (100% efficiency) is forbidden by continuity of free energy, explaining the modest efficiencies of most platforms.
*   **[Qubit encodings in the p-orbital-valley spectrum for enhanced coherence and tunable two-qubit interaction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15993):** Proposes a new qubit encoding in Si using p-orbital and valley states, estimating a dephasing time T2* ~ 10 μs and a tunable two-qubit interaction up to ~1 GHz.
*   **[High-fidelity two-qubit gates with transmon qubits using bipolar flux pulses and tunable couplers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.04965):** Demonstrates a two-qubit gate scheme with peak fidelity of 99.5% using transmon qubits and a tunable coupler, with potential for errors below 1e-3.
*   **[Coloumb blockade in microscopic material defects as a source of decoherence and noise in solid-state quantum circuits](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15252):** Identifies Coulomb blockade in ubiquitous metallic grains as a widespread decoherence mechanism in superconducting circuits, potentially as damaging as two-level system defects.
*   **[Surface-Code Hardware Hamiltonian](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.06201):** Presents a scalable framework to model many-body interactions in surface code QPUs, identifying regimes of computational stability and error domination based on residual qubit-qubit crosstalk.
*   **[Entanglement dynamics in minimal Kitaev chains](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.17586):** Studies the dynamics of entanglement in minimal Kitaev chains, finding that maximally entangled states can emerge and are controllable by the interplay of pairing potential and onsite energy.
*   **[Quantum matrix-element estimators for spin-coupled generalized valence bond wavefunctions (SCGVB)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.12045):** Introduces an ancilla-free method to evaluate matrix elements for strongly correlated wavefunctions using shallow quantum circuits, validated on H4 and C2.

### Topological & Correlated Matter

*   **[Field-induced first order transitions and phase coexistence in the Kitaev quantum spin liquid candidate BaCo2(AsO4)2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15346):** Neutron scattering shows field-induced UUD-to-FM transition is first-order with phase coexistence. Results do not support a QSL near critical field but are explained by bound spin-flip pairs.
*   **[Spontaneous Nanopatterning and Strain Relaxation in SiGe Layers Grown by Oxidative Solid Phase Epitaxy](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15332):** Reports spontaneous nanopatterning of Ge concentration in SiGe layers during oxidative SPE, linked to a hexagonal dislocation network. The pattern spacing is a non-destructive probe of local strain.
*   **[Nanoscale stray fields from micromagnets for optimal spin qubit architecture](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15333):** Micromagnetic simulations of CoFe micromagnets, informed by NV-center magnetometry, predict strong antisymmetric stray fields for spin qubit control, leading to high spin-photon coupling.
*   **[Symmetry-Enforced Dirac Fermions and Structural Metastability in Pentagonal Monolayers of Transition-Metal Ditellurides](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15580):** First-principles calculations show pentagonal XTe2 monolayers are semiconductors with symmetry-enforced 2D Dirac states, emerging from Te-Te dimerization, in contrast to their semimetallic hexagonal phase.
*   **[Dynamical Polarization from Hidden Spin and Orbital Textures in p-Wave Magnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15353):** Shows that optical driving of a p-wave magnet unveils a hidden, resonantly enhanced ac spin polarization and a rectified dc orbital polarization, invisible to period-averaged treatments.
*   **[Altermagnetism without a long-range order](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15954):** Theoretically proposes the concept of an "altermagnetic liquid" where altermagnetic signatures (splitting, nodal lines) exist without long-range magnetic order, relevant for low-dimensional systems.
*   **[Emergent Interfacial Magnetism in Epitaxial RuO2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16093):** First-principles calculations show that magnetism in epitaxial RuO2 can emerge as an interfacial boundary phase at TiO2/RuO2 interfaces, stabilized by electronic reconstruction.
*   **[Large spin splitting at ferromagnetic surfaces of bulk antiferromagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.20178):** Using DFT, predicts large spin splitting (up to 1 eV) on specific surfaces of bulk antiferromagnets like Cr2O3 and FeF2, enabling functional spin splitting in a wider class of materials.
*   **[Magnons reveal topology and dynamics of a skyrmion crystal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16023):** Experiments using a graphene junction reveal that the geometry of a skyrmion Wigner crystal imprints on magnon transport, providing a new probe of topological spin textures.
*   **[Perturbation in the two-dimensional disordered Heisenberg model via Foundation Neural-Network Quantum States](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.05073):** Using advanced neural network quantum states, finds evidence for a stable quantum spin-glass phase in the 2D disordered Heisenberg model.
*   **[Chirality Cannot Be Ferroic in Enantiomorphic Space-Groups](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22501):** Formal group theory proof that structural chirality cannot be a primary ferroic order parameter because transitions to enantiomorphic space groups cannot be driven by a zone-center instability.
*   **[Large CISS Polarization from the Hierarchy Between Transport and Geometrical Spin Currents](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30670):** Proposes a mechanism for large CISS polarization in DNA, suggesting transport current is weak leakage from much larger intrinsic geometrical spin currents.

### Frustrated & Spin Systems

*   **[Thermal Order by Disorder in Resonating-Valence Bond States on the Checkerboard Lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15813):** Derives a local spin model with an RVB ground state and shows thermal decoherence can lead to "order by disorder," resulting in quasi-long-range order at finite temperature.
*   **[How Topology Shapes the Phase Behavior of Polyelectrolytes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15703):** Theory shows compact chain topologies (stars, dendrimers) have a greater propensity for coacervation, and differences in topology alone can drive multiphase separation.
*   **[Quantum Fisher information of magnetic quantum phase transition on Kondo lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16150):** Investigates multipartite entanglement across a Kondo lattice QPT using QFI, proposing experimental tests via inelastic neutron scattering to witness heavy quasiparticle destruction.
*   **[Thermal Order by Disorder in Resonating-Valence Bond States on the Checkerboard Lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15813):** Derives a local spin-1/2 Hamiltonian on the checkerboard lattice with an RVB ground state, where thermal decoherence suppresses quantum interference, leading to quasi-long-range order.
*   **[Absence of Spin-Glass Order on Migdal--Kadanoff Hierarchical Lattices near Three Dimensions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15676):** Rigorous proof of absence of spin-glass order in a class of hierarchical lattices even with dimensions arbitrarily close to 3, challenging prior numerical estimates.
*   **[Deconfined quantum critical point in a dissipative spin-1/2 chain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16039):** Show dissipation in a spin chain can stabilize antiferromagnetic order and realize a deconfined quantum critical point with emergent O(4) symmetry.
*   **[Existence of a Phase Transition in the 1D Ising Spin Glass Model with Long-Range Interactions on the Nishimori Line](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.07130):** Rigorously proves the existence of a finite-temperature phase transition in the 1D long-range Ising spin glass on the Nishimori line for interactions decaying as 1/r^α with 1 < α < 3/2.

*   **[A Flickering Resonance Degeneracy on Entropy-Ruled Charge Transport in Biological Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.15785):** Proposes a theory for charge transport in biological systems where dynamic disorder ("flickering resonance") leads to entropy-ruled mobility that can transform into band-like transport.
