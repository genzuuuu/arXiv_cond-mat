### Superconductivity: Mechanisms & New Materials

- **Pairing from Fractionalized & Flat Bands:** A theory describes superconductivity in a "fractionalized Fermi liquid" where doped electrons form bound states that interact via a dipolar potential, leading to \(d_{x^2-y^2}\) pairing [4]. Separately, a geometry-driven Kohn-Luttinger mechanism is shown to create strong pairing in flat Chern bands, producing extended-s, chiral p-wave, and f-wave superconductivity without relying on spin fluctuations [10].
- **Strain & Pressure Tuning:** In the nickelate \(\text{La}_3\text{Ni}_2\text{O}_7\), tensile strain is predicted to induce an \(s^{\pm}\)-wave pairing state, while compression suppresses superconductivity, offering a pathway to engineer it at ambient pressure [16]. The "transdimensional" anomalous Hall effect in rhombohedral graphite is attributed to a Stoner instability in a flat band, with superconductivity and valley polarization linked by a single order parameter [33].
- **Strongly Correlated Superconductors:** A new theory for two-dimensional systems shows that coupling to ferroelectric quantum critical fluctuations via an interband "Stark" mechanism can strongly enhance the superconducting critical temperature compared to conventional BCS expectations [81]. A cavity was used to enhance the superfluid stiffness and raise the onset temperature of superconductivity in an underdoped cuprate, by engineering its electromagnetic environment [72].
- **Diode & Current-Phase Effects:** Supercurrent rectification (diode effect) with efficiency >20% is observed in graphene Josephson junctions, attributed to inherent asymmetric disorder [42]. A theoretical proposal using ac-flux-driven SQUIDs allows for spectroscopy of the current-phase relation, distinguishing harmonics like \(\sin 2\varphi\) [46].

### Topological & Quantum Matter

- **Altermagnets & Nonlinear Optics:** A nonlinear optical probe (second-harmonic generation) is used to directly detect the ferroic-octupolar order parameter in the altermagnet CoF\(_2\), a key challenge in the field [54]. In d-wave altermagnets, a spin-selective photocurrent is predicted as the leading third-order nonlinear response [133].
- **Fragile & Higher-Order Topology:** A robust signature of fragile topology is identified: it necessarily produces bulk Dirac cones in two-dimensional materials with time-reversal and twofold rotation symmetry, verified in five materials via DFT [58]. A scalable and reconfigurable higher-order topological metasurface operating at GHz frequencies was experimentally demonstrated using split-ring resonators [55]. Bilayer \(\text{ZrTe}_5\) is shown to host a "double quantum spin Hall phase" with two pairs of helical edge states, protected by a spin Chern number [75].
- **Majorana Modes:** A technique using spin-polarized scanning tunneling spectroscopy distinguishes topological Majorana zero modes from trivial Yu-Shiba-Rusinov states in Fe(Te,Se) [30]. A "poor man's" Majorana scheme shows that coupled magnetic adatom dimers on a superconductor can produce a large Josephson diode effect [105].

### 2D Materials & Heterostructures

- **Fermi Polarons & Moiré Excitons:** A room-temperature platform based on a plasmonic metasurface allows tuning and probing of Fermi polarons in few-layer WS\(_2\), revealing a thickness-dependent spectral weight transfer [17]. Strongly coupled "trion polaritons" in a MoSe\(_2\)/WS\(_2\) heterobilayer microcavity exhibit highly nonlinear behavior, including high-velocity hot polaritons with long diffusion lengths [70].
- **Ferroelectricity & Magnetism:** A new class of "superlubric sliding ferroelectrics" is introduced, where polarization arises from a hybrid electronic-ionic mechanism coupling sliding and buckling of a spacer layer, leading to multi-step switching [31]. In monolayer CrC\(_2\)N\(_4\), robust valley contrasting spin splitting and a strain-tunable band gap are predicted for valleytronic applications [21]. Monolayer CrTe\(_2\) is shown to host room-temperature ferromagnetism and antiferromagnetism, controllable via strain and charge doping, with strong spin-lattice coupling [76].
- **Twist & Disorder Effects:** Theoretical work shows that twist-angle inhomogeneity in moiré systems masks the BKT transition and can effectively obscure the difference between nodal and gapped order parameters in the superfluid stiffness [9]. A new operator-ordering correction in twisted bilayer graphene is predicted to cause a distinct splitting of Van Hove peaks visible in STM [24].

### Frustrated Magnetism & Quantum Spin Liquids

- **Exotic Magnetism:** A new frustrated rare-earth triangular lattice antiferromagnet, K\(_3\)NdTe\(_2\)O\(_9\), is shown to host \(J_{\rm eff}=1/2\) moments with persistent spin dynamics and no long-range order down to 50 mK, suggesting a quantum spin liquid candidate [5]. An extended non-magnetic phase is identified in the spin-1/2 Heisenberg model on the ruby-to-maple-leaf lattice, a regime of strong quantum paramagnetism [104].
- **Spin Dynamics & Phases:** The antiferromagnetic transition in the frustrated bixbyite \(\beta\)-Fe\(_2\)O\(_3\) is characterized, revealing a noncollinear structure with a high frustration index [53]. Antisite disorder in double perovskites is used to tune the exchange-bias effect, achieving high values via the creation of competing ferromagnetic and glassy interfaces [12].

### Quantum Simulation & Computation

- **Hamiltonian Engineering:** A new framework for fermionic Hamiltonian engineering uses sequences of local fermionic unitaries to realize complex target Hamiltonians, demonstrated by simulating the Harper-Hofstadter model on a 1088-mode lattice [86].
- **Qubit & Quantum Error Correction:** A protocol using stroboscopic Rydberg dressing is proposed to create squeezed and non-classical motional states in neutral atom arrays, potentially reducing decoherence and boosting gate fidelities [99]. The statistical properties of logical states after measurement ("projected logical ensembles") in surface codes are linked to random matrix theory, revealing connections to universal physics in chaotic quantum dots [85].
- **Generative AI for Science:** The score-matching objective in diffusion models is shown to be directly proportional to a time-asymmetry entropy production, providing a thermodynamic basis for their superior sampling diversity [14]. A new "Paper-to-Program" workflow uses LLMs to convert quantum many-body papers into running code by first explicitly externalizing tacit knowledge [161].
