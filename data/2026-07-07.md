### Superconductivity & Topological Superconductivity
This area features several significant developments, including a new explanation for the origin of pairing from repulsive interactions in cuprates, the proposal of a gate-controlled parity-protected qubit using altermagnetic Josephson junctions, and the experimental identification of a multi-orbital theory for the debated pairing symmetry in Nb-Re. Key findings include a novel mechanism for the nonlinear anomalous Hall effect in Dirac systems, the observation of a pseudo-superconducting diode effect in ferroelectric junctions, and the theoretical demonstration of a pseudo-superconducting diode effect via vortex-number control.

*   **New mechanism for pairing from repulsion in cuprates:** A formalism is proposed where dynamical screening of the repulsive Coulomb interaction, arising from superexchange dynamics of virtual holes in oxygen orbitals, leads to an effective attraction for Cooper pairs. This model yields a temperature-doping phase diagram in semi-quantitative agreement with cuprate observations. [When Repulsion Creates Pairing: A New Perspective on Unconventional Superconductivity](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03022)
*   **Gate-controlled parity-protected qubit:** A theoretical proposal for a superconducting qubit based on a superconductor-altermagnet-superconductor Josephson junction. The altermagnet's spin-dependent properties enable a gate-tunable 0-π transition, leading to a qubit with long coherence times and fully electrical control, eliminating the need for magnetic flux biasing. [Full Gate-Voltage Control of a Parity-Protected Superconducting Qubit with an Altermagnetic Josephson Junction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04097)
*   **Mixed-parity pairing in a weakly-correlated metal:** Using orientation-resolved scanning tunnelling spectroscopy, a mixed-parity superconducting state combining a nodal spin-singlet with a substantial spin-triplet component was identified in the noncentrosymmetric superconductor Nb₁₈Re₈₂. This demonstrates that antisymmetric spin-orbit coupling alone can foster triplet pairing, resolving the debated pairing symmetry of this material. [Nodal superconductivity with spin-triplet component in a noncentrosymmetric weakly-correlated metal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.04742)
*   **Pseudo-superconducting diode effect:** A magnetic-field-free pseudo-superconducting diode effect is proposed in ferroelectric Josephson junctions. The effect arises from the coupling between supercurrent and ferroelectric polarization dynamics, leading to asymmetric critical currents that are highly tunable. [Pseudo-superconducting-diode effect in ferroelectric Josephson junctions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04398)
*   **Vortex-number-controlled Josephson diode polarity:** It is theoretically shown that the polarity of the Josephson diode effect in Corbino junctions can be controlled by the vortex number. This mechanism arises from the vortex selectively filtering spatial Fourier harmonics of local inhomogeneity, providing a generic geometric mechanism for switching diode polarity. [Vortex-Number-Controlled Josephson Diode Polarity in Corbino Junctions](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04104)
*   **Josephson effect in spin-triplet/altermagnet junctions:** A theoretical study proposes using an altermagnetic Josephson junction to detect the intrinsic d-vector in spin-triplet superconductors. The current-phase relationship strongly depends on the d-vector direction, offering a scheme free from the negative influence of magnetic fields. [Josephson effects in the spin-triplet superconductor/altermagnet/spin-triplet superconductor junctions: the detection of the intrinsic d-vector](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03685)
*   **Theory of spinful relativistic superfluids:** An effective field theory is constructed for relativistic superfluids with nonzero angular momentum density, deriving from a single quantized term the relativistic Mermin-Ho relation and anomalous Hall effects. [Theory of Spinful Relativistic Superfluids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04408)
*   **Phase diagram of CeRh₂As₂:** The experimental phase diagram of the superconductor CeRh₂As₂ is reconciled with the parity-switch scenario by considering the combined effects of nonsymmorphic band structure and coexisting antiferromagnetic order, explaining the anomalous initial slope of the high-field superconducting phase. [H-T Phase diagram of CeRh₂As₂: Refinement of the parity-switch scenario](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04928)
*   **Effect of isovalent substitution in PrFeAs(O,F):** Superconductivity in PrFeAs(O,F) is tuned by substituting Sb at the As site. Moderate Sb doping enhances vortex pinning, but a crossover to a disorder-dominated regime is observed at higher Sb concentrations. [Tuning Superconductivity by Isovalent Antimony Substitution in PrFeAs(O,F)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03712)
*   **Correlated-electron theory of triplet-triplet multiexciton states in polypentacene:** A correlated-electron study shows that triplet-triplet multiexciton states in polypentacene are quantum superpositions of configurations with all intertriplet separations, explaining the lack of intramolecular triplet diffusion in these systems. [Correlated-Electron Theory of Triplet-Triplet Multiexciton States in Polypentacene](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03706)
*   **Many-body chaos in excitonic spectra:** Realistic excitonic many-body Hamiltonians from first-principles GW-BSE calculations are shown to exhibit quantum chaos governed by random-matrix universality. This is demonstrated for a WS₂-graphene heterostructure, with disorder driving a crossover to complete chaotic dynamics. [Many-body quantum chaos in excitonic spectra from first principles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03294)
*   **Possible FFLO pairing in altermagnets:** Solving the two-electron problem on a square lattice with d-wave altermagnetism shows that the spin-splitting favors bound pairs with nonzero center-of-mass momentum, suggesting a route to Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov superconductivity in altermagnets. [Finite-momentum bound pairs of two electrons in an altermagnetic metal](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.12905)

### Topological Phases & Quantum Materials
This group highlights new discoveries in topological phases, including a new class of chiral charge density waves with winding phase topology, a scheme for quantum geometric Friedel oscillations in flat-band metals, and the experimental observation of a robust nonlinear Hall effect in polycrystalline magnetic multilayers. Notable theoretical advances include the demonstration of a doubly-commensurate moiré reconstruction in twisted bilayer graphene on hBN and the proposal of a hybrid-order nonlinear topological phase.

*   **Winding charge density waves:** A novel class of chiral charge density waves is proposed, exhibiting macroscopic chirality due to an integer azimuthal phase winding. This "winding CDW" links the geometry of chiral structures to the phase topology of electronic orders. [Winding charge density wave: intertwining of structural chirality and phase topology of electronic order](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03102)
*   **Quantum geometric Friedel oscillations:** A new type of Friedel oscillation is predicted in metals with a (nearly) flat band at the Fermi energy. The oscillation period is set by the momentum-space separation of quantum metric "hot spots," and the decay length is temperature-independent due to "quantum metric protection," offering a powerful way to detect the quantum metric. [Quantum Geometric Friedel Oscillations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04654)
*   **Nonlinear Hall effect in polycrystalline multilayers:** A robust nonlinear Hall effect is observed in polycrystalline magnetic multilayers from 2 K to room temperature. A scaling analysis identifies the effect as being driven by an intrinsic Berry curvature dipole, extending the NLHE to industry-compatible thin-film spintronics. [Observation of Non-linear hall effect in Polycrystalline magnetic multilayes](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04754)
*   **Hybrid-order nonlinear topological phases:** An auxiliary-system formalism is developed to classify topology in nonlinear-eigenvalue systems. This leads to the prediction of hybrid-order topological phases in 2D and 3D, with gapless boundary modes and localized corner/hinge states. [Hybrid-order nonlinear topological phases](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03678)
*   **Moiré-moiré reconstruction in tBG/h-BN:** Conductive atomic force microscopy reveals that twist-configured moiré-moiré reconstruction in twisted bilayer graphene on h-BN gives rise to diverse commensurate double-moiré phases. This establishes a framework for engineering structural and electronic order in multilayer moiré materials. [Twist-configured moire-moire reconstruction governs diverse commensurate double-moire phases in twisted bilayer graphene on h-BN](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02822)
*   **Nonreciprocal transverse currents in Rashba junctions:** A theoretical study shows that a junction between a normal metal and a Rashba metal with an out-of-plane Zeeman field exhibits a finite, nonreciprocal transverse conductivity, offering a mechanism for transverse charge transport without in-plane fields or ferromagnetic contacts. [Nonreciprocal transverse currents in Rashba metal junctions under out-of-plane Zeeman fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27642)
*   **Coexisting CDW and superconductivity in quantizing magnetic fields:** In rhombohedral hexalayer graphene, a superconducting phase is found to be stabilized by a perpendicular magnetic field and persists deep within the quantum Hall regime, coexisting with a CDW order that mixes many Landau levels. [Coexisting Charge Density Wave and Superconducting Order in Quantizing Magnetic Fields](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05039)
*   **Topological electronic states in Ru-based ternary pnictides:** First-principles calculations predict topological semimetallic behavior and phonon-mediated superconductivity in ZrRuAs and HfRuP, with estimated Tc values around 9 K. [Topological Electronic States and Phonon Mediated Superconductivity in Ru Based Ternary Pnictides: ZrRuAs and HfRuP](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04651)
*   **Nodal line semimetal entanglement structure:** Multipartite entanglement is shown to serve as a robust probe for topological phase transitions in strongly coupled holographic nodal line semimetals. The scaling exponents of multipartite measures act as non-local order parameters. [Multipartite entanglement characterizing topological phase transitions in holographic nodal line semimetals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01545)
*   **Chiral edge state spin-momentum locking:** Stokes polarimetry reveals spin-momentum locking for polariton edge states in a honeycomb lattice, where the polarization handedness is locked to the propagation direction, offering a route to spin-controlled unidirectional propagation. [Spin-momentum locking of polariton edge states in honeycomb lattices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03961)
*   **Topological triplons in a kagome valence-bond solid:** A study shows that triplon excitations in the pinwheel valence-bond solid phase on a deformed kagome lattice can acquire nontrivial Chern numbers in the presence of Dzyaloshinskii-Moriya interactions and a magnetic field, leading to a tunable thermal Hall effect. [Topological Triplons in the Pinwheel Valence Bond Solid on the Kagome Lattice](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.09823)

### Altermagnetism & Spintronics
A major focus is on the new field of altermagnetism, with significant theoretical and experimental results. These include the discovery of chiral phonons coupled to altermagnetic spin splitting in CrSb and MnTe, the demonstration of a surface-induced spin-splitter effect in g-wave altermagnets, and the experimental observation of 2D altermagnetism in epitaxial CrSb ultrathin films.

*   **Chiral phonons in altermagnets:** First-principles calculations show that the prototypical altermagnets CrSb and MnTe host locally chiral phonon modes that couple to their momentum-dependent spin-split electronic bands, establishing altermagnets as a platform for chiral phononics and spin-selective lattice control. [Chiral Phonons Coupled to Spin-Split Bands in Altermagnetic CrSb and MnTe](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04802)
*   **Surface-induced spin-splitter effect in g-wave altermagnets:** It is shown that surfaces of a g-wave altermagnet can realize d-wave altermagnetism and a finite spin-splitter effect, which is absent in the bulk. This provides a thin-film engineering strategy for functionalizing non-d-wave altermagnets. [Functionalization of g-wave altermagnets: spin-splitter effect enabled by surfaces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04970)
*   **Two-dimensional altermagnetism in CrSb ultrathin films:** Epitaxial CrSb films on Bi₂Te₃ are shown to retain altermagnetic electronic characteristics down to a thickness of ~1.05 nm, demonstrating the robustness of altermagnetism in the 2D limit. [Two-Dimensional Altermagnetism in Epitaxial CrSb Ultrathin Films](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.12344)
*   **Surface functionalization for 2D altermagnetism:** First-principles calculations propose that surface functionalization (e.g., hydrogenation, oxidation) can transform known 2D antiferromagnets into altermagnets, with monolayer FeSe converted into a d-wave altermagnet showing giant tunnel magnetoresistance. [Surface Functionalization Enables Two-Dimensional Altermagnetism and Giant Tunnel Magnetoresistance](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03908)
*   **Anti-points and anomalous Hall effect in altermagnets:** A mechanism for the intrinsic anomalous Hall effect in 4f antiferromagnetic metals is identified. The effect originates from nonlocal Kondo exchange coupling and spin-dependent intersite hopping, not from a local Zeeman field. [Nonlocal Kondo-exchange-driven intrinsic anomalous Hall effect in localized-4f antiferromagnetic metals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04805)
*   **Correlation-driven altermagnetism in frustrated lattices:** Exact diagonalization studies of the Hubbard model on frustrated lattice clusters reveal that correlation-driven altermagnetism requires geometric frustration and ground-state degeneracy, emerging when a mobile charge carrier is introduced. [Emergence of correlation-driven altermagnetism in Hubbard model on geometrically frustrated lattice-clusters](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.00536)
*   **Spin-orbit torque-driven antiferromagnetic oscillator:** A spin-orbit torque-driven antiferromagnetic oscillator is demonstrated using a nanoconstriction in a synthetic antiferromagnet. This platform reproduces complex nonlinear dynamics relevant for signal processing. [Spin-orbit torque-driven synthetic antiferromagnetic oscillator](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03708)

### Non-Equilibrium & Quantum Dynamics
This section covers a wide range of non-equilibrium phenomena, from the breakdown of the Ising universality class by non-equilibrium coupling to new theoretical frameworks for quantum Mpemba effects, monitored dynamics, and non-Hermitian topology. Experimental results include a direct observation of the Mermin-Ho relation and the demonstration of nonreciprocal optical domain switching in antiferromagnets.

*   **Non-equilibrium breakdown of Ising universality:** A new universality class is discovered when an Ising order parameter is coupled nonreciprocally to a conserved diffusive density. The critical exponents differ from Ising, and fluctuation-dissipation relations are violated, providing a measurable hallmark. [Non-equilibrium coupling to a diffusing density breaks Ising universality](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02661)
*   **Field theory of the Brownian Ising Model:** A complete field-theoretical renormalization-group analysis shows that the Brownian Ising Model flows to a non-equilibrium fixed point distinct from Ising, with an exact scaling relation linking exponents. [Non-equilibrium phase transition in the Brownian Ising Model: field theory, renormalization group, and exact results](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02667)
*   **Quantum tunneling Mpemba effect:** The quantum tunneling Mpemba effect is investigated in a symmetric double-well potential. A universal topological property is proven to govern the non-monotonic behavior of a spectral coefficient, bridging classical boundary-driven dissipation with open quantum dynamics. [Quantum tunneling Mpemba effect](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03845)
*   **Spectral-topology-induced criticality in non-Hermitian metals:** A new framework connects non-Hermitian band topology to many-body criticality using Morse theory. It shows that gain-and-loss selected steady states generate an emergent imaginary Fermi surface with scale-invariant modes. [Spectral-topology-induced criticality in non-Hermitian fermionic metals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05190)
*   **Non-Hermitian skyrmion topological splitting:** The topological charge of a non-Hermitian skyrmion is shown to split at a real-space exceptional ring. The topology from the right eigenstate is homotopy-protected, while the biorthogonal charge breaks down. [Topological charge of a non-Hermitian skyrmion splits at a real-space exceptional ring](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27810)
*   **Optical switching of antiferromagnetic domains:** Deterministic switching of antiferromagnetic domains in LiFePO₄ is demonstrated using mid-infrared light. The switching is driven by the material's intrinsic nonreciprocity through optically generated heat currents, working over a broad wavelength range. [Optical switching of antiferromagnetic domains by nonreciprocal heat current](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04975)
*   **Measurement-induced spatially nonuniform fluctuations:** In a continuously monitored quasiperiodic free-fermion chain, fluctuations of the local particle number exhibit a nonuniform spatial pattern, with a crossover from a long-range structure-dominated regime to a local-environment-dominated regime as measurement strength increases. [Measurement-induced spatially nonuniform fluctuations of the local particle number and their crossover in a quasiperiodic free-fermion chain](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03799)
*   **Nucleation and time-reversal symmetry breaking in nonconserved field theories:** A full account of nonequilibrium nucleation theory is provided for non-equilibrium field theories with non-conserved order parameter, showing that the quasipotential barrier differs from the one found assuming the instanton is the time-reversal of the relaxation dynamics. [Nucleation and time-reversal symmetry breaking in nonconserved scalar field theories](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05194)
*   **Spontaneous flows and interfacial instabilities in living active matter:** Oxygen gradients in dense suspensions of *Euglena gracilis* trigger an active interfacial instability, forming a rotating corona of protrusions. A model shows that oxygen taxis creates the interface, while dipolar active stresses drive the deformation. [Spontaneous flows and interfacial instabilities in oxygen-sensitive living active matter](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31355)
*   **Critical behavior of the driven Curie-Weiss model:** The phase diagram of a Curie-Weiss magnet in a time-periodic field is completed, showing a coexistence of paramagnetic and ferromagnetic phases and a dynamical criticality with differing specific heat exponents. [Critical behavior of the driven Curie-Weiss model](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05130)

*   **Strain- and potential-controlled tunneling in monolayer MoS₂:** A theoretical study shows that strain and a scalar potential provide orthogonal knobs for controlling spin and valley polarization in monolayer MoS₂, enabling electrostatic spin inversion and tunable transport. *   **Chalcogen vacancy effects in TMDC monolayers:** First-principles calculations show that chalcogen vacancies in MX₂ monolayers induce electronic states that reduce the band gap, facilitate charge separation, and enhance photocatalytic capabilities for water splitting and CO₂ reduction. ### Quantum Magnetism & Frustrated Systems
Theoretical and computational work on frustrated magnetism is rich. Highlights include the prediction of a quantum spin liquid from constraint-driven disorder, the demonstration of quantum destabilization of skyrmions in the extreme quantum limit, and a deep investigation into what makes 3D quantum spin liquids possible.

*   **What makes 3D quantum spin liquids possible?** A perspective argues that three-dimensional quantum spin liquids can be stable when local constraints suppress premature order selection, coherent tunneling connects the constrained manifold, and gauge defect topology permits deconfinement. In 3D, regimes unstable in lower dimensions can become stable. [What Makes Three-Dimensional Quantum Spin Liquids Possible?](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03352)
*   **Quantum destabilization of skyrmions:** Using a neural network quantum states method, it is shown that skyrmion stability is severely degraded in the extreme quantum limit (s=1/2) of a frustrated Heisenberg model. The destabilization is driven by quantum longitudinal fluctuations, requiring a spin moment of at least s=3/2 for stable atomic-scale skyrmions. [Quantum Destabilization of Skyrmions in Centrosymmetric Frustrated Magnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03378)
*   **Magnetic ordering of VI₃:** Atomistic spin-dynamics simulations show that the coexistence of two vanadium types with vastly different magnetic anisotropies in VI₃ explains its unusual thickness-dependent Curie temperature. The ordering temperature is tunable by controlling the relative occupation of these configurations. [Magnetic ordering of a van der Waals material combining vastly different magnetic anisotropies](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02759)
*   **Quantum magnetism of the spin-1 kagome lattice:** Large-scale Lanczos diagonalization of the spin-1 kagome Heisenberg antiferromagnet reveals magnetization plateaus at 0, 1/3, 7/9, and 8/9 of saturation. The m=0 plateau is a valence-bond crystal, while high-field plateaus are magnon crystals. [Quantum magnetism of the spin-1 kagome-lattice antiferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03086)
*   **Near itinerancy and slow singlet formation in NaRuO₂:** Resonant inelastic X-ray scattering and other experiments on NaRuO₂ reveal a nearly itinerant system with strong fluctuations that destabilize long-range magnetic order, favoring a quantum disordered ground state described by a random singlet model. [Near itinerancy and slow singlet formation in the triangular lattice NaRuO2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05287)
*   **Phase-controlled interaction between chiral bobbers and skyrmion strings:** Micromagnetic simulations show that the interaction between a chiral bobber and a skyrmion string in the conical phase is intrinsically dependent on a surface phase factor, producing repulsive, attractive, and bonding-like regimes. [Phase-Factor-Controlled Interaction and Bonding between a Chiral Bobber and a Skyrmion String in the Conical Phase](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04266)
*   **Field-induced commensurate-incommensurate transitions in helical antiferromagnets:** An analytical theory describes how an in-plane magnetic field can distort a magnetic spiral and produce transitions between incommensurate and commensurate phases. The theory is applied to the triangular-lattice compound RbFe(MoO₄)₂. [Field-induced transitions from incommensurate to commensurate phases in helical antiferromagnets](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.22343)

### Computational & Theoretical Methods

The method resolves the helicity of the Dirac cone in a topological insulator and is a powerful new tool for quantum materials research. *   **Fixed-point tensor network for compactified boson CFT:** Fixed-point tensors are constructed for the 2D compactified boson conformal field theory at a generic radius. The tensors reproduce the closed-string spectrum and generate stable RG flows along the c=1 moduli space. *   **Generalized dynamical duality for 1D quantum particles:** A theorem is proven showing that 1D systems with arbitrary statistics (bosons, fermions, anyons) asymptotically approach the same momentum distribution after a long-time expansion, provided they have the same scattering length. [Generalized Dynamical Duality of Quantum Particles in One Dimension](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.25056)
*   **Local Order Average-Atom interatomic potentials:** A new method (LOAA) extends the effective Average-Atom method for random alloys to account for local ordering (short-range order) effects. [Local Order Average-Atom Interatomic Potentials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.06459)
*   **NanoBTE: Fast iterative solution of the phonon BTE:** A deterministic finite-volume solver for the phonon Boltzmann transport equation is presented. It supports complex geometries, multiple boundary conditions, and efficient GPU acceleration, enabling multiscale simulations of nanoscale heat transport. [NanoBTE: Fast Iterative Solution of the Phonon Boltzmann Transport Equation for Nanoscale Heat Transport](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03226)
*   **Holographic learning from fermionic spectra:** A data-driven framework based on Neural ODEs is developed to learn the bulk metric functions of a black hole from boundary fermionic spectral functions. [Holographic Learning from Fermionic Spectra: Application to Strange Metal Phenomenology](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02861)
*   **VASP Plugins: Linking VASP with Python:** A new Python plugin infrastructure for the VASP density functional theory code is presented, using pybind11 to expose VASP data as NumPy arrays. This enables rapid prototyping of novel algorithms, demonstrated with structure relaxation and implicit solvent models. [VASP Plugins: Linking the Vienna ab-initio Simulation Package with Python](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04622)
*   **Distributional framework for generative modeling of molecular crystals:** A new framework, MXtalGFlow, is proposed for sampling a thermodynamically meaningful Boltzmann distribution over crystal structures, going beyond sampling disconnected sets of low-energy structures. [A Distributional Framework for Generative Modeling of Molecular Crystals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05266)
*   **Quantum algorithms for constructing exchange-correlation potentials:** A quantum algorithmic framework using a variational quantum eigensolver is demonstrated to construct spin-resolved exchange-correlation potentials for strongly correlated materials, using the Hubbard model as a benchmark. [Quantum Algorithms to Determine Spin-Resolved Exchange-Correlation Potential for Strongly Correlated Materials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16605)
*   **Efficient classical simulation of long-range 2D systems:** A new variational Monte Carlo framework reduces the cost of evaluating the local energy to O(N) for tensor network states, enabling simulations of Rydberg arrays and ion-trap simulators for benchmarking quantum hardware. [Efficient classical simulation of two-dimensional long-range systems: Rydberg arrays and beyond](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05178)
*   **Massive benchmark dataset and pipeline for MLIPs:** The Dyna-Mat-v1.0 dataset provides over 700 condensed-phase molecular dynamics trajectories for benchmarking foundation machine learning interatomic potentials. Results show that "on average" models with lower force errors yield better structural and dynamical observables, but pressure is poorly described. [Dyna-Mat: End-to-end benchmarking of foundation machine learning interatomic potentials in finite-temperature ensembles](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.03433)
*   **dpti: Automated thermodynamic integration for phase diagrams:** An open-source Python package automates thermodynamic integration workflows for phase diagram calculations with machine learning interatomic potentials, demonstrated for silica and ice phase boundaries. [dpti: An Automated Thermodynamic Integration Workflow for Phase Diagram Calculations with Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05015)
*   **Self-consistent vertex-corrected GW for molecular charged excitations:** A hierarchy of self-consistent vertex corrections to the GW method is investigated. The study finds that vertex corrections do not uniformly improve ionization potentials or electron affinities over simple scGW, highlighting the need for more systematic approximations. [Self-consistent vertex corrected GW with static and dynamic screening using tensor hypercontraction: assessment of molecular charged excitations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25581)
*   **A unified electrostatic-to-spin framework for CMOS quantum devices:** A reduced-order analytical framework is developed for multi-gate silicon quantum dot devices, connecting lithographic geometry and electrostatic confinement to many-electron spin filling, useful for design-technology co-optimization. [A Unified Electrostatic-to-Spin Framework for Asymmetric Multi-Gate CMOS Quantum Devices](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.04876)
