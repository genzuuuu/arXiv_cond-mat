- **Resonant Cooling of Nuclear Spins in Perovskites**: In [Resonant cooling of nuclear spins by optically-oriented holes in MAPbI$_3$ perovskite crystals](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01441), a study demonstrates resonant cooling of $^{207}$Pb nuclear spins by photogenerated holes in MAPbI$_3$ perovskite crystals.
- **Exact Stationary State of a Run-and-Tumble Particle**: The paper [Exact Stationary State of a $d$-dimensional Run-and-Tumble Particle in a Harmonic Potential](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.08436) provides the exact nonequilibrium steady state of a run-and-tumble particle in a harmonic trap in any dimension.
- **Orbital-Charge Conversion in MoTe$_2$**: In [Phase-selective orbital-charge conversion in $\mathrm{MoTe_2}$](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01623), a spin-pumping study reveals that the metallic $1T^\prime$ phase of MoTe$_2$ exhibits an orbital Rashba-Edelstein response, while the semiconducting $2H$ phase does not.
- **Quantum Optics of Acoustic Phonons**: In [Quantifying angular momentum of coherently driven circular phonons](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01841), ultrafast X-ray diffraction resolves the circular ionic motion in SrTiO$_3$, quantifying phonon angular momentum and explaining the mechanism for induced magnetism.
- **Temperature-Invariant Kerr Effect in Mn3NiN**: The paper [Temperature-invariant magneto-optical Kerr effect in a noncollinear antiferromagnet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.19709) shows that the infrared Kerr effect in Mn3NiN is temperature-invariant, establishing it as a robust probe of the intrinsic Berry curvature.
- **Anisotropic Vortex Motion in 2D Superconductors**: In [Anisotropic vortex motion and two-dimensional superconducting transition](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05830), it is shown that an anisotropic pinning potential can lead to distinct critical temperatures along different current directions in a 2D superconductor.
- **Kirigami Cuts for LCE Metamaterials**: The paper [Scale-coupling from kirigami cuts controls emergent mechanics in liquid crystal elastomers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30501) demonstrates that kirigami cuts in liquid crystal elastomer sheets harness molecular anisotropy to program emergent mechanics and soft robotics functions.
- **Many-Body Bosonic System Localization**: In [Interaction-rotation driven localization-delocalization of eigenstate in Fock space: An exact diagonalization study on trapped Bose gas](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.01888), exact diagonalization reveals a localization-delocalization transition in a finite system of interacting bosons, driven by interaction strength and rotation.
- **Long-Range Coupling of Polariton Condensates**: The paper [Mirror-mediated long-range coupling and robust phase locking of spatially separated exciton-polariton condensates](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.20924) demonstrates independently tunable, long-range coupling of polariton condensates using a mirror to couple arbitrary sites.
- **Role of Memory in the Generalized Langevin Equation**: In [How wrong is too wrong: A numerical study on the relevance of positional memory in the generalized Langevin equation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.02268), it is shown that neglecting non-linear memory terms in the generalized Langevin equation can lead to inaccurate modeling.
